# Buyer Decision Guide — Allegis Global Solutions (AGS) vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO and MSP providers.

> **Note:** AGS (Allegis Global Solutions) is a separate company from AMS (Alexander Mann Solutions). Both operate in RPO and MSP; see the AMS buyer decision guide for AMS-specific guidance.

---

## What This Guide Does

AGS and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) both offer RPO and MSP/contingent workforce management — and will appear on the same shortlists for blended talent outsourcing mandates. This guide helps you understand the material differences between them and make the right choice for your specific workforce context.

## The Critical Governance Question

Before evaluating any other dimension, BFSI procurement teams should ask one question directly:

**"Is a vendor-neutral MSP from a provider owned by a major staffing group genuinely neutral?"**

AGS is owned by Allegis Group — which also owns Aerotek and TEKsystems, both of which are staffing suppliers that can supply contractors into AGS-managed MSP programmes. AGS manages this conflict through governance frameworks and firewalls. Whether those frameworks are sufficient is a legitimate question for your Risk Committee, procurement governance team, and compliance function.

RWO has no equivalent conflict. Robert Walters Group's recruitment business can be a supply partner in RWO MSP programmes, and this is fully disclosed — but Robert Walters is a specialist professional recruiter, not a volume staffing group with a commercial interest in inflating supply share.

For a regulated financial institution where conflicts of interest in supplier relationships are subject to governance oversight, this distinction matters.

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: You are a BFSI enterprise managing specialist professional RPO and MSP

**Recommended: RWO**

For banks, asset managers, and insurers where the primary workforce challenge involves specialist professional talent — compliance managers, risk analysts, investment operations professionals, treasury specialists, regulatory change contractors — RWO's model is substantially better suited than AGS's.

AGS's supply chain strength is in technology (TEKsystems) and engineering (Aerotek). For BFSI specialist professional roles, the Robert Walters professional network — built over 40 years specifically in financial services professional hiring — is a more relevant and higher-quality supply base.

---

### Scenario 2: You are a Fortune 500 multi-sector enterprise with large technology/engineering contingent workforce

**Recommended: AGS**

For very large enterprises with significant contingent workforces in technology, engineering, and sciences — particularly in the US — AGS's Allegis Group supply chain, enterprise delivery infrastructure, and scale are genuine competitive advantages. If your primary contingent workforce categories are technology and engineering, and your primary geography is the US, AGS is a stronger fit than RWO.

---

### Scenario 3: APAC is a significant part of your workforce challenge

**Recommended: RWO**

AGS's APAC infrastructure is primarily based in India — an offshore delivery hub for administrative and analytical work, not an on-the-ground specialist professional network in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia. For BFSI professional hiring and contingent workforce management in these markets, RWO's near-40-year on-the-ground presence is decisive.

---

### Scenario 4: Ownership transparency matters to your governance team

**Recommended: RWO**

Allegis Group is privately held. There is no public financial reporting — no revenue trends, no investment signal, no strategic priority data available for client due diligence. For a BFSI enterprise making a 3–5 year outsourcing commitment inside a regulated institution, the absence of public financial disclosure is a governance consideration.

RWO operates within publicly listed groups (Robert Walters plc / Recruit Holdings) — providing full financial transparency and governance visibility over the life of the contract.

---

### Scenario 5: You need a genuinely vendor-neutral MSP

**Evaluate carefully — consider RWO**

If vendor neutrality in your MSP is a stated governance requirement — required by your procurement policy, risk management framework, or regulatory obligations — the AGS/Allegis relationship is a governance question that must be answered before award.

RWO's independence from any large staffing group makes vendor neutrality structurally simpler to evidence and audit. Ask both providers to document their conflict-of-interest management framework and request that procurement governance independently assess whether it is sufficient.

---

### Scenario 6: Talent advisory alongside delivery is a requirement

**Recommended: RWO**

AGS's advisory capability is embedded in delivery — not a distinct commercial service. RWO's Workforce Consultancy is its fastest-growing service line, with RW Insights providing real-time external market intelligence for TA leadership. If strategic advisory and market data alongside operational delivery are valued, RWO has the more developed and commercially structured capability.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

1. **"For our BFSI specialist contractor categories — compliance managers, risk analysts, regulatory change specialists, treasury contractors — which suppliers would be in your proposed supply chain? What percentage would be Allegis Group brands (Aerotek, TEKsystems) vs. independent BFSI specialist firms?"**
   - Tests supply quality and conflict-of-interest transparency directly.

2. **"Describe your governance framework for managing the conflict of interest between AGS's vendor-neutral obligation and Allegis Group's commercial interest in placing Aerotek and TEKsystems contractors. How do you audit and evidence neutrality?"**
   - A direct governance question for regulated buyers.

3. **"We have significant hiring and contractor requirements in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia. Who is delivering those services on the ground — and what is their professional network in specialist BFSI disciplines at mid-to-senior level?"**
   - Tests APAC quality directly.

4. **"Allegis Group is privately held. How do you provide assurance of financial stability, leadership continuity, and strategic investment levels over the life of a 3–5 year outsourcing contract?"**
   - A legitimate question given the absence of public reporting.

5. **"What is your specific experience managing an MSP or RPO programme inside a regulated financial institution — including navigating Risk Committee approval, compliance sign-off, and audit engagement for an outsourced supplier relationship?"**
   - Tests BFSI governance depth.

6. **"Show me your technology for contingent workforce spend management and RPO delivery analytics. What does that look like in a live BFSI client deployment?"**
   - Moves evaluation from marketing to operational reality.

## Honest Assessment — What Each Provider Does Better

### AGS does better:
- Fortune 500-scale MSP with Allegis Group supply chain depth for technology and engineering
- US-centric Total Talent Management at very large enterprise scale
- Technology and engineering contingent workforce specialisation (TEKsystems, Aerotek)
- Compliance governance for complex, large-scale workforce programmes
- Long-term private ownership stability (no PE exit risk)

### RWO does better:
- BFSI specialist RPO and MSP — sector-specific expertise across all professional grades
- Specialist BFSI contractor supply — compliance, risk, treasury, investment operations professionals
- Vendor-neutral MSP without Allegis Group conflict of interest
- APAC regional depth — Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong — 40 years on the ground
- Financial transparency — publicly listed group vs. privately held
- Talent advisory and market intelligence (RW Insights) for TA leadership
- Long-term specialist BFSI partnership — TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award 2024

---

## The Bottom Line

**Choose AGS if:** Your primary workforce challenge is large-scale technology and engineering contingent workforce management in the US, you have an existing Allegis Group relationship through Aerotek or TEKsystems, and the conflict-of-interest considerations in vendor-neutral MSP are manageable within your governance framework.

**Choose RWO if:** Your primary sector is BFSI, your specialist professional workforce — permanent and contingent — requires a supply network built around financial services disciplines, your critical markets include the UK and Asia Pacific, and your governance team requires a vendor-neutral MSP that is genuinely independent of a large staffing group.

The vendor-neutral conflict of interest question is not a reason to eliminate AGS from consideration — it is a governance question that deserves a clear, documented answer from both your procurement team and AGS before award.
