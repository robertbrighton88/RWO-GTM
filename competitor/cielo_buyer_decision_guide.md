# Buyer Decision Guide — Cielo vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO providers. This guide is designed to help you make a well-informed decision rather than advocate for a particular provider.

---

## What This Guide Does

Both Cielo and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) are established, credible RPO providers. They occupy very different positions in the market — and understanding where each genuinely excels will help you match the right provider to your actual requirements.

---

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: You are a BFSI enterprise with significant UK and APAC hiring

**Recommended: RWO**

For banking, financial services, and insurance organisations where the hiring challenge sits primarily in the UK and Asia Pacific — particularly Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, and Hong Kong — RWO is substantially better positioned than Cielo.

Cielo's primary delivery infrastructure is in the US and UK (via Poland). Their APAC presence is real but thin, with offshore sourcing from the Philippines rather than on-the-ground teams in the markets where BFSI hiring is most complex. RWO has operated in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia for nearly 40 years — the candidate relationships, local market knowledge, and regulatory understanding in these markets are not replicable from an offshore delivery model.

---

### Scenario 2: You need high-volume permanent hiring across North America and Western Europe

**Recommended: Cielo**

For large enterprise clients with primary hiring in the US and UK, high annual hire volumes, and straightforward (non-specialist-sector) requirements, Cielo's purpose-built platform, scale, and US market presence make them a strong fit. RWO is not primarily a volume-throughput provider — and for clients whose primary challenge is efficient volume hiring rather than specialist quality, Cielo's model is better suited.

---

### Scenario 3: You have permanent and contingent workforce requirements

**Recommended: RWO**

Cielo is a pure-play permanent RPO provider — they do not offer MSP or contingent workforce management. If your workforce challenge includes both permanent professionals and a significant contingent population (contractors, specialist consultants, SOW workers), you need a provider who can manage both under one integrated model.

RWO offers integrated RPO and MSP/CWM, with Everest Group Star Performer recognition in 2025 for their contingent workforce management capability. Managing both under one partnership reduces commercial complexity, data fragmentation, and the governance risk of split oversight.

---

### Scenario 4: Technology is your primary evaluation criterion

**Recommended: Cielo (with caveats)**

If your TA leadership team is technology-first and CieloBridge's analytics, sourcing automation, and hiring velocity reporting are central to what you need, Cielo is the stronger technology choice for RPO delivery tooling.

However, if talent market intelligence — real-time data on salary benchmarks, talent pool depth, and competitor hiring activity, surfaced directly to your TA leadership as a strategic decision-making tool — is equally or more important, RWO's RW Insights platform offers something Cielo's internal dashboards do not.

Ask both providers to demonstrate their technology in the context of your specific hiring challenge, not in a generic product demonstration.

---

### Scenario 5: You need advisory alongside delivery

**Recommended: RWO**

Cielo's advisory capability is embedded in delivery rather than offered as a distinct commercial service. RWO's Talent Advisory practice is its fastest-growing service line — growing +51% in net fee income — and is structured as a dedicated capability for workforce planning, TA operating model design, and market intelligence. If strategic advisory alongside operational delivery is part of your requirement, RWO is more developed in this space.

---

### Scenario 6: Long-term ownership stability matters to your evaluation

**Recommended: RWO**

Cielo is owned by Audax Private Equity — a US mid-market PE firm. PE-backed businesses are oriented toward an exit event (IPO, strategic sale, secondary PE deal), which creates risk of leadership disruption, operational restructuring, and service continuity uncertainty at the point of transaction. This is not a certainty, but it is a legitimate consideration for clients making 3–5 year outsourcing commitments.

RWO operates as part of Robert Walters plc (now within the Recruit Holdings group) — a publicly listed structure with a long-term investment horizon and transparent governance. If ownership stability over the life of your contract matters, this distinction is worth exploring with both providers.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

1. **"For my specific hiring markets — [list your key geographies] — show me who is on the ground, what their local language capability is, and how long they've been in those markets."**
   - Distinguishes genuine local presence from partner coverage or offshore delivery.

2. **"What percentage of your business is permanent RPO vs. MSP/contingent? And if I need to manage both — what does that model look like?"**
   - Cielo's answer will be clear: they don't do contingent. RWO's answer should explain integrated RPO + MSP.

3. **"Walk me through how your delivery model adapts for a regulated BFSI client — specifically, how do you handle compliance sign-off, governance requirements, and Risk Committee processes in your hiring programmes?"**
   - Tests for sector-specific knowledge vs. generic enterprise RPO process.

4. **"Who will actually manage my programme 12 months after contract signature — and is that the same team I'm meeting today?"**
   - Account management handover (senior sellers to junior delivery teams) is a common complaint for both providers. Press for specifics.

5. **"What is your ownership structure, and what does that mean for long-term investment in this relationship?"**
   - Cielo's PE ownership is a legitimate due diligence item. Compare with RWO's listed group structure.

6. **"Show me a specific example of how your technology — Cielo's CieloBridge or RWO's RW Insights — changed a hiring outcome, not just an efficiency metric."**
   - Separates genuine technology value from marketing.

## Honest Assessment — What Each Provider Does Better

### Cielo does better:
- High-volume permanent hiring at enterprise scale
- US and North American market delivery
- Purpose-built RPO technology platform (CieloBridge)
- Employer brand and EVP as a structured service
- Pure-play RPO specialist credibility

### RWO does better:
- BFSI specialist hiring — sector depth that generalists cannot match
- APAC regional quality — Japan, Singapore, Malaysia built over 40 years
- Integrated RPO + MSP/CWM under one partnership
- Talent advisory and market intelligence (RW Insights)
- Candidate attraction via Robert Walters brand in professional APAC markets
- Long-term partnership stability — listed group ownership vs. PE-backed

---

## The Bottom Line

**Choose Cielo if:** Your primary challenge is high-volume permanent hiring in the US and Western Europe, technology platform capability is central to your evaluation, and your workforce requirement is primarily or exclusively permanent (no MSP/contingent).

**Choose RWO if:** Your primary sector is BFSI, your critical hiring markets include the UK and Asia Pacific, you need integrated permanent and contingent workforce management, and you want a specialist partner with 40 years of knowledge in your sector and your markets — not a volume machine.

The most common mistake buyers make in this comparison is evaluating on brand scale and analyst rank rather than on fit for their specific sector, geography, and workforce context.
