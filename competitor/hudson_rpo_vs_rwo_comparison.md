## Service-by-Service Analysis

### RPO (Permanent Hiring Outsourcing)

Both providers offer genuine RPO capability in APAC. This is Hudson RPO's entire focus and the core of RWO's outsourcing business.

- **Hudson RPO advantage:** APAC specialist focus; international expansion RPO is a genuinely differentiated product; for pure APAC-only permanent hiring, Hudson is a credible peer
- **RWO advantage:** Broader model variants (Full, Project, Modular) are all documented; integrated with Robert Walters recruitment brand; BFSI sector depth; global delivery for multi-region needs

**Overall:** Neutral for pure APAC permanent hiring; RWO wins where BFSI depth, global delivery, or service integration matters.

### MSP / Contingent Workforce Management

This is not a competitive battleground — it is a walk-over for RWO.

- **Hudson RPO:** No documented MSP capability
- **RWO:** Everest Group Star Performer 2025 in CWM/MSP; full contingent workforce management; vendor neutral and preferred supplier models; spend visibility, compliance, rate card management

Any client with contingent workforce needs alongside permanent hiring cannot meet those needs through Hudson RPO alone. This creates natural RWO opportunities with clients who are currently Hudson RPO permanent-hiring clients but have unmet contingent workforce needs.

## Where RWO Has the Edge

### 1. Robert Walters Brand in APAC Candidate Markets
The single most powerful differentiator and the hardest for Hudson to counter. Robert Walters has 35–40 years of brand presence in Japan, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Malaysia. Candidates recognise and respond to the Robert Walters name. Hudson RPO operates as an RPO provider — typically white-labelled or less visible to candidates. In markets where talent attraction is competitive, this brand differential translates directly to faster hiring and better candidate quality.

### 2. MSP / Contingent Workforce Management
Decisive capability gap. RWO offers Total Talent Management across permanent and contingent; Hudson cannot. For any client with a blended workforce requiring both RPO and MSP — an increasingly common requirement — RWO is the only credible single-provider option between these two competitors.

### 3. Talent Advisory and Market Intelligence
RWO's workforce consultancy service (+51% NFI growth) and the RW Insights platform give RWO a strategic conversation that Hudson cannot replicate. C-suite buyers who want more than execution — who want a strategic partner for workforce planning — will find more in RWO's offering.

### 4. Global Multi-Region Delivery
60+ countries of talent delivery, 23 country account teams, 6 global service centres. For any mandate spanning APAC and another region, RWO delivers under a single contract. Hudson RPO cannot.

### 5. BFSI Sector Depth — Regulatory Expertise
RWO's primary sector expertise is in banking, financial services, and insurance — built over 40 years in markets like London, Singapore, Tokyo, Hong Kong. The regulatory hiring complexity in BFSI (MAS, JFSA, APRA, FCA compliance hiring) and the operational complexity of working inside banks (risk, governance, internal politics) is a genuine deep knowledge advantage. Hudson serves BFSI clients but does not lead with this sector depth as a primary differentiator.

### 6. Integrated Recruitment + Outsourcing Ecosystem
RWO sits within Robert Walters Group — a 31-country specialist recruitment business. The outsourcing business can draw on the parent network for market intelligence, candidate pools, and sector expertise in ways that Hudson RPO (independent) cannot replicate.

### 7. Analyst Recognition and Award Portfolio
RWO holds documented, named analyst recognition (Everest Group Star Performer 2025 — MSP), a CEO award (HRO Today Provider Superstar), TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award, and SIA recognition. Hudson RPO's analyst and award portfolio is less prominently documented and less multi-dimensional.

## Where Hudson RPO Has the Edge

### 1. Pure APAC Specialist Focus
This is a genuine advantage, not merely a marketing claim. When a company's entire talent problem is APAC-based — and they want a partner whose entire operation is built around APAC — Hudson RPO can argue (credibly) that RWO is a global business for which APAC is one of many regions. This focus argument resonates with certain buyers, particularly those sceptical of global platforms that dilute local expertise.

### 2. International Expansion RPO in APAC
Hudson RPO's most differentiated product. Helping a company enter Japan or Malaysia for the first time, navigating employment law, cultural nuances, and candidate market dynamics — this is where Hudson has built specific, documented expertise. RWO offers this capability but does not lead with it as a primary product identity.

### 3. Perceived Agility for Mid-Market Clients
[Inferred] Smaller mid-market companies entering APAC may find Hudson RPO more accessible and responsive than RWO's more structured programme design. A company hiring 30–50 people per year in APAC may find Hudson's boutique scale better-fitted to their volume than RWO's enterprise infrastructure.

### 4. Australia Home Market
In Australia specifically, Hudson RPO's home-market depth, team scale, and established client relationships are a genuine competitive advantage. RWO competes well in Australia but is not the incumbent the way Hudson is.

## The Key Battleground

### Battleground 1: Cultural Intelligence vs. Brand Heritage

The deal-defining narrative tension in APAC is whether the buyer values:
- **Hudson RPO's claim:** Pure APAC focus, cultural intelligence, we are built in Asia
- **RWO's counter:** 40 years of Robert Walters brand in APAC — Japanese candidates know us, Singapore candidates know us; our cultural intelligence is proven over decades, not claimed in a pitch

RWO wins this argument when the conversation shifts from "who is more focused on APAC" to "whose brand actually attracts better candidates in APAC." The Robert Walters name is the closer.

### Battleground 2: BFSI Mandates in Singapore, Hong Kong, Japan

Financial services hiring in APAC is sophisticated, regulatory-sensitive, and critical. These mandates are typically high value, long-term, and relationship-driven. RWO's BFSI sector depth — regulatory knowledge, understanding of how banks operate internally, long-track record in financial services talent markets — is the decisive differentiator. Hudson's BFSI capability exists but is not a primary brand claim.

### Battleground 3: Multi-Region Scope and Service Integration

As companies mature their outsourcing relationships, they increasingly want to consolidate providers — combining permanent RPO, contingent MSP, and advisory under one partner. This conversation is one Hudson RPO cannot have. Every time a client mentions contingent workforce, advisory consulting, or EMEA/US delivery in the same brief — RWO should win the scope argument decisively.
