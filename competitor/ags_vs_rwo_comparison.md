## One-Line Summary

**AGS:** Enterprise-scale RPO and MSP backed by Allegis Group's staffing supply chain — built for Fortune 500 clients with complex, US-centric, technology and engineering-heavy contingent workforce programmes.

**RWO:** Specialist BFSI-sector RPO and MSP — built for financial services enterprises with specialist professional workforce needs in the UK and APAC, with a supply network oriented toward professional-grade BFSI talent rather than technology and industrial staffing.

## Dimension-by-Dimension Analysis

### 1. Vendor-Neutral MSP — The Critical Dimension

**RWO wins on independence.** The Allegis Group conflict of interest in AGS's vendor-neutral MSP programmes is a legitimate and persistent market concern. AGS is owned by the same group that owns Aerotek and TEKsystems — both of which can be suppliers in AGS-managed MSP programmes. Even with governance firewalls, the perception problem is real and procurement committees in regulated BFSI organisations take it seriously.

RWO has no staffing parent conflict. Robert Walters Group's recruitment business can be a supply partner in RWO MSP programmes, but the structural relationship is transparent — Robert Walters is a specialist professional recruiter, not a volume staffing group, and BFSI clients value this distinction.

---

### 2. Contingent Workforce Supply Chain Quality (BFSI)

**Contested — but RWO wins for BFSI specialist categories.** AGS's supply chain advantage is genuine for technology (TEKsystems) and engineering (Aerotek) contingent roles. For BFSI specialist categories — compliance officers, risk managers, credit analysts, treasury contractors, regulatory specialists — the Allegis Group supply chain is not primarily oriented toward these disciplines. RWO's BFSI professional network, built over 40 years, is a more relevant and higher-quality supply base for these specialist roles.

---

### 3. BFSI Sector Expertise

**RWO wins clearly.** AGS serves financial services as one sector among many — their financial services capability is strongest in technology-for-finance and operational roles (reflecting the TEKsystems heritage). The specialist BFSI professional depth — understanding of regulatory hiring, compliance-adjacent roles, governance navigation inside banks — is not a documented AGS strength.

---

### 4. APAC Capability

**RWO wins decisively.** AGS's APAC infrastructure is built around India as an operational hub — efficient for offshore delivery of analytical and administrative functions, unsuitable for specialist professional RPO in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia where on-the-ground knowledge, language capability, and professional candidate networks are essential. Robert Walters Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia represent 40 years of specialist professional network that AGS cannot replicate.

---

### 5. Fortune 500 Enterprise Scale

**AGS wins clearly.** AGS is built for the largest, most complex global enterprises — Fortune 500 clients with enormous contingent workforces, multi-continent operations, and complex compliance governance requirements. For these clients, AGS's scale, Allegis supply chain, and enterprise delivery infrastructure are genuine competitive advantages. RWO is better suited to large BFSI enterprises rather than multi-sector Fortune 500 conglomerates with vast, diverse contingent workforces.

---

### 6. Ownership Transparency

**RWO wins.** AGS's private ownership limits financial visibility — clients cannot verify revenue trends, investment levels, or strategic priorities through public reporting. RWO's position within publicly listed groups (Robert Walters plc (LSE: RWA)) provides full financial transparency and governance visibility over a multi-year outsourcing relationship.

## When Each Provider Wins

### RWO Wins When:

- **BFSI is the primary sector** — specialist professional RPO and MSP for financial services
- **Vendor-neutral MSP independence** is a governance requirement — no Allegis Group conflict of interest
- **APAC hiring and contractor management** is significant — Japan, Singapore, Malaysia
- **Specialist BFSI contractor supply** (compliance, risk, credit, treasury) is the primary MSP requirement
- **Talent advisory and market intelligence** (RW Insights) are valued alongside delivery
- **Mid-to-large BFSI enterprise** rather than Fortune 500 multi-sector conglomerate
- **Ownership transparency** matters — listed group vs. privately held
- **Long-term specialist partnership** — TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award 2024

### AGS Wins When:

- **Fortune 500 scale** — very large, multi-geography, multi-sector enterprise
- **Technology and engineering contingent workforce** is the primary MSP requirement (TEKsystems, Aerotek supply advantage)
- **US-centric** — AGS's infrastructure and client base is primarily North American
- **Allegis ecosystem** relationships already exist at the group level
- **Very large contingent workforce populations** — thousands of contractors across diverse sectors
