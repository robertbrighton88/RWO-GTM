# Buyer Decision Guide — AMS vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO and MSP providers. This guide is designed to help you make a well-informed decision rather than advocate for a particular provider.

---

## What This Guide Does

Both AMS and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) are credible, established providers in the RPO and talent solutions market. This guide helps you understand where each provider genuinely excels — so you can match the right partner to your actual requirements rather than buying on brand or analyst ranking alone.

---

## The Core Trade-off

| If your priority is... | The stronger choice is likely... |
|---|---|
| Global scale across 20+ countries simultaneously | **AMS** |
| Total Talent Management (permanent + contingent in one model) | **AMS** |
| DEI programme delivery with structured reporting | **AMS** |
| Everest Group Leader-tier analyst credibility | **AMS** |
| BFSI specialist expertise (banking, financial services, insurance) | **RWO** |
| APAC hiring depth (Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong) | **RWO** |
| Quality of hire in specialist, mid-to-senior roles | **RWO** |
| Talent advisory and market intelligence for TA leadership | **RWO** |
| Long-term partnership with low TA leadership turnover | **RWO** |
| Candidate attraction via recognised professional brand | **RWO** |

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: You are a global BFSI enterprise with significant APAC hiring

**Recommended: RWO**

If your primary hiring challenge is in financial services — particularly across Asia Pacific — the RWO proposition is substantially stronger than AMS's. The Robert Walters name has 40 years of candidate trust in Japan, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Malaysia. AMS's APAC delivery is real but thinner, typically supported from regional hubs rather than deep local networks.

For regulatory, compliance, and risk-adjacent hiring inside banks and insurers, RWO's understanding of how financial institutions operate internally — governance layers, risk committees, compliance sign-off — is a genuine differentiator. AMS approaches this as general enterprise hiring; RWO approaches it as sector-specific expertise.

---

### Scenario 2: You need one provider to manage permanent and contingent hiring globally across 30+ countries

**Recommended: AMS**

AMS's Total Talent Management model, global delivery infrastructure, and 120-country reach make them better equipped for large, multi-geography, blended workforce mandates than RWO. If you have significant contingent workforce populations in the US, UK, and multiple European markets alongside APAC, and you want one commercial relationship managing all of it, AMS is likely the stronger structural fit.

---

### Scenario 3: DEI programme delivery is a primary procurement criterion

**Recommended: AMS**

AMS has built DEI into their delivery model as a formal service capability — structured programme design, diverse slate commitments, bias-reduction processes, and DEI analytics. If your organisation has formal DEI governance obligations, board-level reporting requirements, or explicit diversity targets embedded in your hiring function, AMS's capability here is more developed than RWO's.

---

### Scenario 4: You are a mid-to-large BFSI enterprise looking for specialist RPO in the UK and/or APAC

**Recommended: RWO**

This is RWO's core sweet spot. A bank, asset manager, or insurer with significant hiring across the UK and APAC, looking for a partner with genuine sector expertise, APAC regional depth, and the ability to operate inside their organisation's governance structure, is the client RWO was built to serve. AMS can serve this client — but the fit is less precise.

---

### Scenario 5: Talent advisory and market intelligence are part of your requirement

**Recommended: RWO**

RWO's workforce consultancy service — its fastest-growing capability — and RW Insights (a real-time talent market intelligence platform) provide a level of advisory depth that AMS's delivery model does not match in this domain. If you want an outsourcing partner that also helps your TA leadership think strategically about workforce planning and market dynamics, RWO is the stronger choice.

---

### Scenario 6: You are primarily based in the US or North America

**Recommended: AMS**

RWO's primary markets are the UK and Asia Pacific, with the US as a growth market. For US-centric or North America-led mandates, AMS has materially deeper delivery infrastructure. If US hiring is the core requirement with APAC as secondary, AMS may serve you better.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

Use these to go beyond the pitch and understand the reality of what you're buying:

1. **"Where specifically do you have on-the-ground delivery teams in my key hiring markets — not partner networks or remote sourcing?"**
   - This separates real presence from partnership coverage in country-count claims.

2. **"Show me your delivery team for this engagement. Who will actually manage my programme — and what is their tenure with you?"**
   - Tests for account management quality and whether the A-team that presents turns over to a B-team after contract signature.

3. **"What is your experience specifically in [BFSI / your sector]? Can you describe how your delivery model adapts for hiring inside a regulated financial institution?"**
   - Distinguishes genuine sector expertise from generic enterprise RPO.

4. **"What do your clients say about you 18 months into an engagement — not at the point of renewal, but in quarterly reviews?"**
   - Tests for delivery consistency, not just sales positioning.

5. **"How do you measure quality of hire — and can you show me a client example where that metric drove a change in your delivery model?"**
   - Tests whether quality of hire is a genuine operational focus or a marketing claim.

6. **"What does your transition programme look like — and who bears the risk and cost during the handover period?"**
   - Important for both providers — complex implementations carry transition risk that should be transparent in the commercial model.

## Honest Assessment — What Each Provider Does Better

### AMS does better:
- Operating at global scale across diverse geographies and sectors
- Embedding DEI as a formal programme with structured reporting
- Integrating permanent and contingent workforce management in one model
- Analyst credibility at the highest tier (Everest Group Leader)
- US and North American hiring delivery

### RWO does better:
- BFSI specialist hiring — understanding of how financial institutions operate internally
- APAC regional depth — on-the-ground knowledge, candidate relationships, language capability
- Attracting specialist candidates via the Robert Walters employer brand
- Talent advisory — market intelligence and workforce consultancy
- Long-term partnership at mid-to-large BFSI enterprise scale, where the relationship is as important as the process

---

## The Bottom Line

**Choose AMS if:** Your primary requirement is global scale, Total Talent integration across permanent and contingent workforce, formal DEI programme delivery, or breadth across non-APAC markets.

**Choose RWO if:** Your primary sector is BFSI, your critical hiring markets include APAC (particularly Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia), you value specialist depth over generalist scale, and you want a partner — not a vendor — who understands how your organisation works from the inside.

Neither provider is universally better. The right answer depends on your actual hiring context, not the providers' marketing claims.
