# AMS Deep Dive — Research Plan

**Objective:** Produce comprehensive competitive intelligence on Alexander Mann Solutions (AMS) to equip RWO with the sharpest possible positioning, a practical sales battlecard, and a buyer decision framework.

**What we already know (from `competitive_positioning.md`):**
- AMS operates in 120+ countries, 10,000+ talent experts
- Full RPO + MSP + contingent workforce capability
- DEI embedded in every programme
- Everest Group Leader (above RWO's Star Performer)
- Perceived as process-heavy / generalist
- RWO counter: specialist expertise, APAC depth, agility

This plan goes 10x deeper.

---

## Research Workstreams

### 1. AMS Service Architecture & Delivery Model
**Goal:** Understand exactly what AMS sells and how they deliver it — so we can compare service-by-service against RWO.

**Research questions:**
- What are their exact RPO, MSP, and CWM service tiers?
- Do they offer Project RPO / Modular RPO equivalents?
- What does their "Total Talent Management" model actually mean in practice?
- How is their delivery structured (onsite, nearshore, offshore)?
- What's their proprietary technology platform? (likely AMS Achieve or similar)
- How do they handle DEI — is it a separate module or embedded?
- Do they offer advisory/consulting beyond RPO/MSP execution?

**Sources:** AMS website, case studies, solution briefs, webinars

---

### 2. AMS Positioning & Messaging
**Goal:** Map their narrative, value propositions, and how they pitch — so we can pre-empt, reframe, or counter them.

**Research questions:**
- What is their primary positioning statement / headline claim?
- What is their "why AMS" argument?
- How do they position technology (AI narrative)?
- How do they frame DEI as a differentiator?
- What language do they use for RPO vs MSP vs advisory?
- Do they claim any specialist sector depth or is it purely generalist?
- How do they pitch against the market — what pain points do they lead with?

**Sources:** AMS website, thought leadership content, LinkedIn, webinars, analyst summaries

---

### 3. AMS Analyst & Award Standing
**Goal:** Understand where AMS ranks vs RWO in every relevant analyst report — know where we're behind and where we can credibly claim parity.

**Research questions:**
- Everest Group ranking (RPO and MSP/CWM PEAK Matrix) — Leader, Major Contender, or Star Performer?
- NelsonHall NEAT RPO/MSP positioning
- Gartner Magic Quadrant (if applicable)
- SIA recognition
- Any recent analyst commentary specifically on AMS strengths/weaknesses
- Awards: what have they won and when?

**Sources:** Everest Group, NelsonHall, SIA, Gartner public summaries, AMS awards page

---

### 4. AMS Geographic & Sector Depth
**Goal:** Map where AMS is genuinely strong vs where their 120-country claim is thin — identify where RWO has real, defensible ground.

**Research questions:**
- Where are their actual delivery hubs and service centres?
- How strong are they in APAC specifically (Japan, Singapore, Malaysia)?
- What sectors do they serve — is BFSI genuinely a focus or just a claim?
- Do they have specialist BFSI capability or just breadth?
- Where are their biggest and oldest client relationships?
- Are they US-centric (they're now part of Allegis Global Solutions Group)?

**Sources:** AMS website, LinkedIn offices, case studies by sector, news, analyst sector reports

---

### 5. AMS Technology Stack
**Goal:** Understand their tech claims and compare them against RWO's RW Plus / RW Insights.

**Research questions:**
- What is their proprietary platform (name, capabilities, positioning)?
- What ATS/HRIS integrations do they support?
- How do they position AI — matching, screening, analytics?
- Do they have a market intelligence / talent mapping product comparable to RW Insights?
- What third-party tech partnerships do they hold (Indeed, LinkedIn, Beamery, etc.)?
- How do clients describe their tech in reviews?

**Sources:** AMS website, G2, press releases, tech partnership announcements

---

### 6. Market Perception — Voice of the Customer & Employees
**Goal:** Understand how actual buyers and candidates experience AMS — find the gaps between their marketing and reality.

**Research questions:**
- What do clients say on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, or HR forums?
- What are the most common complaints from AMS clients?
- What do candidates experience working with AMS (Glassdoor, Indeed)?
- What do AMS employees say about delivery quality, culture, and leadership?
- Are there any notable client churns, public disputes, or SLA failures?
- What does the recruiter/TA community think of AMS vs RWO on LinkedIn?

**Sources:** G2, Glassdoor, Indeed, LinkedIn comments, Reddit (r/humanresources), HR forums

---

### 7. AMS Recent News & Strategic Direction
**Goal:** Understand where AMS is heading — new products, acquisitions, leadership, market moves — to use as intelligence in conversations and content.

**Research questions:**
- Recent acquisitions or strategic partnerships
- Leadership changes (CEO, regional MDs)
- New product or service launches (last 12–18 months)
- Any restructuring or market exits
- Their AI investment narrative — what have they actually shipped?
- Are they growing, flat, or contracting?
- Any ESG or sustainability commitments that are market-relevant?
- Allegis Group ownership implications — how does this affect client perception?

**Sources:** PR Newswire, Business Wire, LinkedIn, HR industry press (ERE, SHRM, SIA), Companies House / annual reports

---

### 8. AMS Commercial Model & Pricing Signals
**Goal:** Understand how AMS structures and prices deals — so RWO can handle commercial objections effectively.

**Research questions:**
- What contract lengths do they typically push for?
- Do they price on management fee, per-hire, cost-per-hire, or hybrid?
- Are there minimum deal sizes or thresholds?
- Do they require tech platform licensing on top of service fees?
- What does implementation / transition cost look like?
- Are they seen as expensive, mid-market, or price-competitive?
- How do procurement teams experience the AMS sales and contracting process?

**Sources:** Review sites, procurement forums, industry reports, RFP comparisons, RWO sales team intel

---

### 10. Competitive Win/Loss Framework
Map the scenarios where each provider is the better choice — producing an honest, usable decision guide for prospects.

**RWO wins when:**
- BFSI sector expertise is required
- APAC hiring is a significant part of the scope
- Client values quality of hire over volume throughput
- Client is a mid-sized enterprise (not a 50,000-person global corporation)
- Relationship-led, long-term partnership model is important
- Regulatory complexity (risk, compliance governance inside banks) requires navigational expertise
- Candidate brand matters — Robert Walters name improves attraction
- Advisory / market intelligence is part of the requirement (RW Insights)

**AMS wins when:**
- Client needs truly global scale at volume (120+ countries)
- Total Talent Management in one contract is the requirement
- DEI reporting and programme delivery is a primary buying criterion
- Client is a Fortune 500 with complex multi-region contingent + permanent workforce
- Allegis ecosystem relationships are already in place
- Procurement team requires Everest Group Leader-tier analyst credibility

---

### 11. Sales Battlecard — RWO vs AMS
Practical, one-page (or two-page) tool for RWO salespeople:
- AMS's likely objection points against RWO
- RWO's counter-narratives
- Questions to ask that expose AMS weaknesses
- Proof points to deploy
- When to walk away (AMS is genuinely better for this client)
