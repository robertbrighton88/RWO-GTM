## The Core Battle

Pontoon will try to win this deal on **Adecco Group scale, Total Talent capability, and automation-first technology**.

Your job is to make the conversation about **specialist quality, BFSI sector depth, APAC delivery, and the difference between a generalist supply chain and a specialist one**.

> "Pontoon can give you Adecco's network. The question is whether Adecco's network gives you what a BFSI organisation actually needs."

## Questions to Ask That Expose Pontoon Weaknesses

**On BFSI sector depth:**
> "Pontoon serves clients across technology, manufacturing, consumer, and financial services. How many of your dedicated delivery team — in the UK and APAC — have specific experience managing RPO or MSP programmes inside a regulated financial institution? And how do you adapt your delivery model for the governance, risk sign-off, and compliance requirements that are standard in a Tier 1 bank?"

**On APAC quality:**
> "Your APAC delivery draws on Adecco's regional infrastructure. In Japan specifically — where Adecco is primarily known for temporary and general staffing — how does Pontoon deliver specialist professional RPO? Who are the on-the-ground recruiters, what is their professional network at mid-to-senior BFSI levels, and how does that compare to a provider with 35 years of specialist professional recruitment in Japan?"

**On the Adecco brand in candidate markets:**
> "When a candidate in Singapore or Hong Kong receives outreach from this programme — what brand do they see? Adecco is associated with temporary and general staffing in APAC professional markets. Robert Walters is associated with specialist professional careers. Does that brand distinction affect the quality of candidate you attract for BFSI roles?"

**On MSP supply chain quality:**
> "You mentioned Adecco's supply chain as a key advantage for our MSP programme. Of your current supply chain for financial services contractor roles — compliance, risk, treasury, credit — what percentage of suppliers specialise specifically in these disciplines? And how do you assess supply chain quality for specialist BFSI categories vs. generalist contractor roles?"

**On Adecco Group strategic priority:**
> "Adecco Group's primary business is staffing — which is a much larger revenue line than Pontoon's outsourcing operations. How does Pontoon compete for investment, leadership attention, and strategic priority within Adecco Group when the staffing business has a bad quarter? And how does that translate to the delivery team and technology investment assigned to our account?"

## When to Walk Away

Pontoon is genuinely the better choice — and you should qualify down or walk away — when:

- **Very large enterprise contingent workforce programmes** spanning multiple non-BFSI sectors simultaneously — where the Adecco supply chain breadth is a genuine material advantage
- **US is the primary market** and the Adecco/Pontoon US infrastructure outweighs RWO's limited US presence
- **Direct sourcing** of large generalist contractor populations is the primary commercial requirement and the client has invested in this model
- **Adecco Group relationship** already exists at a group level and expanding to Pontoon is a procurement consolidation decision rather than a competitive evaluation

## The Headline Narrative (One Paragraph)

> Pontoon brings the scale of one of the world's largest staffing companies to RPO and MSP. For generalist, multi-sector, high-volume enterprise programmes — particularly in the US and Western Europe — that scale is a real advantage. But for a BFSI organisation hiring and managing specialist professional talent across the UK and Asia Pacific, Adecco's generalist supply chain is the wrong infrastructure. What you need is a provider whose candidate network was built specifically for the roles your business runs on — compliance specialists, risk managers, credit analysts, financial technology professionals. That's what Robert Walters Outsourcing was built for. Pontoon was built for something else.
