# Buyer Decision Guide — PeopleScout vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO providers. This guide is designed to help you make a well-informed decision rather than advocate for a particular provider.

---

## What This Guide Does

PeopleScout and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) are rarely the right answer for the same client — their models are built for different hiring challenges. This guide helps you identify which challenge is yours, and which provider is genuinely suited to solve it.

## The Defining Question

**Before evaluating any other dimension, answer this:** Is your primary hiring challenge a volume problem or a specialist quality problem?

- **Volume problem:** You need to fill a large number of mid-level, operational, or entry roles at scale, with consistent quality and cost efficiency. PeopleScout's model is built for this.
- **Specialist quality problem:** You need to attract and hire specialist professionals — compliance, risk, treasury, investment operations, senior finance — in competitive markets where the wrong hire has real consequences. RWO's model is built for this.

Most BFSI enterprises face both. The question is which challenge is the primary driver of this outsourcing decision.

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: You are a BFSI enterprise hiring specialist professionals across the UK and APAC

**Recommended: RWO**

PeopleScout's delivery model is optimised for volume throughput at operational and mid-level grades. For specialist BFSI professionals — compliance managers, risk analysts, investment operations leads, regulatory specialists, treasury professionals — the quality ceiling of a volume-first model creates real hiring risk. RWO's 40-year specialist BFSI network, combined with near-40-year on-the-ground presence in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia, is the right model for this challenge.

---

### Scenario 2: You need high-volume RPO for operational financial services roles in the US and UK

**Recommended: PeopleScout (or closely evaluate)**

For high-volume operational hiring — contact centre, branch banking staff, insurance processing, back-office operations — PeopleScout's Affinix platform and volume delivery model is well-suited. If the majority of your hiring is operational-grade in the US and UK, with limited specialist professional or APAC requirements, PeopleScout may offer better fit and more competitive pricing than RWO's specialist model.

---

### Scenario 3: Technology platform is a primary evaluation criterion

**Evaluate carefully — they answer different questions**

PeopleScout's Affinix is purpose-built for talent acquisition at volume — mobile-first candidate experience, AI-assisted matching, analytics for hiring pipeline performance. If operational efficiency, time-to-fill, and cost-per-hire at volume are your primary technology requirements, Affinix is a strong choice.

RWO's technology is oriented differently: RW Plus powers specialist delivery, and RW Insights provides your TA leadership with real-time external market intelligence — talent pool depth, salary trends, competitor hiring activity in your specific BFSI markets. If strategic market intelligence is a valued technology outcome, RW Insights fills a gap that Affinix does not address.

Clarify your primary technology need before using platform capability as a decision criterion.

---

### Scenario 4: Employer brand and digital recruitment marketing are requirements

**Recommended: PeopleScout**

PeopleScout's acquisition of TMP Worldwide gives them a genuinely more developed employer brand capability than most RPO providers — EVP development, digital recruitment marketing, social media candidate attraction, and candidate journey design. If structured employer brand services are a requirement alongside RPO, PeopleScout has the more developed capability.

Note: For BFSI hiring in APAC professional markets, the Robert Walters brand may be more valuable than any structured EVP exercise — 40 years of specialist professional brand recognition in Japan and Singapore cannot be matched by digital recruitment marketing.

---

### Scenario 5: You need permanent and contingent workforce managed together

**Recommended: RWO**

PeopleScout is a permanent RPO provider — they do not offer MSP or contingent workforce management. If your BFSI workforce includes specialist contractors, interim professionals, or SOW workers alongside permanent staff — which is typical for financial services enterprises — RWO's integrated RPO and MSP model, backed by Everest Group Star Performer recognition in CWM/MSP, is the relevant solution.

---

### Scenario 6: APAC is a significant part of your hiring mandate

**Recommended: RWO**

PeopleScout's APAC presence is thin and not a primary strategic focus. Robert Walters has operated in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia for nearly 40 years as a specialist professional recruiter. For BFSI professional hiring in these markets — where local knowledge, language capability, and candidate relationships built over decades are material advantages — PeopleScout cannot compete.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

1. **"What percentage of your BFSI client book involves specialist professional roles — compliance, risk, treasury, senior operations — vs. operational and mid-level volume hiring? Show me examples of each."**
   - Distinguishes where each provider genuinely performs vs. where they claim capability.

2. **"For our specialist BFSI roles in Tokyo and Singapore — who actually sources these candidates, in what language, from what network?"**
   - Tests APAC delivery quality directly. PeopleScout's answer will reveal the gap.

3. **"We have both permanent professionals and a specialist contractor population. How do you manage both — and if not, who manages the interface?"**
   - Exposes PeopleScout's absence of MSP capability.

4. **"Affinix tracks time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, and conversion rates. How do you track quality of hire 12 months in — and can you show a BFSI example where your delivery model demonstrably improved that outcome?"**
   - Moves evaluation from volume efficiency to quality of outcome.

5. **"What is your team's specific experience managing a hiring programme inside a regulated financial institution — navigating Risk Committee approval, compliance sign-off, and data governance for outsourced delivery?"**
   - Tests BFSI governance depth where PeopleScout is weak.

6. **"TrueBlue's portfolio is primarily industrial and operational staffing. In professional BFSI markets — particularly APAC — how does that parent brand association affect candidate perception of your programme?"**
   - A legitimate brand association question with direct impact on hiring outcomes.

- Affinix platform — mobile-first candidate experience, AI-assisted volume sourcing

### RWO does better:

- APAC regional depth — Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong — 40 years on the ground
- Integrated RPO + MSP/contingent workforce — permanent and specialist contractors together
- Talent advisory and market intelligence (RW Insights) for TA leadership
- Candidate attraction via Robert Walters brand in APAC specialist professional markets
- Regulatory and governance navigation inside regulated financial institutions
- Long-term specialist partnership — TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award 2024

---

## The Bottom Line

**Choose PeopleScout if:** Your primary challenge is high-volume, operational-grade permanent hiring in the US and UK, Affinix technology is a central requirement, and employer brand and digital recruitment marketing are part of your evaluation.

**Choose RWO if:** Your primary sector is BFSI, your hiring involves specialist professionals in the UK and Asia Pacific, you need integrated permanent and contingent workforce management, and you want a partner with deep sector expertise and 40 years of on-the-ground knowledge in the markets where your most complex hiring sits.

The critical insight: PeopleScout and RWO are not alternatives to each other — they are built for different problems. The mistake buyers make is using the same evaluation criteria for both. Volume efficiency and specialist quality are not measured on the same scale.
