## Executive Summary

- **Who they are:** Allegis Global Solutions (AGS) is the RPO and MSP/Total Workforce Solutions division of Allegis Group — one of the world's largest privately held staffing companies, owner of Aerotek, TEKsystems, and other major US staffing brands. AGS focuses specifically on enterprise-scale talent outsourcing — full RPO, MSP/contingent workforce management, and total talent management — primarily for Fortune 500 and large global enterprises.

- **Primary strength:** Allegis Group's vast private staffing infrastructure underpins AGS's MSP capability in a way that few competitors can match. Aerotek and TEKsystems — two of the US's largest staffing firms — provide AGS's MSP clients with a deep, owned staffing supply chain for contingent workforce. Combined with enterprise-scale delivery infrastructure and a focus on compliance governance and complex workforce management, AGS is a formidable competitor on large, US-centric, enterprise MSP mandates.

- **Primary weakness:** US-centric to a significant degree; generalist in sector positioning; APAC infrastructure is thin for specialist professional RPO; and the Allegis Group private ownership structure limits financial transparency and creates questions about long-term strategic direction for clients making multi-year outsourcing commitments. AGS is primarily oriented toward large Fortune 500 enterprise clients — mid-market and BFSI specialist mandates are less well served.

- **Key competitive dynamic vs. RWO:** AGS and RWO are direct competitors primarily on the MSP/CWM side — both Everest Group-recognised providers of contingent workforce management. On RPO, they compete less directly — AGS is US-enterprise-focused, RWO is BFSI-specialist-APAC-focused. The key battleground is any blended RPO + MSP mandate for a large BFSI enterprise with US and APAC operations simultaneously.

- **Single most important thing RWO should know:** AGS's strongest ground is Fortune 500 MSP at very large US enterprise scale — backed by Allegis Group's Aerotek and TEKsystems supply chain. For BFSI specialist MSP (compliance contractors, risk specialists, interim finance), the quality of the AGS/Allegis supply chain for specialist professional categories is the key vulnerability. RWO should challenge this directly: "AGS can give you scale. Can they give you the specialist BFSI professional contractor supply that your compliance and risk function actually needs?"

## 1. Company Overview & Ownership

### Origins and Structure

Allegis Global Solutions was established as the outsourcing arm of Allegis Group — a US-based, privately held staffing conglomerate founded in 1983. Allegis Group's primary brands include:
- **Aerotek** — engineering, aviation, scientific, and industrial staffing
- **TEKsystems** — IT and technology staffing
- **Allegis Global Solutions (AGS)** — RPO and MSP/Total Talent
- **Major, Lindsey & Africa** — legal staffing and placement
- **Other specialist brands** across various sectors

AGS draws on the broader Allegis Group ecosystem — particularly Aerotek and TEKsystems — to deliver MSP programmes with owned staffing supply. This integrated supply model is a genuine competitive differentiator for technology and engineering-sector contingent workforce programmes.

### Ownership

**Allegis Group is privately held** — one of the largest privately held companies in the United States. This private ownership means:
- No public financial reporting — limited visibility into AGS's revenue, profitability, and strategic investment levels
- No PE exit pressure — ownership is long-term family/founder controlled
- Strategic alignment with Allegis Group's broader staffing interests — which can create tension between AGS's role as a "vendor-neutral" MSP and Allegis's interest in placing its own staffing brands

The private ownership and the Allegis Group staffing conflict-of-interest question are both legitimate due diligence items for any client evaluating AGS for a vendor-neutral MSP engagement.

### Scale and Headcount

[Inferred] AGS employs several thousand dedicated RPO and MSP professionals globally. Their effective scale, when drawing on Allegis Group shared services and supply, is materially larger than AGS's direct headcount implies.

### Global Footprint

AGS has genuine delivery capability across:
- **United States:** Primary and dominant market; deepest delivery infrastructure; largest client base
- **United Kingdom:** Established second market
- **Continental Europe:** Germany, Netherlands, France — moderate depth
- **Asia Pacific:** India (major delivery hub), Singapore, Australia — [Inferred] present but thinner for specialist professional RPO
- **Middle East and Latin America:** [Inferred] limited; primarily served from US or regional hubs

## 2. Service Architecture

### What AGS Offers

**Full RPO**
End-to-end permanent hiring outsourcing — sourcing, screening, assessment, offer management, onboarding. AGS's RPO model draws on Allegis Group technology investment and is positioned for enterprise-scale Fortune 500 clients with complex, multi-geography permanent hiring requirements.

**MSP / Contingent Workforce Management**
This is AGS's most established and differentiated capability. Managing large enterprise contractor, freelancer, and SOW worker populations through vendor-neutral or master vendor models. AGS's MSP offering is uniquely differentiated by the Allegis Group staffing supply chain — for technology and engineering contingent workforce specifically, the ability to draw on TEKsystems and Aerotek as primary suppliers is a material competitive advantage.

**Total Talent Management**
Integrating permanent RPO and contingent MSP under one commercial relationship with unified analytics, reporting, and governance. AGS's Total Talent pitch is credible — they have the infrastructure to genuinely deliver both sides of the equation.

**Direct Sourcing and Talent Community**
[Inferred] AGS has invested in direct sourcing capability — building client-owned contractor talent communities to reduce agency dependency and improve contingent workforce quality and cost.

**Workforce Analytics**
Comprehensive spend analytics, supplier performance management, and workforce planning tools for contingent workforce programmes. This is a mature capability reflecting years of managing large enterprise MSP programmes.

## 3. Positioning & Messaging

### Primary Positioning Claim

AGS positions as the **enterprise workforce solutions partner for complexity** — a provider that manages the full spectrum of workforce acquisition (permanent and contingent) for large, global enterprises with sophisticated workforce governance requirements. Their narrative:

1. **Enterprise scale:** Fortune 500 orientation — built for the largest, most complex global workforce programmes
2. **Total Talent:** Integrated permanent + contingent under one model
3. **Compliance and governance:** Specialisation in complex, compliance-heavy workforce programmes — a genuine strength for regulated industries
4. **Allegis ecosystem:** Access to Aerotek and TEKsystems supply for technology and engineering contingent roles
5. **Analytics:** Mature workforce spend and performance analytics

### The Conflict-of-Interest Problem

AGS's most significant positioning vulnerability is the Allegis Group conflict of interest in vendor-neutral MSP. A vendor-neutral MSP is supposed to select the best supplier for each contingent role, regardless of which firm it is. But AGS is owned by Allegis Group, which includes Aerotek and TEKsystems — both of which are suppliers that would benefit from placement into AGS-managed programmes.

Clients and analysts have raised this conflict repeatedly. AGS manages it through governance structures and firewalls, but the perception issue persists. For BFSI clients considering an AGS vendor-neutral MSP, this is a legitimate due diligence question.

**RWO counter:** "RWO has no staffing parent conflict in our MSP delivery. We're a division of Robert Walters Group — a specialist professional recruiter — not a volume staffing conglomerate. Our supplier selection in MSP is genuinely independent. When we choose a supply partner for your compliance contractor needs, it's based on quality, not internal group revenue."

## 5. Geographic & Sector Depth

### Where AGS is Genuinely Strong

- **United States:** Primary market; deepest delivery infrastructure; largest client base — Fortune 500 US enterprises are their core
- **United Kingdom:** Established presence; significant delivery team
- **India:** Major offshore delivery hub — significant operational headcount

### Where the Claim Thins

- **Japan and specialist APAC:** AGS's APAC presence is built around India as an operational hub, not specialist professional on-the-ground networks in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia. For specialist BFSI professional RPO in these markets, AGS is not equipped to compete with RWO.
- **Specialist BFSI professional:** AGS's sector depth is strongest in technology (TEKsystems) and engineering (Aerotek) — not in banking, financial services, and insurance specialist professional hiring. Their BFSI experience is primarily operational-grade or technology-for-finance, not specialist BFSI professional.

### Sector Depth

AGS's primary sector strengths follow the Allegis Group family:
- **Technology** — TEKsystems supply chain advantage
- **Engineering and sciences** — Aerotek supply chain advantage
- **Financial services** — operational; limited specialist professional BFSI depth
- **Healthcare and life sciences** — present
- **Consumer and retail** — present

For BFSI specialist professional hiring and MSP, the AGS/Allegis supply chain is not oriented around compliance, risk, treasury, and investment operations professionals — which are the specialist BFSI contractor categories that matter to RWO's clients.

## 8. Recent News & Strategic Direction

### Strategic Direction (as of August 2025)

AGS's strategic trajectory:

1. **Total Talent expansion** — converting MSP-only clients to integrated RPO + MSP engagements
2. **Direct sourcing** — building client-owned contractor communities as an alternative to traditional agency supply
3. **Technology investment** — analytics, AI-assisted matching, and workforce planning tool development
4. **Global expansion** — selective growth in EMEA and APAC; India remains the primary non-US delivery hub

### Allegis Group Context

Allegis Group's broader business — Aerotek, TEKsystems — is cyclically sensitive to technology and industrial staffing markets. AGS represents a more recurring revenue model within the group, making it a strategic priority for diversification. However, the strategic alignment between AGS (supposed vendor neutrality) and Allegis Group's staffing brands (benefiting from AGS client programmes) remains a persistent tension.

## 9. Commercial Model & Pricing Signals

[Inferred] AGS operates on:
- **Management fee as percentage of spend** — for MSP programmes; standard model
- **Per-hire plus management fee** — for RPO
- **Long-term enterprise contracts** — 3–5 years; Fortune 500 relationships are long-term by nature

### Pricing Tier

[Inferred] AGS is positioned in the **mid-to-premium tier** — reflecting enterprise-grade delivery, compliance capability, and Allegis Group infrastructure. AGS is oriented toward **Fortune 500 and large enterprise** — significant contingent workforce populations (hundreds to thousands of contractors) and/or large-scale RPO programmes. Mid-market clients with smaller scope are unlikely to receive the same investment of delivery resource and leadership attention.
