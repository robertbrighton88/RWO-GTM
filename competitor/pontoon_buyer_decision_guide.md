# Buyer Decision Guide — Pontoon vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO and MSP providers. This guide is designed to help you make a well-informed decision rather than advocate for a particular provider.

---

## What This Guide Does

Pontoon and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) both offer RPO and MSP/contingent workforce management — and will appear on the same shortlists for blended workforce mandates. This guide helps you understand where each genuinely excels so you can make the right choice for your specific workforce context.

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: You are a BFSI enterprise managing both permanent and contingent specialist professionals

**Recommended: RWO**

For banks, asset managers, and insurers managing specialist professional workforces — both permanent hires and contingent professionals in compliance, risk, credit, technology, and operations — RWO's integrated RPO and MSP model is built for exactly this. The Robert Walters professional network at the contractor supply level is oriented toward specialist BFSI disciplines in a way that Adecco's broad generalist supply chain is not.

Pontoon can manage your contingent workforce programme — but the quality of specialist BFSI contractor supply from an Adecco-backed supply chain is materially different from the specialist professional network RWO brings to the same problem.

---

### Scenario 2: You have a large, geographically diverse contingent workforce across multiple non-BFSI sectors

**Recommended: Pontoon**

For large enterprise clients with significant contingent workforce populations across technology, consumer, manufacturing, and professional services — particularly in the US and Western Europe — Pontoon's Adecco Group supply chain, automation-first delivery model, and MSP scale are genuine advantages. The breadth of Adecco's contractor network across diverse industries is a material differentiator for multi-sector programmes where RWO's specialist BFSI focus is less directly relevant.

---

### Scenario 3: APAC is a significant part of your mandate

**Recommended: RWO**

Adecco has a large presence in APAC — including Japan, where Adecco is one of the largest staffing companies — but Adecco's APAC infrastructure is built for temporary and general staffing, not specialist professional RPO and MSP. The Adecco brand in Japan is associated with temporary placement across general roles, not with specialist banking, compliance, and technology professional hiring.

Robert Walters has operated in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia for nearly 40 years as a specialist professional recruiter — which translates directly into a stronger RPO and MSP candidate network for BFSI and professional roles in these markets.

---

### Scenario 4: Technology and automation are primary evaluation criteria

**Evaluate both carefully**

Pontoon's automation-first positioning and spend management analytics are mature and Adecco Group-backed. If contingent workforce spend transparency, automation of contractor management workflows, and predictive workforce planning are your primary technology requirements, Pontoon's capability is well-developed.

RWO's technology advantage is oriented differently: RW Plus powers their delivery engine, and RW Insights gives your TA leadership real-time external market intelligence — talent pool data, salary benchmarks, competitor hiring signals — that is surfaced as a client-facing decision tool rather than an internal reporting dashboard.

Ask both providers to demonstrate their technology in the context of your specific use case — spend management vs. market intelligence are different needs.

---

### Scenario 5: Direct sourcing is a strategic priority

**Recommended: Pontoon**

If your organisation is pursuing a direct sourcing strategy — building a private talent community of pre-engaged contractors to reduce agency dependency and lower contingent workforce cost — Pontoon has invested specifically in this capability. RWO's direct sourcing model is [Inferred] less developed as a standalone commercial offering.

---

### Scenario 6: Talent advisory alongside delivery is part of your requirement

**Recommended: RWO**

Pontoon's advisory capability is embedded in programme delivery — not structured as a distinct commercial service. RWO's Workforce Consultancy is its fastest-growing service line (+51% NFI growth) and is explicitly offered as a strategic advisory capability alongside operational delivery. If you want a partner who can help your TA leadership think about workforce planning, talent market dynamics, and strategic talent decisions — not just manage a process — RWO is better positioned.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

1. **"For our BFSI contractor population — compliance, risk, technology, operations specialists — show me your supply chain. Which of your suppliers specifically focus on these disciplines? And how does that compare to what the broader Adecco network provides for generalist roles?"**
   - Forces Pontoon to reveal whether their supply chain genuinely serves specialist BFSI or defaults to generalist contractor supply.

2. **"In Japan specifically — where we have significant hiring — who is actually delivering for us? Is it an Adecco temporary staffing team, or a specialist professional RPO team with a professional-grade candidate network?"**
   - Tests APAC delivery quality directly.

3. **"What brand does a candidate see when they receive outreach through your programme? And in APAC professional markets, how do candidates in banking and financial services respond to that brand?"**
   - Brand association in candidate markets is a legitimate and testable differentiator.

4. **"Walk me through how you adapt your delivery model for the governance, compliance, and risk environment inside a regulated financial institution. What does your experience look like inside a Tier 1 bank's operations function?"**
   - Tests BFSI sector knowledge depth.

5. **"What is Pontoon's strategic priority within Adecco Group — specifically when the staffing business is under margin pressure? How does that translate to investment in our account?"**
   - A legitimate strategic risk question for a long-term outsourcing commitment.

6. **"Show me your technology capability for [their primary need: spend management OR market intelligence OR sourcing analytics]. What does that look like in a real client deployment?"**
   - Separates technology marketing from functional capability.

## The Bottom Line

**Choose Pontoon if:** Your primary challenge is managing a large, geographically diverse, multi-sector contingent workforce — particularly in the US and Western Europe — where the breadth of Adecco's supply chain, automation capability, and direct sourcing model are your primary requirements.

**Choose RWO if:** Your primary sector is BFSI, your critical hiring and contractor markets include the UK and Asia Pacific, and the quality of specialist professional talent — permanent or contingent — matters more than the breadth of a generalist supply chain.

The most common mistake in this comparison is assuming that scale and supply chain breadth are universal advantages. For specialist BFSI workforce management, depth of sector knowledge and quality of specialist supply network are more reliable predictors of programme success than the size of the parent company's staffing business.
