## Executive Summary

- **Who they are:** PeopleScout is one of the world's largest RPO providers — a subsidiary of TrueBlue, Inc. (NYSE: TBI), a US-listed workforce solutions company. PeopleScout specialises in high-volume, technology-driven permanent hiring at enterprise scale, with their proprietary **Affinix** talent acquisition platform as their primary differentiator. They operate primarily in North America and the UK, with growing global reach.

- **Primary strength:** Affinix technology platform and high-volume RPO delivery at scale. PeopleScout's investment in talent acquisition technology — AI-assisted matching, mobile-first candidate experience, analytics dashboards — is among the most mature in the RPO market. They are built for clients with large volumes of mid-level and operational permanent hiring requirements, particularly in sectors like retail, healthcare, logistics, and financial services operations.

- **Primary weakness:** Volume-first delivery model creates a ceiling on specialist and senior professional hiring quality. PeopleScout is not built for mid-to-senior specialist roles in BFSI — their model excels at scale and throughput, not at deep sector expertise or relationship-led specialist candidate attraction. APAC presence is thin. No MSP capability documented.

- **Key competitive dynamic vs. RWO:** PeopleScout and RWO rarely compete on the same ground — PeopleScout is a volume RPO provider, RWO is a specialist quality provider. When they do appear on the same shortlist (typically for BFSI operational hiring roles or mixed-tier RPO mandates), the question is: does this client need throughput or does it need specialist quality? The answer for BFSI clients is almost always the latter — and on that ground, RWO is decisively stronger.

- **Single most important thing RWO should know:** Affinix is PeopleScout's genuine differentiator and will be their primary pitch weapon. RWO must be prepared to counter it with RW Plus and RW Insights — and then shift the conversation from technology specification to sector outcomes: "Technology matters. But technology in service of volume throughput is not the same as technology in service of specialist quality in a regulated sector. What does your BFSI organisation actually need?"

## 1. Company Overview & Ownership

### Origins and Structure

PeopleScout was acquired by TrueBlue, Inc. — a US-listed workforce solutions company — and operates as TrueBlue's RPO specialist brand. TrueBlue's broader portfolio includes staffing brands focused on industrial and light manufacturing sectors, making PeopleScout the premium RPO offering within a wider workforce services group.

Headquartered in Chicago, Illinois, PeopleScout has grown through organic expansion and acquisitions — including the acquisition of TMP Worldwide (a talent acquisition and employer brand agency) which significantly expanded their employer brand and digital capabilities.

### Ownership

**TrueBlue, Inc.** — publicly listed (NYSE: TBI). Listed parent structure provides financial transparency comparable to RWO's position within Robert Walters plc / Recruit Holdings.

TrueBlue's broader portfolio is heavily weighted toward industrial and operational staffing — which creates a similar brand association challenge to Pontoon's Adecco problem: PeopleScout is the RPO brand within a parent company primarily known for light industrial and blue-collar staffing. In specialist professional markets, the TrueBlue parentage is not a selling point.

### Scale and Headcount

[Inferred] PeopleScout employs several thousand professionals globally — larger than WilsonHCG and Hudson RPO, broadly comparable to or slightly smaller than Cielo in RPO-dedicated headcount. Their delivery infrastructure is strongest in North America and the UK.

### Global Footprint

PeopleScout operates across:
- **United States:** Primary and strongest market; significant delivery infrastructure
- **United Kingdom:** Established second market; meaningful delivery presence
- **Australia:** [Inferred] present — TrueBlue has broader APAC presence via sister brands
- **Asia Pacific (other):** [Inferred] limited; not a primary strategic market for PeopleScout RPO
- **Europe:** [Inferred] selective; UK is the primary European market

Their claimed global reach (often cited as 70+ countries) represents partnered delivery rather than owned infrastructure in each market.

## 2. Service Architecture

### What PeopleScout Offers

**Full RPO**
End-to-end permanent hiring outsourcing — their core product. PeopleScout's full RPO is distinguished by high-volume delivery capability and the Affinix technology platform integration. Their model is optimised for consistent, scalable delivery of large hiring programmes rather than specialist, relationship-led delivery of small volumes of difficult roles.

**Project RPO**
Time-limited RPO engagements for specific hiring programmes. PeopleScout offers project RPO with Affinix technology integration — useful for volume hiring bursts during business transformation, expansion, or seasonal programmes.

**Affinix-Powered RPO**
Their technology platform (Affinix) is positioned as a complete talent acquisition ecosystem — not just a delivery tool but a client-facing platform. Some clients engage PeopleScout specifically to access Affinix alongside delivery services.

**Employer Brand and Talent Marketing**
Following the TMP Worldwide acquisition, PeopleScout offers employer brand development, EVP design, digital recruitment marketing, and candidate experience design as complementary services. This is a genuine and more developed capability than most pure-play RPO providers.

**Talent Advisory**
[Inferred] Advisory services embedded in delivery — TA transformation, workforce planning, and hiring process design. Less developed as a standalone commercial service than RWO's Workforce Consultancy.

## 3. Positioning & Messaging

### Primary Positioning Claim

PeopleScout positions as the **technology-powered RPO partner for high-volume enterprise hiring** — combining Affinix's AI-driven talent acquisition platform with global delivery scale. Their core narrative:

1. **Affinix platform:** Proprietary, purpose-built talent acquisition technology — mobile-first, AI-assisted, analytics-rich
2. **Scale and global reach:** 70+ countries, enterprise-scale delivery capacity
3. **Employer brand integration:** TMP Worldwide capability — EVP, digital recruitment marketing, candidate experience
4. **Data-driven hiring:** Analytics and market intelligence built into delivery through Affinix
5. **Volume efficiency:** Cost-per-hire optimisation and hiring velocity at scale

### "Why PeopleScout" Argument

PeopleScout's sales narrative is built on technology + scale: "We've built the most sophisticated RPO technology platform in the market, and we deliver at scale. Affinix gives your TA team visibility, analytics, and AI-assisted sourcing. Our scale means we can handle your volume." This is a credible argument for volume-oriented buyers.

### Technology Narrative — Affinix

Affinix is PeopleScout's most distinctive asset. The platform is positioned as a comprehensive talent acquisition ecosystem:
- **AI-assisted candidate matching** — automated shortlisting and skills matching
- **Mobile-first application experience** — simplified candidate journey designed to reduce drop-off
- **Talent marketplace** — gig-economy inspired interface for direct candidate engagement
- **Analytics and reporting** — real-time hiring pipeline data, diversity metrics, cost-per-hire tracking
- **Market intelligence** — labour market data integrated into the delivery platform

Affinix is a genuine competitive differentiator in technology-led evaluations. RWO must be prepared to present RW Plus and RW Insights directly against it — and then anchor the conversation on sector outcomes rather than platform specification.

### Employer Brand Differentiation

The TMP Worldwide acquisition gives PeopleScout a genuine employer brand capability that most pure-play RPO providers lack. This is relevant when employer brand quality is a stated requirement alongside RPO delivery.

- **United Kingdom:** Strong second market — significant presence including major client relationships
- **Australia:** [Inferred] growing; TrueBlue Group has broader Australian operations

### Sector Depth

PeopleScout's primary sectors are **healthcare, retail, financial services operations, logistics, technology, and government/public sector**. Their financial services experience is oriented toward operational and transactional roles — call centre, branch banking, insurance processing — rather than the specialist professional BFSI roles (compliance, risk, investment operations, treasury, regulatory affairs) that define RWO's core market.

This is a critical distinction: PeopleScout can fill BFSI roles at volume. They cannot match RWO's expertise in specialist BFSI professional hiring, regulatory-adjacent roles, or the governance navigation required to build teams inside regulated financial institutions.

## 6. Technology Stack

### Affinix Platform

Affinix is PeopleScout's proprietary talent acquisition platform — positioned as a complete ecosystem rather than a point solution. Documented capabilities:

- **AI-powered matching:** Candidate-to-role matching using skills and competency algorithms
- **Mobile-first experience:** Simplified application process designed for candidate drop-off reduction
- **Talent marketplace:** Direct candidate engagement and pipeline building
- **Real-time analytics:** Hiring pipeline visibility, time-to-fill, source-of-hire, diversity metrics
- **ATS/HRIS integration:** Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle, Taleo, and others
- **Market intelligence module:** Labour market data and benchmarking

Affinix is a genuine investment and a strong technology story. In pure technology specification comparisons, it will likely score well against RW Plus on individual feature dimensions.

**RWO counter:** "Affinix is purpose-built for volume throughput and operational efficiency. RW Plus and RW Insights serve a different purpose — specialist delivery quality and real-time external market intelligence for your TA leadership. The technology question isn't which platform has more features — it's which platform serves your specific hiring challenge better. For BFSI specialist hiring, Affinix's volume-optimisation architecture is less relevant than a platform that knows your talent market."

### TMP Worldwide Digital Capabilities

The TMP acquisition gave PeopleScout expertise in:
- Digital recruitment marketing — programmatic advertising, SEO for job postings
- Social media candidate attraction
- EVP and employer brand content creation
- Candidate journey design and testing

These are genuine complementary capabilities that make PeopleScout's employer brand story more credible than most pure-play RPO providers.

## 9. Commercial Model & Pricing Signals

### Contract Structure

[Inferred] PeopleScout operates on:
- **Per-hire plus management fee** — standard RPO commercial model
- **Volume-linked pricing** — cost efficiencies at higher hire volumes; pricing structure favours large programmes
- **Long-term enterprise contracts** — preferred model; Affinix implementation creates switching cost lock-in

### Pricing Tier

[Inferred] PeopleScout is positioned in the **mid-market tier** — below Korn Ferry RPO, AMS, and Cielo in per-hire cost, and competitive with WilsonHCG. Their volume-optimised model creates cost efficiency at scale that per-hire pricing reflects.

For high-volume operational hiring, PeopleScout may offer more competitive per-hire economics than specialist providers. For specialist, low-volume, high-quality hiring, their per-hire pricing relative to quality of outcome is less favourable.

### Minimum Engagement Size

[Inferred] PeopleScout is best suited to **large enterprise** clients with significant hiring volumes — hundreds to thousands of hires annually. Their Affinix implementation and operational model is less suitable for mid-market clients with smaller, specialist hiring requirements.
