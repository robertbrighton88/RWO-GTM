# Buyer Decision Guide — WilsonHCG vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO providers. This guide is designed to help you make a well-informed decision rather than advocate for a particular provider.

---

## What This Guide Does

WilsonHCG and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) present a rare challenge in RPO evaluation: they sound very similar at the surface level. Both are quality-led, both are human-first, and both position themselves as long-term partners rather than transactional vendors. This guide helps you look past the shared philosophy to identify where each provider genuinely has depth — and where they don't.

## Why This Comparison Is Different

Most competitor comparisons are straightforward: one provider wins on scale, one wins on sector depth, and the choice follows from your priorities. WilsonHCG is different because they share RWO's philosophy. Both say: quality over volume, recruiters matter, relationships last.

The meaningful question is not which provider has the better values — it is which provider has the infrastructure, sector knowledge, geographic depth, and integrated capability to back those values in your specific context.

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: You are a BFSI enterprise with hiring in the UK and APAC

**Recommended: RWO**

WilsonHCG's quality-led positioning is real — but their BFSI sector expertise is generalist, and their APAC presence is new and thin. For a bank or asset manager hiring specialist professionals in London, Tokyo, Singapore, and Hong Kong — and managing those programmes inside complex governance environments — RWO has the sector-specific infrastructure that WilsonHCG is still building.

The Robert Walters brand in APAC professional markets — 40 years of specialist recruitment recognition — is a candidate attraction asset that WilsonHCG simply does not have in those markets.

---

### Scenario 2: You are a US-based enterprise with quality-led RPO as the primary requirement

**Recommended: WilsonHCG (or closely evaluate)**

If your primary hiring challenge is in the US, your sector is not BFSI, and your primary evaluation criterion is candidate experience quality and partnership culture, WilsonHCG is a genuinely strong option. Their US infrastructure is deeper than RWO's, their Baker's Dozen track record is a meaningful client satisfaction proof point, and their commercial model may be more flexible for mid-market mandates.

---

### Scenario 3: You need permanent and contingent workforce managed together

**Recommended: RWO**

WilsonHCG is a pure-play permanent RPO provider. They do not offer MSP or contingent workforce management. If your talent challenge includes specialist contractors, compliance consultants, interim professionals, or any meaningful contingent workforce alongside permanent hiring, WilsonHCG cannot be your integrated partner.

RWO's Everest Group Star Performer recognition in CWM/MSP 2025 reflects genuine, invested capability in exactly this space.

---

### Scenario 4: Candidate experience and NPS are your primary evaluation metrics

**Recommended: WilsonHCG (with scrutiny)**

WilsonHCG's NPS focus is genuine and their Baker's Dozen recognition reflects real client satisfaction. If candidate experience scoring is your primary procurement metric, their track record is a meaningful advantage.

However, before making this the deciding factor, consider: NPS at hire completion is a useful metric, but it measures a narrow outcome. For a 3–5 year outsourcing relationship inside a BFSI enterprise, the quality dimensions that matter most are quality of hire, retention 12 months in, and the provider's ability to operate effectively within your governance environment. Ask both providers how they measure and track these.

---

### Scenario 5: Talent advisory is part of your requirement

**Recommended: RWO**

WilsonHCG's advisory capability is embedded in delivery — not a distinct commercial service line with its own investment and leadership. RWO's Workforce Consultancy is its fastest-growing area (+51% NFI growth) and includes RW Insights — a real-time talent market intelligence platform that gives TA leadership external data for strategic decisions.

If strategic advisory is genuinely part of what you need from an outsourcing partner — not just operational delivery — RWO has the more developed and commercially structured capability.

---

### Scenario 6: Long-term ownership stability matters to your decision

**Recommended: RWO**

WilsonHCG is backed by Great Hill Partners (PE). For a 3–5 year outsourcing commitment, PE ownership introduces legitimate risk: exit events create leadership disruption, account team changes, and potential operational resets. This is not inevitable, but it is a factor worth discussing explicitly.

RWO operates within a publicly listed group — Robert Walters plc, part of Recruit Holdings — with transparent governance and a long-term structural commitment. This shared listed-group credential is a differentiator from PE-backed competitors.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

1. **"You both claim quality-led, human-first delivery. Show me what that means specifically for a BFSI hiring programme in Tokyo or Singapore — who are the recruiters, what is their professional network, and how long have they been in that market?"**
   - Forces specificity behind shared philosophy.

2. **"How do you measure quality of hire 12 months after placement — and can you share a BFSI client example where your delivery model demonstrably improved hire quality and retention?"**
   - Moves evaluation beyond NPS at hire completion to the outcomes that matter.

3. **"We have both permanent professionals and a contingent specialist workforce. How does your model manage both — and if it doesn't, how do you recommend we manage the interface?"**
   - WilsonHCG's answer will reveal the MSP gap immediately.

4. **"What is your ownership structure, and what does that mean for leadership continuity, delivery team stability, and strategic investment over the life of a 3–5 year contract?"**
   - Tests PE ownership risk explicitly.

5. **"Beyond candidate NPS — what are the quality metrics you track that most predict successful BFSI hiring outcomes? And how has your delivery model evolved based on those metrics?"**
   - Separates surface-level quality claims from operational quality discipline.

6. **"How does your team adapt specifically for the governance, risk sign-off, and compliance requirements inside a regulated financial institution — and what experience do your delivery leads have with those environments?"**
   - Tests BFSI sector depth where WilsonHCG is thin.

## The Bottom Line

**Choose WilsonHCG if:** Your primary challenge is US-centric RPO, your sector is not BFSI, candidate experience NPS is your primary evaluation metric, and MSP/contingent workforce management is not in scope.

**Choose RWO if:** Your primary sector is BFSI, your hiring spans the UK and Asia Pacific, you need integrated permanent and contingent workforce management, and you want a specialist partner with 40 years of depth in the markets and sector that matter most to your organisation.

The most important question in this comparison is not "which provider sounds better?" — they will both sound similar. It is "which provider has the specific infrastructure, sector knowledge, and geographic depth for my actual hiring context?" On that question, the answer for most BFSI enterprises is clear.
