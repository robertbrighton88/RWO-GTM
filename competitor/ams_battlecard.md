## The Core Battle

AMS will try to win this deal on **scale, analyst rank, and Total Talent breadth**.

Your job is to make the conversation about **specialist sector depth, APAC quality, and partnership**.

> "AMS can cover 120 countries. The question is: how well do they know yours?"

## Questions to Ask That Expose AMS Weaknesses

Use these to open up RWO's strongest ground:

**On sector depth:**
> "How many of AMS's delivery team have experience working specifically inside a Tier 1 bank, managing a hiring programme through a Risk Committee? For us, that's a standard part of BFSI delivery — not a speciality."

**On APAC quality:**
> "When you're hiring in Japan, how does AMS source candidates locally? Do they have Japanese-speaking recruiters on the ground, or is sourcing managed from a regional hub in Singapore or India? We've been operating in Japan for [X] years — our candidate network is built, not assembled."

**On candidate brand:**
> "When a candidate in Singapore or Hong Kong receives an outreach message for this role — what brand will they see on it? AMS's client is white-label by design. Robert Walters is recognised as a premium employer brand. Does the ability to attract stronger candidates — faster — have a value for your organisation?"

**On Total Talent vs. quality:**
> "AMS's Total Talent model integrates permanent and contingent workforce. That's an elegant commercial structure. But what percentage of your hiring is actually contingent workforce vs. specialist permanent? And does your BFSI governance model allow for joint management of regulated and non-regulated workforce under one external provider?"

**On DEI vs. outcomes:**
> "If AMS's DEI programme produces diverse slates but the quality of specialist candidates isn't there — what does that actually solve for your business? We build diverse candidate pools because we have the sourcing depth to access them — not because we've automated bias tracking into a reporting tool."

**On process vs. partnership:**
> "What does your TA team actually want from an outsourcing relationship — a process provider that reports to SLA, or a partner that sits inside your organisation and thinks like your team? The experience of engaging a 10,000-person global operation is very different from working with a team that knows your business."

## When to Walk Away

AMS is genuinely the better choice — and you should walk away or qualify down — when:

- The client needs **truly global delivery** across 30+ countries simultaneously, including US and EMEA markets outside the UK, where RWO has limited infrastructure
- **Total Talent Management** across permanent, contingent, and SOW is the primary requirement and the client has the organisational maturity to adopt it
- **DEI programme reporting** is a primary procurement criterion being evaluated by a committee with formal DEI governance obligations
- The client is **Fortune 500 scale** with very high-volume (thousands of hires annually) across multiple sectors and geographies — not a BFSI specialist requirement
- The client explicitly requires **Everest Group Leader-tier** analyst validation to pass their procurement committee and you cannot make the case for RWO's Star Performer standing

**Do not win business you cannot serve well.** If a deal is fundamentally an AMS deal, withdrawing with professionalism — and flagging it to the prospect openly — builds more long-term credibility than winning and underdelivering.

## The Headline Narrative (One Paragraph)

> AMS is a strong, well-resourced, Everest Group Leader-tier competitor. Their scale is genuine and their Total Talent model is credible. But for BFSI organisations — banks, asset managers, insurers — the fundamental requirements of hiring are different from any other sector. You are hiring inside complex risk and compliance environments. You are building teams that need regulatory credibility. You are operating in APAC markets where on-the-ground knowledge, language capability, and candidate trust are the difference between finding the right person and settling for who responded. Robert Walters Outsourcing was built for this. AMS was built for scale. Those are not the same thing.
