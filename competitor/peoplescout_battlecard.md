## The Core Battle

PeopleScout will try to win this deal on **Affinix technology and high-volume delivery scale**.

Your job is to make the conversation about **specialist quality vs. volume throughput, BFSI sector depth, and integrated RPO + MSP**.

> "Affinix is a great platform for volume hiring. The question is whether your BFSI challenge is a volume problem or a specialist quality problem."

## Questions to Ask That Expose PeopleScout Weaknesses

**On volume vs. specialist quality:**
> "PeopleScout's model is designed for volume throughput — high-frequency operational and mid-level hiring. Our requirement is primarily specialist BFSI professionals: compliance officers, risk managers, investment operations analysts, regulatory specialists. How does PeopleScout's delivery model adapt for that specialist, relationship-led hiring — and what percentage of your current BFSI client book is at that professional grade level?"

**On APAC delivery:**
> "We have significant hiring in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia. For specialist BFSI professional roles in Tokyo — compliance managers, risk analysts, treasury professionals — how does PeopleScout source those candidates? Is it Affinix-driven digital sourcing, or do you have Japanese-speaking recruiters with professional-grade candidate networks on the ground?"

**On MSP/contingent workforce:**
> "Alongside our permanent BFSI professionals, we have a significant contractor population — compliance specialists, risk consultants, interim finance leads. How does PeopleScout manage that contingent workforce alongside the permanent RPO? And if it's out of scope, what does the interface look like between two separate providers?"

**On TrueBlue brand in professional markets:**
> "TrueBlue's broader portfolio is industrial and operational staffing. In professional BFSI markets — particularly APAC — how does that parent brand association translate to candidate perception? And specifically, does the 'TrueBlue group' brand help or hinder your ability to attract specialist banking and financial services professionals?"

**On Affinix vs. specialist outcomes:**
> "Affinix looks impressive for volume metrics — time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, application conversion rates. For our BFSI specialist hiring, the quality metric we care about is quality of hire 12 months in and retention inside a regulated environment. How does Affinix help with that — and can you share a BFSI client example?"

**On BFSI governance:**
> "Our hiring inside the bank involves navigating Risk Committee approval for outsourced delivery partners, compliance sign-off on sourcing processes, and data governance for candidate information. How does PeopleScout's delivery model adapt for those governance requirements — and what experience does your team have operating inside a Tier 1 bank's governance structure?"

## When to Walk Away

Walk away — or qualify down — only in these circumstances:

- **The requirement falls outside RWO's core sector expertise** — BFSI (banking, financial services, insurance) and Pharma & Life Sciences. If the client's primary need is volume hiring in technology, consumer, healthcare operations, or another sector where RWO has no specialist depth, PeopleScout's generalist volume model may genuinely be a better fit — and there is no competitive value in pursuing it.
- **A commercially viable engagement cannot be structured** for both RWO and the client — if the deal scope, volume, or commercial terms mean RWO cannot deliver quality outcomes profitably, or the client cannot achieve the value they need at a sustainable price point, decline cleanly.

**Do not walk away because PeopleScout has a more sophisticated technology platform, stronger US infrastructure, or better employer brand capabilities.** These are not reasons to concede a specialist BFSI or Pharma mandate. RWO's sector depth, APAC infrastructure, and quality of hire track record are exactly where PeopleScout's volume-first model falls short.

## The Headline Narrative (One Paragraph)

> PeopleScout is an excellent provider for what it does: high-volume, technology-powered permanent hiring at enterprise scale. Affinix is a real platform with real capabilities. But for a BFSI enterprise hiring specialist professionals in London, Tokyo, Singapore, and Hong Kong — managing compliance managers, risk analysts, and investment operations professionals inside the governance structures of regulated financial institutions — PeopleScout's volume-optimised model is the wrong instrument. Robert Walters Outsourcing was built for specialist professional hiring in exactly these markets and exactly this sector. That's not a technology claim. It's 40 years of on-the-ground expertise that Affinix cannot replicate.
