## The Core Battle

AGS will try to win this deal on **enterprise scale, Total Talent breadth, and Allegis Group supply chain**.

Your job is to make the conversation about **vendor-neutral independence, BFSI specialist supply quality, and APAC expertise**.

> "AGS brings the Allegis Group network. The question is whether a network built around technology and engineering staffing is the right supply chain for a BFSI specialist workforce."

## Questions to Ask That Expose AGS Weaknesses

**On vendor-neutral conflict of interest:**
> "AGS is owned by Allegis Group, which includes Aerotek and TEKsystems as staffing suppliers. In your proposed MSP model for our BFSI contractor programme — specifically for compliance specialists, risk managers, and treasury contractors — what percentage of supply would come from Allegis Group brands vs. independent specialist BFSI staffing firms? And how does your governance framework document and audit this?"

**On BFSI specialist supply quality:**
> "Your supply chain for our MSP programme would draw on Allegis Group brands — primarily TEKsystems and Aerotek. Both are excellent for technology and engineering contractors. For our specialist BFSI contractor categories — compliance officers, credit risk analysts, regulatory change specialists, treasury professionals — what percentage of TEKsystems and Aerotek's contractor base is in these disciplines? And what independent specialist BFSI suppliers would be in your supply chain?"

**On APAC delivery:**
> "We have significant contractor and permanent hiring requirements in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia — specifically in compliance, risk, and investment operations. AGS's APAC infrastructure is primarily based in India. Who is actually managing our BFSI specialist contractor supply in Tokyo and Singapore — and what is their professional network at mid-to-senior level in those markets?"

**On private ownership transparency:**
> "Allegis Group is privately held, so we can't review public financial statements. For a 3–5 year outsourcing commitment inside our institution, our governance team will ask: what assurance do we have about AGS's financial stability, leadership continuity, and strategic investment levels? How does AGS provide that assurance in the absence of public reporting?"

**On BFSI governance navigation:**
> "Bringing an outsourced MSP provider inside our bank requires Risk Committee approval, compliance sign-off on data governance, and ongoing audit engagement. How has AGS navigated that process with previous BFSI clients — specifically the risk governance framework for an outsourced contingent workforce programme inside a regulated institution?"

## When to Walk Away

AGS is genuinely the better choice — qualify down or walk away — when:

- **Fortune 500 multi-sector enterprise** with very large, diverse contingent workforce primarily in technology and engineering — the Allegis supply chain advantage is real in these categories
- **US-centric Total Talent Management** at very large enterprise scale — AGS's US infrastructure and enterprise delivery model is materially stronger
- **Allegis Group relationship** already exists at the account level — TEKsystems or Aerotek relationships that make AGS the natural consolidation play
- **Non-BFSI sector** where AGS's technology and engineering supply strength is the relevant differentiation

## The Headline Narrative (One Paragraph)

> Allegis Global Solutions is a well-resourced, enterprise-grade MSP and RPO provider — their Allegis Group supply chain is a genuine advantage for technology and engineering contingent workforce at scale. But for a BFSI enterprise managing specialist professional talent — compliance contractors, risk specialists, interim finance professionals — the right question is not how large the supply chain is, but how relevant it is. Allegis Group's network is built around technology and industrial staffing. Robert Walters Outsourcing's network was built around specialist BFSI professional talent over 40 years. For BFSI specialist MSP, that difference matters more than enterprise scale. And for a regulated institution's procurement committee: we have no staffing parent conflict of interest. AGS does.
