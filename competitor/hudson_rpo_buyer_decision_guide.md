# Choosing Between Hudson RPO and Robert Walters Outsourcing — A Decision Guide for HR and Procurement Leaders

> **About this guide:** This document is written for HR Directors, CHROs, Heads of Talent Acquisition, and Procurement leaders evaluating RPO and talent outsourcing partners in the Asia Pacific region. It is designed to be genuinely useful — not a one-sided endorsement of either provider. Where assessments are based on inference from available market information rather than verified data, this is noted. We recommend using this guide as a starting framework and testing all observations in your conversations with each provider.

---

## Overview

Hudson RPO and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) are two of the most credible specialist talent partners operating in Asia Pacific. Both position on quality rather than volume, both have genuine APAC delivery capability, and both have earned meaningful client relationships in the region. Choosing between them is not a straightforward decision — it depends heavily on the nature of your talent challenge, your geographic footprint, and how you define a successful outsourcing relationship.

This guide sets out the conditions under which each provider is likely the stronger choice, the trade-offs involved, and the questions you should ask both in any competitive evaluation.

**Important context:**
- Hudson RPO is a **pure-play APAC RPO specialist** — their entire business is built around recruitment process outsourcing in Asia Pacific.
- Robert Walters Outsourcing is an **integrated talent outsourcing specialist** — offering RPO, Managed Service Provider / Contingent Workforce Management (MSP/CWM), and Talent Advisory, across 60+ countries, with a particularly deep APAC heritage.

These are meaningfully different propositions. The right choice depends on which model fits your organisation's current and future needs.

## When Hudson RPO Is Likely the Better Choice

### 1. Your mandate is purely APAC-focused, with no current plans to extend to EMEA or the US

Hudson RPO's entire delivery model is built around Asia Pacific. If your immediate challenge is APAC-only — and you do not anticipate needing a single provider to also deliver into Europe or North America in the near future — Hudson's concentrated APAC focus may work in your favour. You will be dealing with a provider for whom APAC is everything, not one of several regional priorities.

### 2. You are entering one or two new APAC markets for the first time

Hudson RPO has built specific expertise in helping companies navigate new APAC markets — particularly the cultural, compliance, and candidate engagement complexities of markets like Japan, Singapore, or Malaysia. If your primary challenge is "we need to hire our first 20 people in Japan and we have never hired there before," Hudson's market entry speciality is a genuine differentiator.

### 3. You are a mid-market organisation with a defined, contained hiring scope

If your APAC hiring programme is relatively contained in volume and geography — say, 30–100 hires per year across one to three APAC markets — Hudson RPO's boutique specialist approach may be better calibrated to your scale. Mid-sized organisations can sometimes feel lost within the infrastructure of larger providers; Hudson's scale means your programme is more likely to be a meaningful account for their business.

### 4. Your primary hiring need is permanent headcount with no contingent workforce requirement

Hudson RPO's offering is focused on permanent hiring. If contingent workforce (contractors, temporary staff, project-based resources) is not part of your talent equation, you will not be paying for capability you do not need.

### 5. Australia is your primary or sole APAC market

Hudson RPO is headquartered in Brisbane and Australia is their home market — deepest team, longest relationships, strongest infrastructure. For Australian-focused mandates, Hudson is particularly well-positioned.

### 6. Cultural fluency in specific APAC markets is your primary evaluation criterion

Hudson RPO's core narrative around cultural intelligence is credible. If you are evaluating providers primarily on demonstrated understanding of how hiring works in specific Asian markets — and you are less concerned about global scale, technology platforms, or advisory services — Hudson makes a strong case.

## When Robert Walters Outsourcing Is Likely the Better Choice

### 1. You need both APAC and non-APAC hiring managed by a single provider

RWO delivers talent into 60+ countries and holds account teams in 23 countries. If your talent programme spans APAC and the UK, APAC and the US, or multiple regions simultaneously, RWO can manage this within a single contract and a single client relationship. Hudson RPO cannot service this in the same way and would need to introduce a third party for non-APAC delivery.

### 2. Your workforce includes both permanent hires and contingent workers

RWO offers MSP/Contingent Workforce Management alongside RPO — recognised by Everest Group as a Star Performer in the CWM/MSP PEAK Matrix 2025. If contractors, temporary staff, or statement of work arrangements are part of your workforce, RWO can manage your entire talent picture under one programme. Hudson RPO does not offer MSP.

### 3. You operate in Banking, Financial Services, or Insurance in APAC

RWO's primary sector expertise is BFSI — built over nearly 40 years across the UK and Asia Pacific. If you are hiring compliance, risk, finance, technology, or operations professionals within a banking or financial services environment — and particularly if you need a partner who understands how to navigate internal bank governance, risk frameworks, and regulatory requirements — RWO's sector depth is a meaningful differentiator. They understand not just the talent market but how banks actually work from the inside.

### 4. Japan and Singapore are strategic hiring markets for you

Robert Walters has operated in Japan and Singapore for approaching 40 years. The Robert Walters brand is well-recognised among professional candidates in both markets — this translates into measurably better candidate engagement when Robert Walters is running the recruitment programme. If attracting high-quality Japanese or Singaporean professionals is a priority, RWO's candidate brand recognition is a concrete operational advantage.

### 5. You want more than execution — you want strategic workforce insight

RWO offers a Talent Advisory and Workforce Consulting service line that goes well beyond RPO delivery — covering workforce strategy, skills gap analysis, ED&I advisory, employer brand consulting, and market intelligence (via the RW Insights platform). If you want a partner who can inform your workforce strategy, not just fill your requisitions, RWO's advisory capability is significantly more developed.

### 6. Real-time market intelligence is part of your evaluation

RWO's proprietary RW Insights platform provides real-time data on talent pools, competitor hiring activity, and salary benchmarking. If data-driven hiring decisions and talent market visibility matter to your HR or C-suite leadership, this is a capability Hudson RPO does not appear to match with an equivalent proprietary product.

### 7. You want a provider with documented, verified analyst recognition

RWO holds documented recognition from Everest Group (Star Performer, MSP/CWM PEAK Matrix 2025), HRO Today, SIA, TALiNT Partners (TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award 2024), and the Transform Awards. If analyst and award standing factors into your provider shortlisting or procurement governance, RWO's portfolio is well-documented and verifiable.

### 8. You are managing a large, complex, multi-country APAC programme

RWO operates with 6 global service centres and an enterprise-level delivery infrastructure. For large hiring programmes — multiple APAC markets simultaneously, high volumes, complex stakeholder environments — RWO's operational infrastructure is more robust. A mid-sized specialist like Hudson RPO may face capacity constraints at the upper end of programme complexity.

## Key Questions to Ask Both Providers

The following questions are designed to help you test each provider's claims rigorously. They apply to both Hudson RPO and RWO — ask the same questions of each and compare the quality and specificity of the answers.

### On APAC Delivery Depth

1. **"For [specific APAC market — Japan/Singapore/Malaysia], can you demonstrate local delivery capability — not managed from another country, but genuinely on-the-ground with local-language teams?"**
   *Why this matters: APAC delivery is only as good as the in-market capability. Test whether "presence" means a team or a sales office.*

2. **"What is the Robert Walters / your brand's candidate recognition in [Japan / Singapore]? How does that translate into response rates when reaching out to passive candidates?"**
   *Why this matters: In tight talent markets, who is making the call matters. Test whether the provider's brand opens doors or creates friction.*

3. **"Walk me through how you would source candidates for a senior [BFSI / Technology / Finance] role in [specific market]. What is your specific sourcing methodology and what networks do you draw on?"**
   *Why this matters: Distinguishes genuine sector and market expertise from generic sourcing.*

### On Service Scope

4. **"Do you offer Managed Service Provider / Contingent Workforce Management? If yes, is it within your own organisation or through a partnership? If we need to manage contractors and permanent hires within the same programme, how does that work in practice?"**
   *Why this matters: Reveals whether you would need two providers or can consolidate.*

5. **"Beyond filling requisitions, what strategic advisory services do you offer? If we need a workforce strategy review, skills gap analysis, or a diversity audit — is that within your scope, or would we need to engage a separate consultancy?"**
   *Why this matters: Distinguishes transactional RPO providers from strategic talent partners.*

6. **"If we need to expand this programme to include UK or US hiring in Year 2 — how would that work? Who would deliver it and under what commercial structure?"**
   *Why this matters: Tests global delivery capability without directly confronting either provider.*

### On Technology

7. **"What is your proprietary technology platform? What does it do that a generic ATS does not? Show me a live demonstration of the analytics and market intelligence capability."**
   *Why this matters: Distinguishes platforms from tool integrations. Ask for a demo — not a slide.*

8. **"How do you use AI in your delivery? Where does the AI actually make a decision or recommendation, and where does a human make the final call?"**
   *Why this matters: Many providers make AI claims that are primarily marketing. Test the specific use cases and the human-AI balance.*

### On BFSI and Regulatory Depth

9. **"Can you walk me through your experience managing hiring inside a financial services organisation with [MAS / JFSA / APRA / FCA] compliance requirements? What specific processes do you have for regulated role hiring?"**
   *Why this matters: Generic RPO providers often underestimate the compliance complexity of BFSI hiring. Test depth of regulatory knowledge.*

10. **"What is your approach to working inside a bank's internal governance environment — dealing with internal committees, risk approvals, and compliance sign-off for a new TA programme?"**
    *Why this matters: Building a new recruitment model inside a bank is operationally complex. This question distinguishes providers who have genuinely done it from those who have simply hired people for banks.*

### On Partnership Quality and Track Record

11. **"What is your longest-standing client relationship in APAC, and what does that partnership look like today versus how it began?"**
    *Why this matters: Long-term partnerships reveal whether the relationship evolves or remains transactional.*

12. **"Tell me about a client you lost in APAC in the last two years — what went wrong, and what did you learn?"**
    *Why this matters: How a provider discusses loss and failure reveals more about their culture and accountability than how they discuss success.*

## The Decision Framework

Use this framework to guide your final decision between the two providers.

### Step 1: Define Your Geographic Scope

- **APAC-only, no foreseeable non-APAC need:** Both providers are viable. Continue to Step 2.
- **Multi-region (APAC + EMEA or US) now or within 24 months:** Robert Walters Outsourcing. Hudson RPO cannot service this within a single provider relationship.

### Step 2: Define Your Workforce Type

- **Permanent hiring only:** Both providers are viable. Continue to Step 3.
- **Permanent and contingent workforce (contractors, temps, SOW):** Robert Walters Outsourcing. Hudson RPO does not offer MSP.

### Step 3: Define Your Sector

- **Banking, Financial Services, Insurance:** Robert Walters Outsourcing. The depth of BFSI sector expertise — regulatory knowledge, understanding of internal bank operating environments — is a meaningful differentiator.
- **Technology, professional services, or other sectors:** Both providers are viable. Continue to Step 4.

### Step 4: Define Your Primary APAC Markets

- **Australia-primary mandate:** Consider Hudson RPO seriously — this is their home market.
- **Japan and/or Singapore as primary markets:** Robert Walters Outsourcing. The Robert Walters candidate brand recognition in Japan and Singapore is a direct operational advantage in competitive talent markets.
- **Broad APAC (multiple markets simultaneously):** Robert Walters Outsourcing. Infrastructure and multi-country delivery capability at scale.

### Step 5: Define Your Advisory Needs

- **Execution-only — fill roles efficiently:** Both providers are viable. Evaluate on delivery quality, cultural expertise, and commercial terms.
- **Strategic advisory required — workforce strategy, market intelligence, ED&I:** Robert Walters Outsourcing. The advisory service line is not a meaningful part of Hudson RPO's offering.

### Step 6: Define Your Technology Expectations

- **Standard RPO delivery — process efficiency, reporting:** Both providers are viable.
- **Proprietary market intelligence and AI-powered analytics:** Robert Walters Outsourcing (RW Plus, RW Insights).

---

**A final note:** The most important thing any HR or procurement leader can do in this evaluation is ask both providers for references from clients with similar profiles — similar size, similar markets, similar hiring complexity. A provider's track record with a client who looks like you is more predictive of your likely experience than any pitch presentation. Ask specifically for APAC client references, for clients in your sector, and — if relevant — for clients who have grown the relationship from a single-country mandate into a broader programme over time.
