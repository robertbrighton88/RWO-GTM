# Hudson RPO Deep Dive — Research Plan

**Objective:** Produce comprehensive competitive intelligence on Hudson RPO to equip RWO with sharp positioning, a practical sales battlecard, and a buyer decision framework — with particular focus on APAC, which is the primary competitive battleground between these two firms.

**What we already know (from `competitive_positioning.md`):**
- APAC specialist — the most direct competitor to RWO in their strongest region
- International expansion expertise; cultural fluency
- Quality positioning; companies expanding into new geographies
- Smaller global scale; less brand recognition outside APAC
- RWO counter: 40-year brand heritage, integrated recruitment + outsourcing capability, broader APAC footprint

This plan goes 10x deeper.

---

## Research Workstreams

### 1. Hudson RPO Service Architecture & Delivery Model
**Goal:** Understand exactly what Hudson RPO sells and how they deliver it — with special attention to APAC capability.

**Research questions:**
- What are their exact RPO service tiers and delivery models?
- Do they offer MSP / Contingent Workforce Management or is it purely RPO?
- Do they offer Project RPO / Modular RPO equivalents?
- How do they handle international expansion mandates — what is the actual delivery model?
- How is delivery structured in APAC (onsite, nearshore, offshore)?
- What is their proprietary technology platform (if any)?
- Do they have a talent advisory / consulting layer?
- What is their cultural fluency / localisation claim in practice?

**Sources:** Hudson RPO website, solution briefs, case studies, webinars

### 10. Competitive Win/Loss Framework

**RWO wins when:**
- Robert Walters brand recognition in APAC is a hiring advantage (especially Japan, Singapore)
- BFSI specialist expertise at depth is required in APAC
- Client needs both APAC and UK/EMEA RPO in the same contract
- Integrated recruitment + outsourcing capability matters (RWO can draw on RW placement network)
- Advisory / market intelligence is part of the brief (RW Insights)
- MSP / Contingent Workforce Management is required alongside RPO
- Long-term partnership record matters (RWO's 40-year APAC heritage)

**Hudson RPO wins when:**
- Client is entering a new APAC market and needs a specialist in-market expansion guide
- Cultural fluency and on-the-ground APAC expertise is the primary requirement
- Client does not need UK/EMEA/US delivery — purely APAC-focused mandate
- Client is smaller mid-market and values a more focused, boutique APAC partner
- RWO's Robert Walters brand is seen as generalist in a specific market Hudson knows better

- Questions to ask that expose Hudson RPO's scale limitations outside APAC
