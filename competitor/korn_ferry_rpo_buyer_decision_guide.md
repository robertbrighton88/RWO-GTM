# Buyer Decision Guide — Korn Ferry RPO vs. Robert Walters Outsourcing

> **Audience:** HR and TA leaders, CHROs, CPOs, and procurement professionals evaluating RPO providers. This guide is designed to help you make a well-informed decision rather than advocate for a particular provider.

---

## What This Guide Does

Korn Ferry RPO and Robert Walters Outsourcing (RWO) overlap more than most competitor pairings in the RPO market — both operate at the quality end, both serve enterprise clients, and both bring genuine advisory depth alongside delivery. But they are built for meaningfully different use cases, and choosing the wrong one creates real operational and commercial friction.

## Scenario-by-Scenario Guidance

### Scenario 1: Your primary RPO scope is senior and leadership hiring

**Recommended: Korn Ferry RPO**

If your outsourced talent requirement is focused on Director, VP, MD, and C-suite hiring — roles where leadership competency assessment, succession calibration, and the depth of the Korn Ferry brand signal quality to candidates — Korn Ferry RPO is the more naturally suited provider. Their assessment frameworks, Pay and Workforce Intelligence, and Intelligence Cloud are genuinely valuable at this level of hiring in ways that general RPO delivery cannot match.

---

### Scenario 2: You are a BFSI enterprise with broad hiring needs across all grades

**Recommended: RWO**

For banks, asset managers, or insurers running an outsourced talent function that spans the full professional range — graduates, analysts, specialists, managers, directors, and senior leadership — RWO's delivery model is substantially better suited than Korn Ferry's. Korn Ferry's operational RPO delivery at mid-professional and specialist levels is less proven and significantly more expensive per hire than specialist RPO providers. RWO's 40 years of BFSI sector knowledge — including how to source compliance specialists, risk professionals, technology-for-finance teams, and treasury operations staff — spans the full hiring range.

---

### Scenario 3: You need integrated permanent and contingent workforce management

**Recommended: RWO**

Korn Ferry RPO is a permanent hiring solution. They do not offer MSP or contingent workforce management. If your talent challenge includes both a permanent professional hiring requirement and a contractor or specialist contingent workforce — common in BFSI — you need a provider who can manage both under one integrated model. RWO offers integrated RPO and MSP/CWM, with Everest Group Star Performer recognition in contingent workforce management in 2025.

---

### Scenario 4: The Korn Ferry exec search relationship already exists

**Consider carefully before choosing**

If your organisation already has a deep executive search relationship with Korn Ferry, expanding to RPO has a clear commercial logic: one firm, one relationship, one data set. Before proceeding, however, consider:

- **Conflict of interest:** Korn Ferry's exec search practice generates placement fees on individual searches; their RPO practice generates management fees. These incentive structures can create tension. Ask Korn Ferry explicitly how they manage this conflict.
- **Operational fit:** Exec search is episodic and high-touch; RPO is ongoing and requires operational scale. Ask to meet the RPO delivery team specifically and understand how they are resourced and managed relative to the core exec search business.
- **Pricing structure:** Executive search pricing is very different from RPO commercial models. Ensure the RPO contract is structured on RPO terms — management fee plus per-hire — rather than inflated by exec search margin assumptions.

---

### Scenario 5: Your critical hiring markets are in APAC

**Recommended: RWO**

Korn Ferry has genuine executive search capability in APAC — particularly in Australia, Singapore, and Japan. But executive search delivery in APAC and RPO delivery in APAC are fundamentally different:

- RPO requires volume sourcing, local-language capability, and on-the-ground candidate networks at professional (not just executive) levels
- In Japan specifically, the labour market requires Japanese-language recruiters with deep local knowledge — not exec search consultants who handle a small number of senior mandates annually
- The Robert Walters brand has 40 years of recognition in Japan, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Malaysia at the professional levels where RPO operates. Korn Ferry's brand recognition in these markets is primarily at the exec search tier.

---

### Scenario 6: Talent advisory and market intelligence are part of your requirement

**Evaluate both carefully**

Korn Ferry has a deeper strategic advisory capability — workforce analytics, leadership competency frameworks, succession planning tools. If you are buying a long-term talent strategy partner as well as delivery, Korn Ferry's advisory layer is more developed.

RWO's Talent Advisory practice is growing rapidly (+51% NFI) and includes real-time talent market intelligence (RW Insights) surfaced directly to TA leadership as a decision tool. RWO's advisory is more operationally oriented — helping TA leaders make better decisions in their hiring programmes — than Korn Ferry's advisory, which is more oriented toward C-suite talent strategy.

If you need both, consider whether one provider's strength in one area outweighs the other's, or whether a bifurcated approach (Korn Ferry for leadership succession advisory + RWO for operational RPO and MSP) is a realistic commercial option.

## The Questions You Should Ask Both Providers

1. **"Walk me through your delivery model for a compliance analyst role in Tokyo — who sources this candidate, in what language, from what network, and what is the expected time-to-fill?"**
   - Tests APAC RPO delivery quality directly. Korn Ferry's answer will reveal whether their APAC capability is exec search infrastructure or genuine RPO delivery.

2. **"What percentage of your RPO book of business is senior leadership hiring vs. professional operational hiring? And what does your delivery model look like at each tier?"**
   - Reveals whether Korn Ferry's RPO is truly multi-grade or primarily senior-focused.

3. **"Our workforce includes both permanent professionals and a significant contingent contractor population. How does your model handle both — and who manages the interface between permanent and contingent?"**
   - Exposes Korn Ferry's absence of MSP capability.

4. **"If we already engage Korn Ferry for executive search — how does the RPO team manage the potential conflict of interest between exec search placement fees and RPO management fees?"**
   - A critical governance question if a Korn Ferry exec search relationship exists.

5. **"What is the total cost of this programme — management fee, per-hire costs, and any analytics platform licensing — compared to specialist RPO market rates?"**
   - Forces Korn Ferry to price transparently against RPO benchmarks rather than exec search benchmarks.

6. **"Show me the team that will actually deliver my programme on day 90. Are they the same people presenting to me today — and how are they resourced relative to Korn Ferry's exec search and advisory businesses?"**
   - Tests whether RPO receives equivalent internal investment and talent to the core exec search business.

## Honest Assessment — What Each Provider Does Better

### Korn Ferry RPO does better:
- Senior and leadership hiring with assessment integration
- Talent analytics and workforce intelligence at a strategic level
- Leadership succession and competency calibration alongside RPO
- Unified relationship across exec search, leadership development, and RPO
- Premium brand signal for executive candidate attraction

### RWO does better:
- Operational RPO across all grades — entry to senior professional
- BFSI specialist expertise at operational delivery level — compliance, risk, technology, treasury
- APAC regional depth for professional hiring — Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong
- Integrated RPO + MSP/contingent workforce management
- Talent advisory at RPO-market pricing and operational intelligence
- Candidate attraction for specialist BFSI roles via Robert Walters brand in APAC

---

## The Bottom Line

**Choose Korn Ferry RPO if:** Your RPO requirement is primarily senior and leadership hiring, leadership assessment and workforce analytics are central to your buying criteria, and the Korn Ferry relationship already exists in your organisation at exec search level.

**Choose RWO if:** Your RPO requirement spans all professional grades, your primary sector is BFSI, your critical hiring markets include the UK and Asia Pacific, you need integrated permanent and contingent workforce management, and you want a specialist operational partner who understands how banks actually work — not a premium advisory firm extending its reach into RPO.

Both providers are worth evaluating. Neither is universally superior. The right answer depends almost entirely on what your actual hiring challenge is — not on brand recognition or analyst ranking.
