## The Core Battle

Korn Ferry will try to win this deal on **brand prestige, assessment capability, and talent analytics**.

Your job is to make the conversation about **operational delivery across all grades, BFSI sector depth, APAC capability, and integrated RPO + MSP**.

> "Korn Ferry is the world's best executive search firm. The question is whether your RPO challenge is an executive search challenge — or something more."

## Questions to Ask That Expose Korn Ferry RPO Weaknesses

**On operational delivery at scale:**
> "Korn Ferry RPO is excellent for leadership hiring. But our mandate includes [X hundred/thousand] mid-to-professional grade hires annually across compliance, technology, risk, and operations. Can you walk me through how Korn Ferry delivers at that volume and at those grade levels — who is on the team, what's the sourcing model, and how does the per-hire cost compare to specialist RPO providers?"

**On APAC delivery quality:**
> "We have significant hiring in Japan, Singapore, and Malaysia. For our Tokyo operation specifically — does Korn Ferry have Japanese-speaking RPO recruiters on the ground with mid-professional-level candidate networks? Or is APAC RPO delivery managed from a regional hub with exec search support?"

**On MSP/contingent workforce:**
> "Our workforce includes a significant contingent population alongside our permanent professionals. How does Korn Ferry manage contingent workforce alongside the permanent RPO? And if that's out of scope — how do you recommend we manage the interface between the two?"

**On conflict of interest:**
> "If Korn Ferry is managing our RPO — including our leadership hiring — and also conducting executive search for us separately, how does the firm manage the potential conflict between RPO per-hire pricing and exec search placement fees? Has that tension ever affected delivery quality for a client?"

**On RPO investment priority:**
> "Korn Ferry's primary business is executive search and leadership advisory. Where does RPO sit in terms of investment priority — headcount, technology, leadership attention — relative to those core businesses? And how does that translate to the quality of the team assigned to our programme?"

**On pricing transparency:**
> "What does the total cost look like for our programme — management fee, per-hire, and any platform or analytics licensing? And how does Korn Ferry's total cost compare to specialist RPO providers for an all-grades BFSI programme?"

## When to Walk Away

Walk away — or qualify down — only in these circumstances:

- **The requirement falls outside RWO's core sector expertise** — BFSI (banking, financial services, insurance) and Pharma & Life Sciences. If the client's primary sector is technology, industrial, consumer, or another area where RWO has no specialist depth, RWO's differentiation does not apply against Korn Ferry's broader advisory model.
- **A commercially viable engagement cannot be structured** for both RWO and the client — if the deal scope, fee model, or commercial terms mean RWO cannot deliver quality outcomes profitably, or the client cannot achieve the value they need at a sustainable price point, decline cleanly.

**Do not walk away because Korn Ferry has a stronger executive search brand, a more sophisticated assessment framework, or deeper board-level relationships.** These are competitive challenges to navigate, not reasons to concede. RWO wins on operational BFSI delivery, APAC infrastructure, integrated RPO and MSP, and cost-effective specialist quality — dimensions where Korn Ferry RPO consistently underdelivers.

## The Headline Narrative (One Paragraph)

> Korn Ferry RPO deserves respect — it is a distinctive and credible offering for clients whose RPO challenge is primarily about senior hiring quality and leadership intelligence. But for a BFSI enterprise running an outsourced talent function across all grades — from compliance analysts to C-suite — the exec search heritage that makes Korn Ferry compelling at the top creates real constraints at the levels where most of your hiring volume sits. There is no assessment framework that replaces 40 years of operating inside banks in Tokyo, Singapore, and London. And there is no prestige brand that manages your contractor population alongside your permanent team. Robert Walters Outsourcing was built for the full picture. Korn Ferry was built for the top of it.
