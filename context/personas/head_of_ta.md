# Persona: Head of Talent Acquisition (Head of TA / TA Director)

## Role Overview

The Head of TA is RWO's most important day-to-day champion. They own the recruitment function operationally — managing the team, the tools, the hiring manager relationships, and the metrics. They are the most likely person to initiate an RPO evaluation, drive the selection process, and manage the relationship once a contract is in place.

This persona sits below the CHRO but is closer to the problem. They feel the pain of under-resourcing, poor agency performance, and unreliable delivery every day.

---

## What They Care About

- **Delivery** — filling roles on time, to the right quality standard, without drama
- **Capacity** — having enough resource to handle hiring volumes without burning out the team
- **Hiring manager satisfaction** — their internal customers; bad experiences damage their reputation
- **Data and reporting** — being able to show the CHRO and CFO what's working and why
- **Quality of hire** — not just filling seats; hiring people who stick and perform
- **Tools and technology** — ATS, CRM, analytics; they're often more tech-literate than their CHRO
- **Candidate experience** — affects employer brand and offer acceptance rates
- **Diversity sourcing** — often responsible for executing on the CHRO's DEI commitments

---

## Their Biggest Frustrations

- Being asked to do more with fewer resources (especially post-2022 TA team reductions)
- Agency partners who are inconsistent, over-promise, and don't understand the culture
- No real data on what's working — gut feel rather than metrics
- Hiring managers who bypass TA and go direct to agencies
- Slow time-to-fill on specialist or senior roles where the internal team lacks deep networks
- Being reactive rather than strategic — no capacity for workforce planning or proactive sourcing
- Previous RPO implementations that made their job harder, not easier

---

## How They Buy

- Often the **primary evaluator and day-to-day decision-maker** (especially in mid-market)
- In enterprise, they lead the RFP process and present recommendations to the CHRO
- Highly process-oriented — will want detailed SLAs, implementation plans, transition timelines
- Technology capability is a key evaluation criterion — they will demo RW Plus
- References and case studies matter — they want to talk to existing clients
- Sensitive about their team's role changing — need to be reassured their jobs are safe
- Will look at Everest Group, NelsonHall, SIA ratings as part of shortlisting

---

## What RWO Means for This Persona

RWO gives the Head of TA back the capacity to be strategic. The Modular RPO model is specifically designed for this persona — they keep control, choose what to outsource, and their team focuses on the work that builds their career.

**The core message for this persona:**
*"We make your team better, not redundant. You set the strategy; we deliver the execution."*

---

## Trigger Events

- TA team headcount frozen but hiring volumes increasing
- A major hiring programme (transformation, expansion) that overwhelms existing capacity
- High agency spend with no accountability or data
- Dissatisfaction with current RPO provider (switching conversation)
- New TA Director appointed — reviewing all vendor relationships
- Digital transformation programme creating specialist hiring surge (tech roles, change roles)

---

## Objections to Anticipate

- "I lose control of my hiring process" → RW Plus visibility; SLAs; embedded delivery model
- "My team will feel threatened" → Modular RPO framing; RWO augments, doesn't displace
- "We've been burned before" → Diagnose the previous failure; Speedy Hire case study
- "The implementation will be too disruptive" → Project RPO model; 6-week go-live; phased approach

---
