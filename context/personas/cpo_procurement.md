# Persona: Chief Procurement Officer / Head of Procurement (CPO)

## Role Overview

The CPO or Head of Procurement becomes a central stakeholder in two scenarios: (1) when the organisation is evaluating an MSP/contingent workforce management solution, where procurement owns the vendor management agenda; and (2) when the RPO/MSP contract value is large enough to trigger a formal procurement process, bringing them into the RPO evaluation.

In regulated or cost-focused industries (financial services, pharma, large enterprises), procurement often co-owns or drives the vendor selection process for talent outsourcing.

---

## What They Care About

- **Spend visibility and control** — particularly for contingent workforce where spend is often invisible, fragmented, and non-compliant
- **Vendor consolidation** — reducing the number of suppliers for efficiency and leverage
- **Compliance** — IR35 (UK), worker classification (US), Modern Slavery Act, data privacy; contingent workforce is a regulatory minefield
- **Cost efficiency** — not just lowest price, but total cost of ownership and measurable ROI
- **Contract performance** — SLAs, KPIs, reporting cadence, governance frameworks
- **Risk management** — what happens if the provider fails? Contractual protections?
- **Supplier diversity** — increasingly asked to ensure supply chain includes diverse-owned businesses

---

## Their Biggest Frustrations

- Contingent workforce spend that sits outside their visibility — hired directly by hiring managers, no central oversight
- Multiple staffing vendors with no consistency, no data, and no accountability
- HR and TA teams who run their own vendor relationships without procurement involvement
- RPO/MSP providers who promise ROI but can't evidence it clearly
- Long implementation timelines that delay benefits realisation
- Contracts that lock the organisation in without performance accountability

---

## How They Buy

- Process-led — RFP, RFI, shortlisting, scoring frameworks, reference checks
- Focused on contractual terms, SLAs, governance, and exit clauses
- Will use analyst reports (Everest Group) as part of supplier qualification
- Influenced by total cost of ownership modelling, not just headline fee
- May be the primary decision-maker for MSP; co-decision-maker for RPO (alongside CHRO/Head of TA)
- Relationship matters less than evidence — they want data, not lunch

---

## What RWO Means for This Persona

For MSP, RWO gives procurement the visibility, control, and compliance infrastructure they've been asking for. For RPO, RWO gives procurement a credible, analyst-recognised vendor with a track record they can evidence to stakeholders.

**The core message for this persona:**
*"Complete visibility and control over your entire workforce spend — permanent and contingent — with a single trusted partner."*

---

## Trigger Events

- Contingent workforce audit reveals uncontrolled spend and compliance gaps
- IR35 or worker classification regulatory change requiring immediate action
- New CPO appointed — rationalising vendor base and renegotiating contracts
- Merger/acquisition requiring workforce supply chain integration
- Cost reduction mandate requiring contingent spend analysis
- Existing MSP provider contract renewal — opportunity to switch

---

## Objections to Anticipate

- "We want the cheapest provider" → Total cost of ownership conversation; cost of non-compliance; cost of poor hires
- "We prefer to manage vendors directly" → MSP reduces complexity, not control
- "Your scale is smaller than [Randstad/Cielo]" → Everest Group recognition; quality over volume; APAC depth
- "We need global coverage" → 60+ countries, 23 account teams; hybrid model where RWO leads, others complement

---

## Proof Points That Resonate

- Everest Group Star Performer 2025 — CWM/MSP PEAK Matrix (analyst validation for MSP)
- £2bn+ recruitment budget under management — scale and spend management track record
- TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award — performance over time, not just at contract start
- RW Plus — real-time spend dashboards, compliance reporting, vendor performance data
- Workforce consultancy advisory — RWO can advise on workforce strategy, not just execute it
