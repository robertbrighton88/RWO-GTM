# Persona: Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)

## Role Overview

The CHRO is the most senior stakeholder in the talent outsourcing decision. They set the people strategy, own the relationship with the CEO and board on workforce matters, and are accountable for the organisation's ability to attract, retain, and develop talent at scale.

In large enterprises, the CHRO rarely initiates an RPO conversation — but they must champion it, sponsor it, and sign off on it. In mid-market companies, they may be more directly involved in evaluation and selection.

---

## What They Care About

- **Talent as a strategic capability** — they want to be seen as a business partner to the CEO, not an admin function. Everything they buy must reinforce that narrative.
- **Workforce agility** — the ability to scale hiring up or down without structural overhead
- **Quality of hire and retention** — mis-hires are career-damaging; attrition is a board-level metric
- **DEI outcomes** — often have board-level commitments on diversity that must be met
- **Employer brand** — protecting and enhancing how the organisation is perceived in talent markets
- **Cost efficiency** — not the primary driver, but must be justifiable to the CFO
- **Risk and compliance** — particularly in regulated sectors (financial services, pharma, legal)

---

## Their Biggest Frustrations

- Internal TA teams that can't scale fast enough without exploding headcount
- Over-reliance on expensive agencies with no consistency or data
- No visibility into what's being spent on contingent workforce
- RPO providers that deliver volume but not quality — then disappear when it goes wrong
- Being asked by the CEO "why can't we hire fast enough?" without the tools to answer it
- DEI commitments that can't be delivered because the sourcing strategy doesn't change

---

## How They Buy

- **Sponsor**, not always the day-to-day decision-maker
- Will delegate evaluation to Head of TA or HR Operations — but the final decision comes back to them
- Buys on trust and reputation first, then proof points
- Analyst recognition (Everest Group, HRO Today) carries weight in justifying the decision upward
- Wants a long-term strategic relationship, not a contract to manage
- Will typically require CFO sign-off on the commercial terms

---

## What RWO Means for This Persona

RWO elevates the CHRO's strategic position. By outsourcing execution to a trusted partner, the CHRO can redirect their team toward workforce planning, talent development, and organisational design — the work that earns a seat at the table.

**The core message for this persona:**
*"We handle the execution so you can focus on the strategy."*

---

## Trigger Events

- New CEO appointment with talent growth mandate
- Board-level DEI commitment requiring a sourcing transformation
- M&A activity requiring rapid headcount scaling
- Digital transformation programme creating a hiring surge
- Post-restructuring TA team too lean to support growth
- Entering APAC or EMEA markets without local hiring expertise

---

## Objections to Anticipate

- "We already have a TA team" → RWO augments, not replaces
- "We've tried RPO before" → ask what failed; position specialist expertise as the differentiator
- "It feels like giving up control" → RW Plus dashboard; embedded delivery model

---

## Proof Points That Resonate

- Workforce consultancy +51% NFI — signal that CHROs are paying for strategic advice
- +36% more hires (European bank) — quality intelligence driving better outcomes
- TIARA Long-Term Partnership Award — validates the "strategic partner" claim
- 40-year heritage — trust built over time

---

## How to Reach This Persona

- Industry events: HR Tech, UNLEASH, CHRO roundtables
- Thought leadership: salary surveys, workforce trends reports, DEI research
- Warm referral from existing Robert Walters recruitment relationship
- HRO Today media (CEO recognition creates inbound)
- LinkedIn — CHROs are active on LinkedIn around people strategy topics
