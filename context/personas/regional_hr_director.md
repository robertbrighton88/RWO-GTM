# Persona: Regional HR Director (APAC / EMEA)

## Role Overview

The Regional HR Director is the most locally relevant stakeholder for RWO — particularly in Asia Pacific, where RWO has its deepest expertise and longest track record. They are responsible for delivering the people agenda across a defined geography, often spanning multiple countries with different labour laws, talent markets, and cultural norms.

This persona is frequently underserved by global RPO providers whose APAC or EMEA delivery is thinly staffed and lacks genuine local knowledge. RWO's geographic depth is a direct answer to their most acute pain.

---

## What They Care About

- **Local market expertise** — someone who actually understands hiring in Japan, or Singapore, or Malaysia; not a global provider with a flag on a map
- **Cross-border hiring complexity** — different employment laws, work visa requirements, salary norms, and candidate behaviours across markets
- **Speed** — hiring managers in their region need talent now; slow delivery damages their credibility
- **Candidate quality** — in specialist markets (finance in Singapore, tech in Japan), quality matters more than volume
- **Cultural fit** — understanding local workplace culture in candidate assessment
- **Language capability** — Japanese, Mandarin, Bahasa, Korean — providers that can recruit in-language
- **Compliance** — employment law varies dramatically across APAC; getting it wrong is a legal and reputational risk
- **Reporting back to global HQ** — needs to demonstrate performance against global metrics while navigating local complexity

---

## Their Biggest Frustrations

- Global RPO providers that manage APAC from a desk in London or New York
- Candidates who drop out because the recruiter didn't understand the local market
- Slow time-to-fill because the provider is sourcing from a generic global database
- Having to educate a new provider on every market every time
- Being squeezed between global HQ's standardised processes and local market realities
- No visibility into what other hiring is happening across the region — duplication, inconsistency

---

## How They Buy

- Often **initiates** the outsourcing conversation regionally, then escalates to CHRO/global HR
- May have autonomy to appoint a provider up to a certain contract value
- Values relationships and referrals highly — will ask peers in the same markets who they use
- Wants to see genuine on-the-ground presence, not a regional office with 2 people
- Pilot project or proof of concept in one market is a common entry point
- Cultural awareness in the sales process matters — a Western-centric pitch will not land

## Trigger Events

- Regional headcount growth mandate requiring cross-country hiring at scale
- Expansion into a new APAC market (Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia) without local hiring capability
- Previous global RPO provider failing to deliver in specific APAC markets
- New Regional HR Director appointed — auditing vendor relationships
- M&A requiring rapid workforce integration across multiple APAC jurisdictions
- Digital transformation programme requiring regional specialist hiring

---

## Objections to Anticipate

- "You're too expensive for this region" → ROI conversation; cost of slow/bad hires in competitive markets
- "We use a global provider who covers APAC" → Ask about their on-the-ground team size in specific markets; local knowledge test
- "We prefer local providers in each market" → Consistency of quality and data across markets; single reporting framework
