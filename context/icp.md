# Robert Walters Outsourcing — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## What the ICP Is NOT

Before defining who RWO is for, it's worth being explicit about who they're not targeting:

- **Not** organisations primarily seeking the lowest cost-per-hire
- **Not** companies with predominantly entry-level, high-volume commodity hiring
- **Not** businesses without a meaningful HR or TA function to partner with
- **Not** companies with under ~200–300 annual professional hires (Full RPO economics unlikely to stack up)
- **Not** organisations looking for a short-term transactional supplier

## ICP — Situational Triggers

These are the moments when a company becomes an active ICP:

**1. Digital or business transformation programme**
The single most common engagement trigger. A large-scale transformation creates a defined, time-bound hiring surge that overwhelms internal TA capacity.
*→ Project RPO or Full RPO conversation*

**2. Entering a new market or geography**
Expanding into APAC or EMEA without local hiring expertise or networks.
*→ RWO's geographic depth and local market knowledge*

**3. Internal TA team downsized, hiring volumes recovering**
Post-2022 TA headcount reductions left many organisations under-resourced as markets pick up.
*→ Modular RPO or Full RPO to bridge the gap*

**4. Contingent workforce spend out of control**
No visibility on what's being spent on contractors, agencies, and SOW engagements.
*→ MSP / CWM conversation*

**5. Previous RPO provider underperforming**
Clients who tried RPO and had a bad experience are in-market for a better provider — warm conversations.
*→ Differentiate on quality, expertise, and brand (Speedy Hire case study)*

**6. Talent scarcity in specialist roles**
Hiring in financial services, technology, or legal in APAC or EMEA where candidate shortages are acute.
*→ RW Insights, specialist network, brand attraction advantage*

**7. DEI mandate under pressure**
Board or regulatory pressure to improve diversity outcomes in hiring.
*→ ED&I advisory; RPO with built-in diversity sourcing*

**8. CHRO or CPO newly appointed**
New leaders often audit vendor relationships and reset the talent strategy.
*→ Advisory-led entry conversation; position RWO as a strategic partner from day one*

## ICP — Psychographic Profile

Beyond firmographics, the right ICP client thinks this way:

- Views talent as a **strategic capability**, not a cost centre
- Wants a **partner relationship**, not a transactional vendor
- Is comfortable with **data and analytics** as part of the hiring conversation
- Has **C-suite sponsorship** for the outsourcing decision (CHRO/CPO involved, not just TA manager)
- Thinks in **multi-year timeframes** — not looking to solve this quarter's hiring problem
- Values **quality of hire** over speed or volume (though both matter)
- Is open to **changing how they've always done it** if the evidence is there

## ICP — Sector Deep-Dive

### Banking, Financial Services & Insurance (BFSI) ✅ Primary — Core ICP
This is where RWO wins. The depth of expertise, case study base, and relationship network is concentrated here.
- Banks, asset managers, insurers, FinTech, wealth management
- Complex regulatory environment (FCA, MAS, HKMA, SEC) → compliance-heavy hiring RWO understands deeply
- Salary competition in financial centres → RW Insights for real-time market intelligence
- RWO knows how banks operate internally — risk, governance, compliance, procurement — and can navigate the complexity of embedding solutions within them
- APAC financial hubs (Singapore, HK, Tokyo) and UK (London) are the two strongest markets
- Strong proof point base: European bank +36% hires; financial advisor talent mapping case study

### Technology & Digital 🟡 Secondary
- Transformation programmes = Project RPO trigger (KPMG, Speedy Hire)
- Often adjacent to BFSI (FinTech, bank technology transformation) — plays well alongside primary sector
- Skills scarcity → specialist network matters

### Legal & Compliance 🟡 Secondary
- Niche, specialist roles — quality positioning wins
- Often sits within financial services clients (in-house legal at banks)

### Pharma & Life Sciences 🟡 Secondary
- Regulated, specialist, global — structural alignment with RWO strengths
- Less proven case study base vs. BFSI

### Public Sector 🔴 Out of ICP
- RWO positioning is private sector specialist; public sector procurement dynamics are misaligned
- ANZ public sector dependency caused -23% result in 2024 — a cautionary signal

## ICP Summary Statement

*Robert Walters Outsourcing is built for banking, financial services, and insurance organisations — primarily in the UK and Asia Pacific, with growing presence in EMEA and the US. The ideal client is an enterprise or upper mid-market BFSI organisation with 200+ professional hires per year, operating in a complex regulatory environment, and looking for an RPO or talent partner who already understands how to work within the constraints of their world. The ideal engagement is sponsored at CHRO or CPO level and triggered by a transformation programme, market expansion, TA capacity gap, or dissatisfaction with a generalist provider who couldn't navigate the internal complexity.*
