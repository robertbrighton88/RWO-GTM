# Robert Walters Outsourcing — Messaging

## Core Value Proposition

> *"Agile recruitment outsourcing solutions tailored to your unique requirements, seamlessly scalable to match your evolving needs and objectives."*

Three things this phrase does deliberately:
- **Agile + scalable** — addresses the fear of being locked into a rigid contract
- **Tailored** — counters the "off the shelf" objection
- **Evolving needs** — signals a long-term partner relationship, not a transaction

---

## Brand Voice

| Dimension | Character |
|---|---|
| Authority | High — speaks with confidence and expertise |
| Warmth | Present — human-first, empathetic to client pressure |
| Formality | Semi-formal — professional but accessible |
| Urgency | Low — no fear-mongering or pressure tactics |
| Data orientation | Strong — metrics, benchmarks, and insights anchor credibility |
| Aspiration | High — "shaping the future," "fulfil unique potential" |

**In plain English:** RWO sounds like a trusted senior advisor who has seen everything, understands your situation, and isn't going to oversell. Confident without being arrogant. Data-informed but not cold.

## Messaging by Service Line

### RPO
- Lead with **outcomes**: time-to-hire, quality of hire, cost-per-hire improvement
- Frame as **freeing your team to focus on strategy**, not just outsourcing a function
- Emphasise **flexibility**: Full, Project, and Modular — client chooses the model
- Key proof point: *+36% more hires per quarter* for a European bank using RW Insights intelligence

### MSP / Contingent Workforce
- Lead with **visibility and control** — most clients don't know what they're actually spending on contingent labour
- Frame as a **compliance and risk management** story as much as a cost story
- Emphasise **spend under management** and the analyst credibility: *Everest Group Star Performer 2025*

### Talent Advisory
- Lead with **strategic relevance** — position this at CHRO/CPO level, not TA manager level
- Frame as *"intelligence that shapes decisions, not just data that describes the past"*
- Use the workforce consultancy growth (+51% NFI) as a signal that the market is already here
- ED&I: position as both a commercial differentiator and a compliance enabler

## Key Messaging Pillars

### 1. Trusted Partner, Not a Vendor
Language: "strategic partner," "extension of your team," "aligned with your culture"
Avoid: "provider," "supplier," "service delivery"

### 2. Human + Technology
Language: "tech-powered delivery and human-led insight," "ethical AI," "data-driven, people-first"
Avoid: AI hyperbole — RWO messaging is deliberately measured on this

### 3. Global Reach, Local Depth
Language: "nearly 40 years in markets like Japan and Singapore," "talent into 60+ countries," "on-the-ground expertise"
Avoid: Generic "global footprint" language that every competitor uses

### 4. Full Talent Agenda
Language: "from sourcing to strategy," "one partner across permanent, contingent, and advisory," "the full breadth of the talent agenda"
Avoid: Positioning as RPO-only — the advisory layer is a key differentiator

### 5. Quality, Not Volume
Language: "specialist mid-to-senior hiring," "hard-to-fill roles," "quality of hire"
Avoid: Volume, speed, throughput language that positions RWO as a commodity provider

> *"We take the time to listen to and fully connect with the organisations we partner with. That's what delivers better outcomes."*

**On differentiation:**
> *"We're not just keeping pace with the future of work — we're shaping it."*

**On technology:**
> *"AI is not going to replace recruiters. But recruiters without data fluency will be replaced."*

---

## What RWO Does NOT Do in Messaging

- Does not compete on **price** — no cost-efficiency, "save money" lead messaging
- Does not use **fear or urgency** tactics — no "talent crisis" alarm language
- Does not over-promise on **AI** — measured, responsible narrative
- Does not use **jargon-heavy** corporate speak — direct, plain, confident
- Does not position as **generalist** — always specialist, always mid-to-senior, always quality
