## Sector Patterns — Pharma HR
- Patent cliff events → dual-mandate TA (exits + growth simultaneously) → strong RPO entry
- New CHRO appointments = 6–12 month window to shape vendor relationships
- APAC manufacturing scale-up often counter-cyclical to US/EU restructuring → geographic RPO white space

## Sector Patterns — Corporate Spinoffs / Carve-outs
- Spinoff CPO appointments (building from scratch) = highest-urgency project RPO entry; outreach window is first 90 days before vendor relationships form
- Spinoff entities need: TA process design, ATS/HRIS selection, employer brand, workforce planning — full advisory + project RPO scope
- Do NOT route spinoff CPO as a referral from the parent entity — treat as an independent commercial opportunity
- Pattern confirmed: Fluence Energy (Larissa Cerqueira) and Mobility Global — both IPO/separation HR builds

## Sector Patterns — BFSI / Asset Management HR
- New CPO/CHRO appointment = 6–12 month window; highest-priority trigger for outreach
- Post-restructuring TA rebuild (post-cuts) → modular RPO or specialist search entry
- Single TA leader covering 10+ countries = structural under-resourcing signal; APAC market entry via local delivery
- Large divestiture events create TA model reset — team shrinks but load may not
- Family-owned or privately held firms (BI, B. Braun, Fidelity) often have internal insourcing philosophy — lead with Advisory, not outsourcing

## Sector Patterns — Industrial / Medtech HR
- New facility construction = discrete project RPO window (engineering/manufacturing hiring surge, defined timeline)
- New TA CoC establishment = Talent Advisory entry for operating model design benchmarking
- Capital Markets Day / new strategy announcements = 4–6 month pre-announcement Talent Advisory window

## Sector Patterns — Large US Banks (BFSI)
- AI-led attrition ("don't backfill") running simultaneously with specialist growth hiring (AML, compliance, front office) = dual-mandate TA pressure — structural case for modular RPO as flex capacity
- New discrete operations hub builds (e.g. Belfast AML 1,000 roles) = project RPO entry point; approach before sourcing channels are locked in (first 90 days of announced programme)
- CEO-level market expansion signals (e.g. Japan) = APAC HR Director outreach trigger; internal HR team too small to manage surge alone
- Large US banks have deeply entrenched internal TA pride — do NOT lead with outsourcing language; lead with market intelligence and specialist sourcing partnership
- VMS/MSP incumbents at US banks are often US-only — EMEA/APAC contingent workforce may be completely unmanaged (separate opportunity)

## Sector Patterns — Large US Banks (BFSI) — Additional Pattern (JPMorgan)
- Deeply insourced US banks may create a new internal Talent Transformation function (Allison Horn at JPMorgan, Aug 2025) when they want to build AI-augmented TA capability internally — NOT outsource. The new function head is always the highest-priority Advisory contact: they are building from scratch, have no existing vendor relationships in the new role, and need external benchmarks.
- New Talent Transformation function head appointment = Talent Advisory entry window (first 6 months). Lead with: "what does best-in-class look like at peer firms?" Do NOT lead with RPO.
- New London campus builds (e.g. JPMorgan Riverside South, 12,000 staff over 6 years) = sustained project RPO entry for early careers and lateral hiring. Frame as programme partnership, not staffing PSL.

## Sector Patterns — Large European CIB Banks (BFSI)
- M&A integration events (two AM/CIB entities merging) = highest-urgency Talent Advisory entry: TA harmonisation, employer brand rationalisation, redundancy support, and backfill hiring all active simultaneously
- Support function efficiency programmes (cost/income ratio targets) create internal pressure to reduce HR headcount — directly conflicts with integration TA volume, making external capacity argument compelling
- CHRO from Wealth Management / commercial background (e.g. Sofia Merlo) = lead with commercial language (ROI, efficiency, quality of hire) not HR process language
- European CIB banks (BNP, SocGen, Deutsche) typically have insourced TA model — entry point is modular/project RPO or early careers programme, NOT full outsource
- Japan CIB HR leadership gap is a recurring pattern — Tokyo CIB entities are often understaffed in HR/TA relative to their hiring volume

## Sector Patterns — Global Pharma / Focused Biopharma
- Post-demerger (e.g. GSK / Haleon split 2022) = TA function right-sized down; subsequent pipeline-driven growth creates renewed volume pressure on smaller team = strong modular RPO entry
- CEO succession from commercial (CCO to CEO) background = new pressure on CPO to demonstrate talent ROI and pipeline delivery readiness — Talent Advisory entry point via business case framing
- Global RPO incumbents (e.g. Korn Ferry at GSK) typically cover UK/US/LatAm; APAC is structurally thin — one embedded RPO recruiter is not sufficient for a region with Singapore exec hiring, Japan growth market, and Malaysia hub
- "Dual mandate" TA pressure (R&D exits + commercial launch hiring simultaneously) is the specific pharma TA pain point that most clearly justifies flex RPO capacity

## Sector Patterns — Japanese Megabanks (BFSI)
- Group CHRO at Japanese megabanks is typically a career insider with a low Western conference profile — do NOT approach top-down first; find externally-oriented CHRO/CPO at the regional entity level (e.g. APAC CHRO, EMEA CHRO) as the primary entry
- Megabanks with international ambition will hire externally-oriented CHROs for APAC/EMEA regions who come from Western FS firms (e.g. Mookharjea at Mizuho from AIG/StanChart); these are the commercially accessible senior contacts
- AI-driven clerical workforce reduction programmes in Japan megabanks (common pattern 2026+) create redeployment advisory needs — frame as capability transition, not restructuring
- Japanese megabank TA model is deeply insourced; entry is via project/modular RPO for specific growth hiring (US CIB expansion, APAC front office) not full outsource
- HR/TA leadership gap in Japan entity (Tokyo) is consistent across all Japanese banks in this research — Navigator searches for Japan-based HR Directors rarely return public results; rely on Robert Walters Japan BFSI practice for warm introductions
- EMEA CHRO appointments at Japanese banks (e.g. Mizuho, Nomura, SMBC) tend to hire IB specialists from peer European/US banks — these contacts are comfortable with external partnerships and respond to operating model / advisory framing

## Sector Patterns — German Corporates (Reinsurance / Industrial)
- German companies with Works Councils (Betriebsrat) = full RPO for German domestic operations is not commercially viable without co-determination agreement. Do NOT pitch full outsource for Germany. Frame all Germany engagement as Talent Advisory, benchmarking, or discrete project support.
- International operations (UK, Singapore, Japan, APAC) of German HQ firms face no Works Council constraint — these are the commercially accessible entry points.
- German corporate HR sponsor at board level (Arbeitsdirektor / Labour Relations Director) is often a commercial or operational executive rather than a career HR professional — leads with ROI and efficiency language, not HR process language.
- Confirmed at: Munich Re (Stefan Golling — underwriter/mathematician with HR as portfolio add-on); Commerzbank (Sabine Mlnarsky — labour lawyer background).
- New C-suite configuration at a German firm (new Chair, CFO, CTO simultaneously) = operating model review window; approach within 6 months of appointment.
