## 2. Strategic Context

Royal London is in a sustained growth and transformation phase, not a cost-cutting mode. CEO Barry O'Dwyer has publicly framed digital transformation as existential: *"Failing to transform would be an existential risk."* [Verified: PwC CEO Survey interview, 2024]

**Growth agenda creating talent demand:**
1. **Digital / technology transformation** — Royal London is mid-way through a multi-year technology programme. They are moving to cloud-based architecture (Oracle Cloud ERP already live), building a digital-first customer experience, and investing heavily in data and AI capability. The CEO noted that hiring competition for data specialists is intense and that they differentiate on demonstrating a genuine long-term data commitment. This is a live hiring pressure point. [Verified: PwC interview; Oracle case study]
2. **Bulk Purchase Annuity expansion** — The BPA business launched in late 2024 and is scaling aggressively (18 transactions in FY2025). This is a new capability requiring specialist actuarial, risk, investment, and operational hiring. [Verified: 2025 Results]
3. **Private markets build-out** — Acquisition of Dalmore Capital and organic private markets hiring in 2024. New products launching across 2025 requiring investment professionals. [Verified: 2025 Results; Funds Europe]
4. **Workplace Pensions growth** — 19% growth in new business sales; £100m platform investment signals significant technology and people build-out ahead. [Verified]
5. **Ireland growth** — Irish operations (Royal London Insurance DAC) posted 76% new business growth in H1 2025. This is an emerging hiring market for Royal London. [Verified: H1 2025 Results]
6. **RLAM international expansion** — Appointment of a Chief Client Officer for international growth; RLAM is building out its distribution and client servicing capability globally. [Verified: Funds Europe]

**Operating model context:**
Royal London has a mature internal People function with a Chief People Officer on the Group Executive Committee. The organisation appears to rely on internal TA supported by its direct employer brand (winner of Best Place to Work, In-house Recruitment Awards 2025). [Inferred: IHR Award signal, LinkedIn posts]. No evidence of an incumbent RPO provider has been identified — this is white space.

**Workforce pressure points:**
- Data, AI, and technology talent competition is acute and explicitly called out by the CEO
- BPA build requires specialist, niche talent that internal TA may struggle to source at pace
- RLAM is hiring across multiple new capability areas (private markets, international distribution) simultaneously
- Internal TA team has won employer brand recognition — suggesting a mature function that may be protective of its remit, but likely open to specialist augmentation for niche or growth-surge hiring

Internal TA teams are not always well-equipped to source from these pools, particularly at speed.

2. **Technology transformation hiring** — Royal London's multi-year digital programme creates a sustained demand for change management, cloud architecture, data engineering, and programme delivery professionals. This is precisely the demand profile that leads to TA capacity overruns and agency dependency without a structured partnership.

3. **Employer brand protection under volume pressure** — Royal London has invested heavily in its employer brand and culture (IHR Award win). As hiring volumes grow, maintaining quality of candidate experience becomes harder. An RPO partner with brand sensitivity is important here.

4. **Ireland growth** — With Irish operations growing 76% on new business, Royal London may not have a deep local TA capability in Dublin. This is a potential modular RPO or project RPO opportunity.

5. **Data / AI talent in a competitive market** — The CEO has called this out explicitly. RWO's RW Insights capability (market salary data, skills benchmarking) is directly relevant to addressing the structural salary disadvantage in attracting data professionals to a mutual insurer versus fintech or Big Tech.
