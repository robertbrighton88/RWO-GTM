The company is undergoing its most significant transformation in a generation, driven by the looming patent cliff for Keytruda. Biosimilar competition for Keytruda is expected to emerge in the US as early as 2028. To prepare:
- Merck announced in Q2 2025 a plan to **cut approximately 6,000 jobs globally** (~8% of headcount), targeting **$3 billion in annual cost savings by end of 2027**
- Savings will be "fully reinvested" into new product launches — up to 20 pipeline products with blockbuster potential
- The company is **reducing its global real estate footprint** and **optimising its manufacturing network**
- In parallel, Merck is **investing in new growth**: broke ground on a $1 billion biologics centre of excellence in Delaware (creating ~4,500 US jobs); acquired Verona Pharma (~$10 billion) in July 2025 for COPD pipeline
- The **Human Health business has been split** into Oncology and Specialty/Pharma/ID units — a structural reorganisation creating new TA complexity

## 2. Strategic Context

### Primary Transformation Themes

**1. Keytruda Patent Cliff — Existential Reallocation**
Keytruda accounts for over 50% of pharmaceutical revenues. With US biosimilar entry expected from 2028, Merck is under board-level pressure to diversify its pipeline while simultaneously cutting operational costs. This creates a dual mandate for HR: **execute a complex, multi-year downsizing** while **simultaneously building capability in new therapeutic areas**. [Verified — FiercePharma; BioSpace; PharmVoice]

**2. Structural Reorganisation — Oncology Spin and Dual Business Units**
The split of Human Health into two standalone units (Oncology; Specialty/Pharma/ID) represents a fundamental organisational redesign. Each unit will require dedicated TA pipelines, workforce planning, and HRBP support. New leadership (Foard, Oosthuizen) will bring their own talent priorities. [Verified — FiercePharma; BioPharma Dive]

**3. 6,000 Job Cuts — Phased Through 2027–2028**
The reductions will be phased over several years, primarily affecting administrative, sales, and R&D roles globally. This creates a complex TA challenge: managing exits while continuing to hire for growth roles (biologics manufacturing, oncology pipeline, new acquisitions). A lean internal TA team managing both streams is a clear RPO/advisory opportunity. [Verified — PharmExec; FiercePharma; NJBIZ]

**4. Acquisitions Driving Integration Complexity**
The July 2025 acquisition of Verona Pharma (~$10 billion) will require HR integration work across talent mapping, onboarding, and cultural alignment. M&A-driven TA complexity is a recurring theme at Merck — the company has a dedicated HR M&A function (Kim Zavasky, HR Director M&A). [Verified — PharmExec; LinkedIn/Zavasky profile]

**5. Manufacturing Scale-Up**
Simultaneous with cost cuts, Merck is expanding its biologics manufacturing footprint (Delaware, Singapore). Singapore remains a critical hub with active hiring. This creates demand for volume TA in specialist manufacturing and sciences roles — an RPO sweet spot. [Inferred from MSD Singapore operations data and Delaware investment announcement]
