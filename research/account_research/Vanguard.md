## 2. Strategic Context

**New CEO Transformation Agenda:**
Salim Ramji has moved quickly to reshape Vanguard's senior leadership and strategic positioning. Having spent 18 years at BlackRock (where he built the iShares ETF business into a market leader), Ramji brings a growth and commercialisation mindset that is new to Vanguard's historically insular culture. His priorities appear to be:
1. Rebuilding Vanguard's relevance in wealth management (hence the new division and Rotenberg hire)
2. Accelerating AI adoption and technology investment
3. Internationalising Vanguard's offer more aggressively (the International division is underweight vs. BlackRock/Fidelity)
4. Building a more externally competitive leadership team (10+ senior Wall Street hires)

**Workforce Operating Model Change — Insourcing from Infosys:**
In 2020, Vanguard transferred 1,300 roles to Infosys as part of a recordkeeping/operations outsourcing deal. By early 2026, the relationship is being wound down: Infosys is transitioning services back to Vanguard, with 248 affected employees offered direct Vanguard roles (project managers, data analysts, compliance consultants, recordkeeping administrators). This signals a broader shift: Vanguard is rebuilding internal operational capacity. [Verified — Philadelphia Inquirer, Feb 2026]

**AI Strategy:**
Jon Couture (CHRO) has publicly stated that Vanguard's approach to AI is human-centred: "technology is most effective when paired with strong leadership, clear values, and human judgment." Couture is actively building "cultural carriers" throughout the 20,000-person workforce to sustain high performance through transformation. [Verified — corporate.vanguard.com]

**New Wealth Division Hiring Pressure:**
The launch of a new Advice & Wealth Management division (Jan 2025) creates an internal hiring wave for client-facing advisory, wealth planning, and technology roles — a talent profile that is competitive and specialist, exactly the profile where RPO flex capacity adds value. [Inferred]

**International Growth Potential:**
Vanguard's international footprint (~500 UK/Europe staff; smaller presence in Singapore/Tokyo/Melbourne) is structurally thin vs. the scale of AUM it manages internationally. Ramji's growth agenda is likely to include international expansion, particularly in Europe and Asia. [Inferred from Ramji's BlackRock background and International division structure]

**Angle 1 — "Competing for talent in markets where Vanguard's brand is not yet dominant"**

**Angle 2 — "Scaling a new division without scaling headcount proportionally"**
The Advice & Wealth Management division is being built from scratch. Financial planning and advisory talent is finite and contested. Tiffany Haley's team will need flex capacity to source, assess, and pipeline specialist candidates at volume — without necessarily adding permanent TA headcount. A modular RPO relationship for specialist functions (wealth advisory, client experience, digital) would allow Vanguard to meet division hiring targets without permanently expanding its internal TA team.

**Angle 3 — "The AI + talent paradox for asset managers"**
Jon Couture has publicly stated that "technology is most effective when paired with strong leadership, clear values, and human judgment." This is the right frame for a Talent Advisory opening: as Vanguard invests in AI to enhance its investment and client experience, what does its talent pipeline need to look like to sustain that? RWO can position around helping Vanguard map the skills gap between its current workforce and the AI-augmented operating model Ramji is building — starting with a market intelligence briefing, not a sales pitch.
