Prudential plc is a British-domiciled multinational life insurance and asset management group operating exclusively in Asia and Africa. ## 2. Strategic Context

### Growth Agenda: Asia-Africa Pure Play

Prudential plc is executing a focussed Asia-Africa growth strategy with ambitious targets: 15–20% CAGR in new business profit from 2022 to 2027; gross operating free surplus of at least $4.4 billion by 2027. CEO Anil Wadhwani (appointed February 2023, previously CEO Manulife Asia) has described agency transformation as "our number one priority." [Verified: prudentialplc.com strategy documents, FY2024 results presentation]

### Key Strategic Initiatives Creating TA Demand

1. **Agency force transformation:** Recruitment quality and productivity improvement of the 65,000-strong agent network is the explicit top priority. Programmes such as PRUVentures (Malaysia) recruit higher-calibre agents with better retention outcomes; the plan is to roll this out across markets. This creates a significant, sustained volume hiring challenge — not traditional corporate TA, but a specialist recruitment challenge.

2. **Technology and AI transformation:** Prudential is digitising agency (PRUForce platform), customer experience (PRUServices), and underwriting (straight-through processing; 70% issue time reduction target). The Bengaluru GCC launch (March 2025, 400 tech/AI/data/ops roles over 3 years) is the structural investment vehicle. Digital transformation reduced claims processing times by 30% in 2024. [Verified: prudentialplc.com Bengaluru press release; FY2024 presentation]

3. **Geographic expansion and concentration:** Growth markets include India (CEO commitment to Finance Minister Sitharaman, March 2025), Greater China, ASEAN. The regional CEO structure was redesigned in late 2024/early 2025:
   - Kenneth Rappold appointed Chief Strategy and Transformation Officer (November 2024)
   - Angel Ng: Regional CEO Greater China + Group Customer, Wealth and Product (October 2025)
   - Naveen Tahilyani: Regional CEO Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, India, Africa + Group Agency and Health (October 2025)
   - Dennis Tan: Regional CEO Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar + Group Partnership Distribution (January 2025)
   This reorganisation generates HR/TA complexity as functional responsibilities shift to regional CEOs.

4. **Health business transformation:** Building a health ecosystem — from traditional insurance to health services provider — across key ASEAN markets. This is a distinct capability-building exercise requiring specialist health/digital hiring.

5. **GCC build (India):** 400 technology, AI, data, and operations roles in Bengaluru within three years. Officiated by CTOO Anette Bronder (May 2024). While outside RWO's primary delivery markets, this signals the scale of tech hiring demand the group is managing simultaneously.

### Restructuring / Headcount Context

Prudential plc is in growth mode, not restructuring mode. [Verified: research eliminates confusion with Prudential Financial WARN notices]

### Operating Model Shift Creating HR Pressure

CEO Wadhwani stated explicitly: "Historically the business was run more like a holding company than an operating company." The pivot to an operating company model, with dual-reporting lines and stronger centre-to-market accountability, creates ongoing organisational design work and HRBP demand across all markets. Simultaneously, the Group HR function must build capability to support a leaner, more operationally integrated structure across 24 markets. [Verified: FY2024 CEO presentation script]

## 8. RWO Positioning Summary

### Primary Talent Challenges

1. **Multi-market TA capacity under-resourcing:** A small Group TA function (Andrew Chang leading the "Eastern Hemisphere") managing talent acquisition across 24 markets simultaneously — technology, insurance specialist, leadership, and agent-adjacent corporate hiring all live at once. 2. **Concurrent transformation programmes creating simultaneous hiring surges:** Bengaluru GCC build, PRUForce/PRUServices tech transformation, health business expansion, and regional CEO structure refresh all create hiring demand that a lean Group TA team cannot absorb without external flex capacity.

3. **Operating model transition HR complexity:** Shifting from holding company to operating company creates ongoing organisational design, HRBP capability requirements, and leadership hiring across all markets — exactly the Talent Advisory and modular RPO use case.

4. **Market-specific TA maturity gaps:** Prudential operates in markets with very different TA market conditions — Singapore (competitive, transparent), Hong Kong (competitive, dual-market mainland/international), Malaysia (strong RWO presence), Southeast Asian growth markets (Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand — less mature TA infrastructure). A single internal model cannot serve all of these effectively.

5. **New TA leadership in role — vendor relationship window open:** Viola Tang's "Excellence and Effectiveness" mandate (building the TA framework) and Andrew Chang in the Group TA lead role (formalising the function) represent an optimal window to shape how external partnerships are structured.
