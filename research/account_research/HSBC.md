## 2. Strategic Context

### Organisational simplification programme (2024–2026)
[Verified — HSBC Holdings Annual Results 2025; Bloomberg March 2026]

CEO Georges Elhedery (appointed September 2024) initiated the most significant restructuring in HSBC's modern history upon appointment. Key elements:

- **Operating committee halved:** The Group Executive Committee of 18 was replaced by a Group Operating Committee of 12. Elhedery eliminated co-management roles explicitly, stating publicly that "senior executives hid behind co-head roles" (Bloomberg, December 2025).
- **Cost savings target:** $1.5 billion annualised cost reduction by end-2026 — delivery expected six months ahead of plan (H1 2026). $1.0 billion restructuring charge taken in FY2025.
- **Geographic refocus:** Exiting low-margin western markets (US investment banking, European M&A and ECM) while doubling down on Asia and Middle East. Target: >50% of pre-tax profit from Asia and Middle East.
- **Hang Seng Bank privatisation:** Completed January 2026, removing the minority float and simplifying governance.
- **Financial targets 2026–2028:** RoTE of 17%+ in each year; 5% revenue growth by 2028; operating expenses +1% annually; CET1 ratio maintained 14%–14.5%.

### AI-driven workforce reduction programme (announced March 2026)
[Verified — Bloomberg, 19 March 2026; multiple press corroboration]

This is the most current and commercially significant talent development for RWO:

- HSBC is mulling the reduction of up to **20,000 roles** (approximately 10% of global headcount) over a 3–5 year multi-year programme driven by AI automation.
- Primary targets: **non-client-facing roles in global service centres** — operations, compliance, finance, IT.
- CEO Elhedery confirmed in 2025 annual results that HSBC "accelerated GenAI adoption across HSBC, moving from experimentation to scaled delivery" and will "expand enterprise-wide AI adoption through 2026."
- Programme is at early stages; no final decisions made. Natural attrition and targeted reductions are both being considered.
- Context: This creates a dual TA challenge — managing exits and outplacement while simultaneously hiring for specialist growth roles (CIB coverage, wealth management, compliance leadership).

### TA function transformation (2024–2025)
[Verified — Eightfold case study; myHRfuture podcast]

- As of April 2025, HSBC unified Talent Acquisition and Talent Management under a single global leader (Noel Brown, Global Head of Enterprise Talent). This consolidation is explicitly designed to eliminate "duplication of processes and teams" and create "a sharper set of solutions."
- HSBC uses Eightfold (skills inferencing) and SAP SuccessFactors; invested in TechWolf (skills intelligence) with Rob Etheridge joining TechWolf's board as observer.
- Regional TA Academy launched in Europe (led by Claire Hunt).
- Emerging talent function covers approximately 3,000 annual early career hires and 5,000 experienced hire professionals per year in UK alone.

### M&A and strategic exits
[Verified — HSBC Annual Results 2025]
- Sale of French retained loan portfolio finalised.
- Winding down M&A and ECM operations in UK, Europe, and USA.
- Genpact partnership expanded 2024 for AI-powered finance operations outsourcing (Southeast Asia and Europe) — signals appetite for strategic outsourcing partnerships beyond core banking.

## 8. RWO Positioning Summary

### Primary Talent Challenges

1. **Dual-mandate TA under AI restructuring:** HSBC is simultaneously managing up to 20,000 potential role reductions (non-client-facing, global service centres) and growing specialist headcount (CIB Asia, Wealth APAC, compliance). The internal TA team will be stretched — or may itself be reduced — while facing the highest-complexity hiring environment in years. This is the defining talent challenge.

2. **APAC growth with thin internal TA infrastructure:** HSBC's strategic pivot to Asia (>50% of profit target) means Singapore, Hong Kong, Tokyo, and Kuala Lumpur must hire at pace for CIB, Wealth, and Commercial Banking. Internal APAC TA resources are likely not scaled to this ambition, especially as the UK-centric TA team manages restructuring.

3. **Global service centre workforce transition:** Malaysia, and other GSC markets, face AI-driven role displacement. The HR teams in these markets need expertise in managing large-volume role transitions, outplacement, and replacement hiring for higher-skill roles — a capability gap RWO can fill.

4. **Operating model integration risk:** The April 2025 TA/TM consolidation under Noel Brown is recent and may not have stabilised. New operating models create process gaps and capacity shortfalls that third-party specialists can address through project RPO or Advisory.

5. **Specialist BFSI talent scarcity:** CIB front-office, wealth management, compliance/AML, and quantitative roles are globally scarce. HSBC is competing with Goldman, JPM, UBS, and local Asian banks for the same profiles. RWO's specialist BFSI search capability is directly relevant.
