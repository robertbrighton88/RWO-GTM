## 2. Strategic Context

### 2a. US Corporate Banking Expansion — "Project North Star"
[Verified — FinancialContent March 2026; Nikkei Asia; Bloomberg]

Mizuho has executed a sustained and aggressive US growth strategy over the past decade. By March 2026, it was ranked Best Bank for Corporate Banking in the US (Crisil Coalition Greenwich) for the second consecutive year — co-top with JPMorgan Chase and Goldman Sachs. This was achieved through:

- Acquisition of Greenhill & Co. (M&A advisory boutique; ~$550m, completed by early 2025)
- Acquisition of Augusta & Partners (renewable energy advisory; July 2025)
- Mass lateral hiring of senior bankers from US bulge brackets — Richard Robinson (Deutsche Bank), Jon Connor (HSBC), and multiple sector specialists in technology, healthcare, and energy
- US Americas CEO and leadership team running active 24-month "war for talent" across IB, leveraged finance, and structured products

**Talent implication for RWO:** Active lateral hiring across US markets; significant recruitment activity across IB divisions; however, US is not a primary RWO delivery market. This intelligence is relevant as context for understanding Mizuho's overall hiring philosophy and investment in talent.

### 2b. AI Workforce Transition — Japan Clerical (February 2026)
[Verified — Bloomberg, Japan Times, Nikkei Asia, UPI, February 2026]

Mizuho announced it will transition approximately 5,000 clerical positions in Japan to AI systems over the next 10 years. This represents one-third of its ~15,000 clerical workforce at core banking units and group companies. Key details:

- Investment: Up to JPY 100 billion (~$670m) over 2026–2028 in AI development and implementation
- Application: Document verification (account openings, fund transfers), customer data registration
- Affected employees will be redeployed to sales roles: NISA, investment trusts, corporate sales, analytical support
- Official framing: "Not a headcount reduction" — a shift of human resources into focus areas
- Driven by profitability improvement mandate

**Talent implication for RWO:** The redeployment mandate creates demand for reskilling, capability assessment, and change management TA support. Internal staff moves create downstream specialist hiring pressure in customer-facing and analytical roles. This is a significant workforce transformation event unfolding in Japan — the most relevant single trigger for RWO Talent Advisory engagement.

### 2c. EU Universal Bank Integration (April 2025)
[Verified — Mizuho press release April 2025]

Mizuho Bank Europe N.V. (Amsterdam) merged with Mizuho Securities Europe GmbH (Frankfurt) to create a single universal bank entity offering integrated banking and securities services across the EU. Footprint rationalised to Amsterdam, Frankfurt, Madrid, and Paris; offices in Brussels, Dusseldorf, Milan, and Vienna transferred.

**Talent implication:** Cross-entity integration creates TA complexity: role duplication resolution, employer brand rationalisation, and the need to backfill some roles while eliminating others across the newly merged entity. Emma Dolby (CHRO EMEA, appointed May 2024) is managing this integration.

### 2d. EMEA CIB Growth
[Verified — Mizuho EMEA news releases 2025]

Matthew Ponsonby appointed as President & CEO of Mizuho International plc and Head of CIB EMEA (2025, incoming from BNP Paribas). Senior hires underway across trade finance and fixed income leadership. EMEA is an active growth market alongside the US.

### 2e. CANADE HR Framework
[Verified — Mizuho mizuhogroup.com/who-we-are/strategy/hr]

New unified HR system "CANADE" launched across five group companies. Key features:
- Enables cross-company talent transfers and concurrent assignments
- Personalised career pathways based on employee narratives
- Mid-career hiring acceleration, especially in digital domains
- Quarterly career dialogue between employees, managers, and HR specialists

**Talent implication:** The group is actively trying to build an internal mobility culture and compete for mid-career talent externally — two drivers that create TA volume pressure on an HR team historically configured for Japan-domestic, seniority-based hiring.

### 2f. Cross-Border Talent Globalisation
[Verified — Nikkei Asia interview with Mizuho executive]

Mizuho has stated an intention to globalise talent through cross-border job transfers, encouraging foreign employees hired outside Japan to transfer globally. This signals an ambition to build a genuinely international talent pool, but creates significant logistical and TA complexity.
