## 2. Strategic Context

### ADVANCE Plan (2022–2025) — Completed
Air Liquide's five-year strategic plan ADVANCE concluded at end-2025, having achieved or exceeded all financial and extra-financial targets ahead of schedule. Key outcomes: €4.4 billion annual investment, ROCE exceeding 10%, women in engineering/professional roles reaching 33%+ (target 35%). [Verified — Air Liquide strategy page]

### New Strategic Direction — Capital Markets Day H2 2026
Air Liquide has confirmed it will present its next strategic plan at a Capital Markets Day in H2 2026. In the interim, the company has committed to a further +100 basis points operating margin improvement target for 2027, extending cumulative progress to +560 basis points since 2022. [Verified — FY2025 press commentary]

### Group Transformation Programme (active since September 2024)
This is the most significant HR-relevant strategic development. Air Liquide launched a multi-year transformation programme in September 2024 structured around four pillars:
1. **Organisational simplification** — removing hierarchical layers, creating direct Operations-to-Functions interaction; merging Engineering & Construction with Global Markets & Technologies into a single Engineering & Technologies unit (effective January 2025)
2. **Extension of business service centres** — expanding HR and finance shared services hubs (France, Portugal, Krakow already live; covering 28 countries and 28,000 employees)
3. **Industrial and commercial initiatives**
4. **Leveraging data** [Verified — Air Liquide press release, July 2024]

The transformation has already generated meaningful headcount reductions across all geographies since early 2024. Restructuring costs in 2025 were slightly over €200 million, primarily in Europe (France, Iberia, Italy). [Verified — Q3 2025 Activity Report]

**Implication for HR/TA:** The transformation creates a dual mandate — managing workforce reductions in legacy structures while simultaneously recruiting for growth functions (Electronics, Hydrogen, Healthcare). This creates acute TA complexity and a strong case for scalable, flexible resourcing.

### Electronics Growth — Singapore and APAC
Air Liquide's Electronics segment is its fastest-growing business line, driven by semiconductor demand. The September 2025 €130 million Singapore investment (operational 2027) is a direct hiring trigger — engineering, operations, and commercial roles. [Verified — Air Liquide press release, September 2025]

### Hydrogen Investment
€8 billion investment target by 2035 across production, distribution, and mobility. Projects active in Japan (ATR technology for INPEX project), Europe (Normandy hydrogen basin), and North America. Hiring profile: specialist engineers, project managers, commercial leads. [Verified — Air Liquide strategy page]

### Healthcare Division Restructuring
Home Healthcare activity in France was restructured as part of the 2025 transformation. The UK Healthcare division (350+ employees) appears stable and growth-oriented, supplying NHS and home care. [Verified — FY2025 results commentary; uk.healthcare.airliquide.com]

### Executive Leadership Context
CEO François Jackow has framed the transformation as improving agility and directly serving customers — not primarily a cost-cutting exercise, though personnel savings are a stated outcome. The creation of a Group Industrial Direction under Pascal Vinet and reorganisation of European clusters under Emilie Mouren-Renouard are the most visible structural changes. [Verified — Air Liquide press release, July 2024]

## 8. RWO Positioning Summary

### Primary Talent Challenges

1. **Dual-mandate TA complexity:** Air Liquide is simultaneously managing headcount reductions in legacy/restructured parts of the business (Southern Europe, Home Healthcare France) and growth hiring in high-demand sectors (Electronics, Hydrogen, Healthcare UK). Managing both through an HRBP-led internal model is operationally stressful.

2. **APAC Electronics scale-up:** The Singapore semiconductor facility (€130M, operational 2027) and Japan hydrogen projects create a multi-year, high-volume but time-bounded hiring spike. Building permanent internal TA capacity for a project ramp — and then having to unwind it — is a known pain point that project RPO or modular RPO solves directly.

3. **Thin APAC TA capability:** The public footprint suggests Air Liquide's APAC TA function is lightly staffed relative to hiring volumes. With Workday Recruiting now live, the infrastructure is in place — but execution capacity (sourcers, TA partners, hiring manager advisory) may not be.

4. **New strategic plan design (H2 2026 Capital Markets Day):** Air Liquide must present a new workforce architecture as part of its next strategy. The OD and workforce planning function is likely under pressure to redesign role families and talent pipelines for Electronics, Hydrogen, and decarbonisation roles. Talent Advisory and labour market intelligence are directly relevant.

5. **HR shared services expansion:** The BSC model is growing (France/Portugal/Krakow covering 28 countries). Whether TA is being incorporated into this model or kept decentralised is a key question. If TA is being centralised, there is a natural entry point for RPO-as-a-service delivery alongside the existing shared services infrastructure.
