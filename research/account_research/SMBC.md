### New Medium-Term Management Plan — FY2026 Launch

A new medium-term plan launches in FY2026 (from April 2026). The stated framing is: "How can SMBC Group increase our corporate value by growing together with our clients and society in this age of change and uncertainty?" Specific targets will be announced at plan launch. [Verified: smfg.co.jp/english/gr2025/ceo/] This is a significant trigger: new strategic cycles typically drive HR operating model reviews and TA investment decisions.

### HR System Overhaul — January 2026

SMBC implemented a fundamental personnel system overhaul in January 2026 — the first since the 2002 merger of Sakura Bank and Sumitomo Bank. The reform breaks from seniority-based legacy practices and moves to role-based evaluation, enabling professional career paths regardless of tenure, and granting employees autonomy over career and workstyle. This is the single most significant HR transformation event in SMBC's history. [Verified: smfg.co.jp/english/company/organization/hrs.html]

### "One Global HR" — Global CoE Model (FY2025)

In FY2025 SMBC established a Global CoE (Centre of Excellence) model with five CoEs, four led by overseas local employees. Reporting lines from local HR heads were unified to the Head Office Human Resources Department. SMBC is actively integrating banking and securities (SMBC + SMBC Nikko) HR operations globally. [Verified: smfg.co.jp/english/company/organization/hrs.html]

This structural shift — from decentralised country HR to a centrally governed global model — directly elevates the importance of globally standardised TA and talent management capability.

### Global CIB and Jefferies Expansion

September 2025: SMBC and Jefferies Financial Group significantly expanded their Global Strategic Alliance. SMBC increased its Jefferies equity ownership to up to 20% and provided USD 2.5 billion in new credit facilities. New joint ventures include: Japanese equities research/sales/trading/ECM JV (expected launch January 2027); EMEA joint origination/underwriting/execution of syndicated leveraged loans. [Verified: businesswire.com, September 2025]

This is a direct TA pressure trigger: hiring senior CIB, capital markets, and product bankers globally to staff up the partnership — particularly in London (EMEA) and Tokyo.

### AI Investment

SMBC allocated JPY 50 billion in FY2024 for Generative AI. Stated ambition: "SMBC as an AI-leading Financial Institution." AI will reshape the talent portfolio — driving demand for DX professionals and potentially reducing headcount in operational roles. [Verified: smfg.co.jp/english/gr2025/ceo/]

### Multi-Franchise Asia Strategy

Active investment stakes and operations in India (YES BANK), Indonesia (Bank BTPN), Vietnam (FE Credit stake), Philippines (BDO Unibank partnership). Talent deployment across these emerging markets is a stated HR priority, including the "Indo wo Shiro Program" (junior employee India assignments) and Global Japan Program (90 overseas employees to Japan annually). [Verified: smfg.co.jp/english/company/organization/hrs.html]

### US Digital Bank Closure — Jenius Bank

Jenius Bank (SMBC's US digital bank subsidiary) is winding down. 173 employees affected; WARN notices filed January 2026 in Georgia, Illinois, and Maine (total 182+ affected). [Verified: WARN tracker records, January 2026] This represents a strategic pivot away from US retail toward institutional/CIB. RWO US relevance is therefore limited to CIB/markets hiring in New York.
