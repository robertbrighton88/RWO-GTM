## 2. Strategic Context

### Growth Agenda — Launches Now Live

Boehringer Ingelheim has transitioned from pipeline anticipation to active commercial launch across two major products:

**Hernexeos (zongertinib):**
- FDA accelerated approval for second-line HER2-mutant NSCLC: August 2024
- FDA approval expanded to first-line HER2-mutant NSCLC: February 26, 2026 (expedited via FDA's National Priority Review Voucher — approval in six weeks)
- Commercial launch in the US is underway. This is BI's first oncology product.
- EU and Japan regulatory submissions in progress. [Verified — FiercePharma / MedCity News / PharmaExec]

**Jascayd (nerandomilast):**
- FDA approval for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF): October 7, 2025 — first new IPF therapy approved in over a decade
- FDA approval for progressive pulmonary fibrosis (PPF): December 19, 2025
- Regulatory submissions filed in EU, China, and Japan
- First commercial prescriptions underway in the US. [Verified — PRNewswire / AJMC / HCPLive]

This is not a future pipeline story — it is an active, multi-product commercial launch cycle. BI needs medical affairs, market access, regulatory, field sales, and commercial operations talent now, simultaneously across US, EU, and APAC markets. The TA function is under immediate and sustained pressure.

### Broader 10-Year Pipeline
Over 10 new Phase II/III trials starting within the next 12–18 months (as of H1 2025). BI has committed publicly to 25 new product launches over the next decade. Verducatib (bronchiectasis) received FDA breakthrough designation in 2024. [Verified — BI H1 2025 results]

### US Restructuring (2024–2025)
In 2024, BI cut approximately 724 US sales jobs (including 49 at its Ridgefield, CT HQ). The trigger was poor biosimilar Humira sales (Cyltezo), and the company simultaneously shifted from a traditional face-to-face sales model to a hybrid omnichannel model. Animal Health followed with a WARN notice for 90 employees (NJ, effective June 2025). [Verified — FiercePharma / BioPharma Dive / warntracker.com]

The TA function has therefore been managing simultaneous downsizing (Human Pharma US sales; Animal Health) and aggressive build (oncology commercial, IPF/PPF commercial, APAC manufacturing). This is textbook modular RPO territory.

### Japan Manufacturing Scale-Up
The EUR 300 million Yamagata expansion (groundbreaking June 2025) will create significant hiring demand for manufacturing, quality, and engineering talent in Japan over the next 2–4 years. [Verified — BI press release]

### Digital Transformation Board Priority
Harsha Deshmukh joined the Board in February 2026 to lead IT and Global Business Services. His mandate — "driving digital transformation and further development of business services" — may create downstream TA demand for tech, data, and digital operations talent globally. [Verified — GlobeNewswire November 2025]

### Skills-Based Workforce Transformation

The CLO Martin Hess has articulated a federated learning model with 500+ internal contributors globally. [Verified — iventiv.com / BI Annual Report 2023]

## 8. RWO Positioning Summary

### Primary Talent Challenges

1. **Active commercial launch TA at scale (now, not future):** Two drugs are commercially live in the US (Hernexeos, Jascayd), with EU and APAC launches to follow in 2026. BI needs oncology commercial and pulmonary disease commercial talent simultaneously — two new therapeutic paradigms for the company, requiring talent profiles that differ from their historical hiring. Internal TA capacity was not designed for this simultaneous multi-launch scenario.

2. **Japan manufacturing scale-up:** The EUR 300 million Yamagata expansion creates a 2–4 year horizon of significant manufacturing and engineering hiring in Japan. BI's internal TA function in Japan is unlikely to be sized for this volume.

3. **Simultaneous restructuring and growth complexity:** BI is managing US downsizing (Human Pharma sales + Animal Health), while growing in oncology commercial, IPF/PPF commercial, R&D, and manufacturing globally. TA teams caught between these mandates are a textbook case for modular RPO support.

4. **No confirmed EMEA or APAC RPO provider:** The white space outside North America (where Cielo is embedded and now handling Hernexeos/Jascayd launch hiring) represents a clean, uncontested entry point for RWO across UK, Singapore, Japan, and Malaysia.

5. **Skills-based workforce transition:** BI's enterprise skills platform (BI University) creates a natural conversation about whether the TA model is aligned to skills-based hiring — an advisory entry point that isn't a direct RPO pitch.

6. **Oncology as a new commercial discipline:** Hernexeos is BI's first oncology commercial product. Building an oncology commercial team from scratch in a new therapeutic area is one of the highest-pressure TA scenarios in pharma — requiring specialised sourcing, assessment, and speed to hire.
