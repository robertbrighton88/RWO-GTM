## 2. Strategic Context

### The UniCredit Takeover Bid — Defining Event (March 2026)
UniCredit SpA (Italy) launched a voluntary exchange offer for Commerzbank on 16 March 2026, valuing the bank at approximately €35bn. UniCredit holds ~26% direct stake plus ~4% via total return swaps. Commerzbank's board rejected the offer as misaligned with its strategy and at "very low price." The German government (Chancellor Friedrich Merz) called the bid "unacceptable." Settlement, if UniCredit succeeds, is not expected before H1 2027. [Verified — Bloomberg, Disruption Banking, Retail Banker International, March 2026]

**HR / TA implications of the bid:**
- Uncertainty creates internal TA paralysis — business units will hold headcount decisions pending clarity
- Verdi union has warned >10,000 jobs at Commerzbank would be at risk in a combined entity (double structures in IT, ops, back office)
- Mlnarsky's team is managing employee uncertainty while simultaneously executing the Momentum restructuring — a dual pressure on a single HR function
- The independence narrative ("10,000 x No") is active — employee engagement and retention are existential priorities right now

### "Momentum" Strategy — CEO Orlopp's Independence Agenda
Commerzbank announced an upgraded "Momentum" strategy in February 2025 as a direct defensive response to UniCredit's accumulation of its stake. Key elements:

- **3,900 FTE cuts in Germany by 2028**, primarily in central functions and operations — delivered via early partial retirement and natural attrition, not forced redundancy [Verified]
- **Net headcount maintained at 36,700** through international hiring, especially Poland and APAC [Verified]
- **€700m restructuring charges** in 2025 (already booked) [Verified]
- **AI investment: €600m change budget**; generative AI use cases expanding (legal contracts, virtual assistant "Ava") [Verified]
- 2028 targets: net result €4.2bn, ROTE 15%, cost-income 50%, CET1 13.5% [Verified]

### mBank (Poland) as Growth Engine
mBank revenues surged 37% in FY2025. Commerzbank is actively increasing headcount in Poland as a cost-efficient location — implying active TA requirements for the Polish subsidiary parallel to German reduction.

### AI-Driven Operating Model Shift
COO Christiane Vorspel oversees digital transformation. The bank is investing heavily in AI-enabled operations — this means some back-office HR/TA processes will be automated, but specialist and relationship roles (front office, compliance, risk) still require quality talent sourcing.
