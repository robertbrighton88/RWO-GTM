## 2. Strategic Context

### New Leadership Agenda (Established 2024–2025)

CEO **Martina Cheung** took office November 1, 2024, replacing Doug Peterson after a planned succession. Cheung previously ran Ratings (2022–2024) and Market Intelligence (2019–2022). Her appointment triggered a full Executive Leadership Team reset announced October 17, 2024. CPO **Girish Ganesan** joined as part of this ELT, also effective November 1, 2024. [Verified — S&P Global press release Oct 2024]

The new CEO + new CPO combination, installed simultaneously, created an organisational inflection point that is now 16 months into execution. The "people forward" EVP and AI-readiness programme are Ganesan's primary deliverables under Cheung.

### AI-Driven Workforce Plateau — Critical New Signal (Q4 2025 Earnings)

At the February 10, 2026 earnings call, management stated explicitly that **revenue growth will outstrip headcount growth over the next several years**, and that the company has **"reached peak headcount growth in certain areas where they've accelerated the application of AI functions."**

Supporting data points from the same call:
- **Over 10% of internal applications eliminated** in 2025 through automation
- **Targeted run-rate expense reduction in the Enterprise Data Office (EDO) exceeding 20% by end of 2027**
- Over half of total data workflows are now automated

This is a material shift from the growth-hiring narrative. For 2026 and beyond, S&P Global will likely hire fewer people in aggregate — but specialist profiles (AI/ML engineering, private markets data, Kensho platform) will remain in demand. Internal TA teams may shrink at exactly the moment that specialist sourcing complexity increases. [Verified — Q4 2025 earnings call transcript / TipRanks / Bayelsawatch]

### Google Cloud / Gemini Partnership (December 2025)

S&P Global's December 2025 partnership with Google Cloud extends the AI transformation from internal tooling (Kensho Spark Assist, Eightfold) to the product layer. The Gemini Enterprise deployment across the workforce deepens the skills-based architecture conversation — the HR function needs to manage employees whose job content is shifting faster than their job descriptions.
