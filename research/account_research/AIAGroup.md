## 1. Company Overview

**Full Name:** AIA Group Limited
**Ticker / Listing:** HKEX: 1299 (Main Board, Hong Kong Stock Exchange; IPO 2010)
**Headquarters:** Hong Kong (Group HQ and registered office)
**Founded:** 1919 (Shanghai)
**Sector:** Life & Health Insurance (Pan-Asian)

### Business Overview [Verified — AIA Group website, 2024 Annual Results press release]

AIA is the largest independent publicly listed pan-Asian life and health insurer. It operates across 18 markets in Asia-Pacific, serving over 43 million individual policyholders and more than 16 million group insurance members. The Group distributes through three primary channels: tied agency (its largest and most strategic), bancassurance partnerships, and direct/digital.

AIA does not operate in Europe or the US as a meaningful insurance market — its geographic footprint is entirely within Asia-Pacific.

**Key operating markets include:** Hong Kong & Macau, China, Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia, South Korea, Australia, Taiwan, New Zealand, India (JV), Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam, Cambodia, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, Brunei.

## 2. Strategic Context

### Growth Without Restructuring [Verified — AIA 2024 Annual Results; Q3 2025 results press release]

AIA is firmly in growth mode. Unlike most BFSI peers, AIA has not announced any group-level restructuring, headcount reduction programme, or operating model overhaul. All 18 markets delivered growth in 2024; 11 markets achieved double-digit VONB growth in Q3 2025.

This creates a **volume-driven TA challenge** rather than a restructuring one: the business is expanding rapidly, particularly in agent recruitment and digital/technology talent, but the internal TA function appears relatively modest in scale relative to the Group's operating complexity.

### Key Talent Demand Drivers

1. **Agency recruitment at scale** — AIA's "Premier Agency" model requires continuous high-volume recruitment of quality agents. New agent recruits grew 18% in 2024. This is an ongoing, multi-market, multi-lingual hiring programme at significant scale. [Verified]
2. **Technology and AI talent** — Following the completion of its US$800m tech rebuild, AIA is now recruiting for AI/data/analytics talent to embed GenAI into operations, distribution, and customer experience. Cara Ang confirmed a "build" strategy for tech talent alongside buy and borrow. [Verified — McKinsey interview]
3. **Leadership pipeline** — AIA promotes heavily from within (Owen Addison promoted from Group CHRO Office to Singapore CHRO; multiple internal promotions announced in Singapore in 2020). The internal pipeline is a strategic HR priority. [Verified]
4. **ASEAN market expansion** — ASEAN VONB grew 15% in 2024; Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia, and Indonesia all growing. This creates localised TA pressure across multiple markets simultaneously. [Verified]

### HR Operating Model [Inferred from available signals]

AIA operates a **federated HR model**: there is a Group CHRO (Cara Ang, based in Hong Kong) supported by a Group CHRO Office, with country-level CHROs or HR Directors in each operating market reporting into both the Group function and local country CEOs. Singapore, Malaysia, and Hong Kong & Macau have confirmed country CHROs. This model means TA decisions can be made at both group and market levels — creating potential entry points at multiple tiers.

The Singapore entity uses Hudson RPO as its recruitment partner (see Section 5). The Hong Kong entity previously used Hudson RPO in a similar capacity. This is the most significant competitive intelligence for RWO.

### Leadership Signal [Verified]

Sir Mark Tucker's return as Chairman (Oct 2025) is strategically significant. Tucker built AIA from its IPO and has strong views on talent and organisational quality. His return is likely to reinforce cultural and people investment — a tailwind for TA quality conversations. CEO Lee Yuan Siong has publicly emphasised that "our culture sets us apart" (McKinsey, 2023).
