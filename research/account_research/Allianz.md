## 8. RWO Positioning Summary

### Primary Talent Challenges

1. **APAC local TA delivery gap:** Group TA operates a "one template, one process" model managed from Munich. The Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong, and Japan entities face real-world hiring complexity that a globally managed template cannot fully serve — specialist roles, local language, regulatory licences, and senior/executive hiring all require local market depth. The Allianz APAC business is growing (Capital Markets Day targets), but TA resourcing in market is thin.

2. **Allianz Partners AI-driven workforce transition:** 1,500–1,800 roles being eliminated across travel insurance call centres — creating an immediate need for redeployment support, outplacement, and workforce transition advisory. Simultaneously, replacement specialist roles (tech, data, AI operations) need to be filled. This is a textbook dual-mandate talent challenge.

3. **UK TA model in reset:** New CPO (Suzanne Scott, Nov 2025) is inheriting a UK TA function in the middle of an executive team refresh and an integration of LV= General Insurance. She is likely assessing the operating model, vendor relationships, and employer brand position — a six-to-twelve-month window to influence.

4. **Senior/specialist recruiting across BFSI:** As Allianz grows its commercial, asset management, and specialised P&C lines, senior external hiring (Allianz Commercial, AllianzGI UK/Singapore/Japan) is growing outside the scope of the insourced TA model.

5. **Malaysia growth and talent:** Allianz Life Malaysia is actively expanding its agency distribution. As a Bursa Malaysia-listed entity it faces investor and regulatory scrutiny on talent practices. Local HR team is strong but scale of ambition may outpace internal TA capacity.
