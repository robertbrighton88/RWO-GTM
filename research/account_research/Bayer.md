## 2. Strategic Context: Transformation & Restructuring

### "Dynamic Shared Ownership" (DSO) [Verified]

CEO Bill Anderson (joined June 2023; contract extended through March 2029) launched one of the most radical restructurings in Bayer's history. The operating model — **Dynamic Shared Ownership (DSO)** — eliminates bureaucracy by:

- Collapsing hierarchy from 12–13 layers down to 3–5
- Teams of 5–15 people forming around specific goals, rotating across projects
- Leadership roles reframed as "VACCs" — Visionaries, Architects, Catalysts, Coaches
- Heavy investment in coaching capability globally
- By mid-2025, rolled out across half the global business; 100+ front-runner teams live

DSO is the subject of a Harvard Business School case study and has been featured in MIT Sloan Management Review, Fortune, and CNBC.

**Sources:** [Bayer DSO Page](https://www.bayer.com/en/strategy/dynamic-shared-ownership), [Harvard Business School Case](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=67228), [CNBC — Great Flattening](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/23/great-flattening-bayer-ceo-experiment-employees-working-without-bosses.html)

---

### Restructuring Scale [Verified]

- **~11,000–12,000 positions eliminated** from peak headcount of ~100,000
- Workforce: ~100,000 (peak) → ~88,500 (end 2025)
- Management layers cut approximately in half
- Germany: protected from compulsory redundancies until December 30, 2026
- US: restructuring largely complete by end of 2025
- CEO Anderson confirmed additional reductions expected over "the coming 18 months" (late 2025 statement)

**Sources:** [PharmExec](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/bayer-layoffs-12-000-ongoing-2-3-billion-restructuring-plan), [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/business/bayer-expects-more-layoffs-to-come-following-a-brutal-two-years-of-cost-cutting)
