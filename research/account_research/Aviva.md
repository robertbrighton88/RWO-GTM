## 2. Strategic Context

### Direct Line Acquisition — The Defining Event
Aviva completed the acquisition of Direct Line Group (DLG) for £3.7bn on 1 July 2025, making it the UK's largest personal lines insurer. This is Aviva's largest deal in a decade. [Verified — Insurance Business, Aviva newsroom]

**Workforce impact:**
- Combined workforce of ~33,000–36,000 (post-acquisition)
- Between 1,600 and 2,300 roles at risk over three years (5–7% reduction) [Verified — Personnel Today, Insurance Journal]
- Roles at risk: head office, senior management, insurance operations, IT/technology, marketing, claims management
- Mitigation: natural attrition, ~800–900 open vacancies at Aviva available for DLG employees
- Cost synergy target raised from £125m to £225m; capital synergies >£500m expected by end 2026

**Integration status (as at March 2026):** Well advanced. Direct Line leadership cleared (CEO and CFO departed post-close). Jason Storah (Aviva GI CEO) leads combined GI entity. New GCS structure confirmed February 2026. Centre of Excellence for data analysis, software development, and QA creating 200+ new roles (announced alongside GCS restructure). [Verified — Insurance-Edge, February 2026]

### Strategic Workforce Planning
Aviva completed a Workday re-implementation in 2023, building skills profiles and career-pathing infrastructure across ~22,000 employees at the time. Now scaling to the full 36,000-person organisation. The next phase involves Workday Skills Cloud and a talent marketplace. [Verified — UNLEASH interview with Danielle Harmer, August 2024]

### AI and Technology Transformation
CEO Dame Amanda Blanc has identified AI as one of three core opportunities for the 2025–28 period. Aviva is deploying AI in claims summarisation, medical underwriting, and customer service. Microsoft Copilot rollout was planned for automation of routine tasks (as of H2 2024). [Verified — UNLEASH; 2025 results]

### Aviva Investors APAC Expansion
Aviva Investors appointed Sandra Cheng as CEO, Asia Pacific & Head of APAC Distribution in October 2024, based in Singapore. This represents a growth push in APAC distribution — but headcount will be small (asset management distribution, not insurance operations). [Verified — Aviva Investors press release]

### Key Structural Note
Aviva sold the vast majority of its Asian insurance operations between 2004 and 2021. There are no HR or TA operations in RWO's core APAC markets (Singapore, Japan, Malaysia, Hong Kong) beyond a very small Aviva Investors Singapore office. **RWO's opportunity at Aviva is a UK-only account.** [Verified]
