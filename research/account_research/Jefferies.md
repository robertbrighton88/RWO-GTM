## 2. Strategic Context

### 2.1 Growth Phase — Not Restructuring

Jefferies is in a significant growth phase following several years of countercyclical investment. Unlike most bulge-bracket banks, Jefferies actively hired MDs through 2021–2024 market downturns, adding approximately 38 MDs per year on average. This strategy is now paying off: 2024 revenues were up 50%, advisory revenues grew 17% in Q1 2025, and the firm has surpassed Citi and Lazard in advisory fee league tables. [Verified — eFinancialCareers, Fortune, Business Wire 2025]

**Implication for RWO:** Jefferies is not cutting headcount — it is growing. This creates real TA demand, not restructuring complexity. The firm will need to hire, onboard, and integrate talent across its expanding footprint.

### 2.2 APAC Expansion — Active and Aggressive

Jefferies' APAC business is a deliberate strategic priority. Key recent hires:
- Lars Aagaard (from Barclays) — Head of Financial Sponsors, Asia
- Jason Lam — Head of Asia TMT Investment Banking
- Abhinandan Jain — Head of Infrastructure and Industrials, India
- Robert Levine and Cale McCulloch (from CLSA) — senior traders, Hong Kong [Verified — Bloomberg, ainvest.com, 2025]

Japan revenues grew 3% in 2025; Asia Pacific is cited as 40% of global GDP and a core growth region for the firm. Jefferies hosted a joint Jefferies/SMBC event in Tokyo in October 2025, signalling commitment to Japan specifically. [Verified — Jefferies.com event agenda]

**Implication for RWO:** Active senior hiring across Singapore, Hong Kong, and Tokyo creates lateral recruitment pressure — particularly for scarce front-office talent and, by extension, middle and back office support roles.

### 2.3 Secondaries Market Boom — Talent Retention Under Pressure

Jefferies is the leading secondaries advisory franchise on Wall Street, advising on over $31bn of transactions in H1 2025 (versus $103bn total market volume). The firm has tightened employment contracts for its Private Capital Advisory team — imposing one-year gardening leave for MDs/Partners — in response to Moelis poaching Jefferies' Global Head of Private Capital Advisory. [Verified — Private Equity Wire, Bloomberg, eFinancialCareers 2025]

**Implication for RWO:** Talent retention is a live board-level concern. The firm is burning significant management bandwidth on non-compete management, departures, and replacement hiring in a competitive segment.

### 2.4 HR Transformation — Active Investment

An open VP role titled "Head of HR Transformation" was posted in early 2025, reporting directly to the Global Head of Human Capital. Responsibilities explicitly include: HR process automation, AI-driven talent solutions, **contingent workforce management enhancement**, data-driven decision making, and cross-functional HR/IT collaboration. Salary: $150,000–$165,000 base (New York). [Verified — Wall Street Careers / LinkedIn job posting]

**Implication for RWO:** This is a high-signal indicator that Jefferies is investing in HR operating model change — including contingent workforce management — at a senior level. This is a live white-space entry point for RWO's MSP/CWM proposition.

### 2.5 AI Adoption Underway

HCLTech has published a case study on Jefferies' adoption of generative AI for corporate functions. The firm's CEO cited AI as "the singular focus" in 2025 following four years focused on human capital issues. [Verified — HCLTech video, eFinancialCareers]

**Implication for RWO:** Jefferies is likely to be open to technology-enabled service solutions rather than purely headcount-based RPO. RWO's RW Plus platform and AI-informed delivery model is well-positioned here.
