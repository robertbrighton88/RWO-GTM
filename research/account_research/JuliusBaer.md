## 2. Strategic Context

Julius Baer is in the middle of a significant multi-year transformation driven by three compounding pressures: the Signa real estate collapse (which caused major credit loss provisions in FY2023), regulatory scrutiny, and an unsustainable cost base. The new CEO, Stefan Bollinger (appointed January 2025, from Goldman Sachs where he was Co-Head Private Wealth Management EMEA), has made restructuring his defining mandate.

**Key strategic developments:**

**Executive board contraction (February 2025):**
The executive board was cut from 15 members to 5 (CEO, COO/Deputy CEO, CFO, CRO, GGC). This is a signal of radical simplification. Critically, the dedicated CHRO seat on the executive board — held by Guido Ruoss since 2024 — was eliminated. HR functions were absorbed into the COO's remit. Nic Dreckmann (COO) carried HR/Corporate Affairs until his departure (announced April 2026 effective April 13, 2026). His successor, Jean Nabaa (ex-HSBC/Standard Chartered/BAML COO), took over the COO seat on April 13, 2026. [Verified — EQS News, Fortune, Bloomberg, Finews.asia]

**400 job cuts (February 2025):**
Approximately 400 roles eliminated globally, approximately 5% of the workforce. Cuts concentrated in back-office and support functions in Switzerland. Restructuring costs approximately CHF 55 million. [Verified — Private Banker International, Citywire, SWI Swissinfo]

**Cost savings programme:**
Extended cost programme targeting CHF 110 million gross additional savings on a run-rate basis by end 2025, on top of CHF 130 million already delivered by year-end 2024. Adjusted cost/income target: below 67%. [Verified — FY2025 results]

**Organisational redesign (April 2025):**
New three-region client model. One unified Products & Solutions function. Digital Business Transformation established as a standalone unit. These changes create significant HR/TA complexity: new team structures, new reporting lines, new leadership appointments in each region. [Verified — Finews Asia, Private Banker International]

**Asia growth agenda (concurrent with cost cuts):**
Despite back-office cost-cutting in Switzerland, Julius Baer is actively hiring front-office relationship managers in Asia. In H1 2024, nearly 60 new RMs were hired (approximately +10% year-on-year). Asia AuM grew from CHF 98bn (end 2023) to CHF 114bn (H1 2024). Julius Baer has stated Asia is a structural growth priority, with Singapore as the primary APAC hub. New Singapore office opened (move to One@Changi City for COO, Finance, HR and Audit teams). [Verified — Finews, WealthBriefingAsia, Hubbis]

**Regulatory/governance backdrop:**
Following the Signa losses, Julius Baer is under pressure from FINMA (Swiss regulator) to improve credit concentration risk controls. New board chair Noel Quinn (ex-HSBC Group CEO, April 2025) brings governance credibility. This regulatory pressure adds a compliance-hiring dimension. [Verified — multiple press sources]

**CEO and leadership character:**
Stefan Bollinger is a commercial operator (Goldman Sachs private wealth, not a traditional banker). His focus is clearly on revenue quality, cost efficiency, and a tight operating model. He is unlikely to entertain large outsourcing proposals without a compelling commercial case. His framing of talent is around "disciplined entrepreneurship" and productivity, not HR process. [Inferred from press commentary and remuneration report language]
