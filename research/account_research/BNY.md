## 2. Strategic Context

### Platform Operating Model Transformation
BNY is ~three years into the most significant structural overhaul in its post-merger history. CEO Robin Vince is converting BNY from a fragmented, siloed conglomerate into a unified financial services **platforms company**. [Verified — Euromoney "World's Best Bank Transformation 2025"]

Key structural changes:
- Client onboarding: centralised under single leadership with one call centre replacing fragmented subsidiary processes
- Clearing: three separate businesses merged into one unified operation
- Trading: two businesses combined under unified leadership
- Payments: multiple tech stacks consolidated under central ownership

**Progress milestones:**
- Q4 2024: first wave complete — client onboarding, payments, treasury services, loans, structured debt; ~25% of workforce migrated
- Spring 2025: >50% of workforce on new model
- 2026 target: corporate functions (legal, risk, finance) to complete migration; 80%+ of workforce on platforms model

**Financial result:** ~$500m in 2024 savings; expense ratio reduced from 71.3% (2022) to 67% (2024); record $20.1bn revenue FY2025; 8 consecutive quarters of positive operating leverage; 28% EPS growth YoY [Verified — BNY Q4 2025 Earnings]

### AI Strategy — Eliza Platform
BNY's **Eliza** enterprise AI platform is a multi-agent orchestration layer deployed at scale. As of January 2026:
- 20,000 "Empowered Builders" — human employees trained to create and deploy custom AI agents
- 130+ "Digital Employees" — AI agents with system logins and human managers performing tasks (code remediation, payment validation)
- 99% of 52,000 employees have received Eliza training
- Partnership with OpenAI (2025) and Google Cloud Gemini (expanded Eliza platform)
- CEO frames this as "workforce augmentation" — grow capability without scaling expenses [Verified — BNY AI page; OpenAI case study; PYMNTS.com]

**Workforce implication:** BNY explicitly intends to contain headcount growth through AI; this is not a layoff programme but a strategic intention to expand output per employee. "Digital employees" replacing some manual task roles. [Inferred — from CEO statements on expense management]
