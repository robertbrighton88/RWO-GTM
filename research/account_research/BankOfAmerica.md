## 2. Strategic Context

### AI-Led Operational Efficiency & Headcount Management

CEO Brian Moynihan declared at the Q4 2025 earnings call: "The number one priority is to work the headcount through operational excellence and applications of new technologies, including AI." [Verified: Banking Dive, January 2026] After two years at ~213,000, headcount is expected to drift downward in 2026 via attrition management — not active layoffs. CFO Alastair Borthwick confirmed: "Every time someone leaves, we take the opportunity to evaluate whether the role needs to be replaced." [Verified]

Key AI impact metrics:
- AI reduced coding work by 30%, equivalent to ~2,000 roles [Verified: Q4 earnings commentary]
- The bank holds 1,500+ AI-specific patents [Verified: Fast Facts]
- "Build once, reuse" AI architecture standardised on Erica platform across 60,000 tech workers [Verified: Fortune, March 2026]
- Erica for Employees deployed internally; AI-powered tools rolled out across Merrill, banking, and operations divisions [Verified]
- $4B annual investment in new technology initiatives including AI [Verified]

### UK & Ireland Expansion — Belfast AML Hub

In September 2025, Bank of America announced a new operations facility in Belfast, Northern Ireland, targeting up to 1,000 new roles in Global Financial Crimes and AML Operations (KYC/CDD, quality assurance, compliance). [Verified: multiple sources, September 2025 and January 2026] Recruitment began January 2026. This is Bank of America's first presence in Northern Ireland and a significant vote of confidence in the UK, personally endorsed by CEO Moynihan. Hannah Scullion, VP Talent Acquisition (Risk & Compliance), is leading Belfast hiring.

This Belfast programme represents the largest single discrete hiring event RWO has observed at this account and is a clear project RPO entry point for the UK market.

### Japan Expansion Signal

In March 2025, CEO Moynihan stated publicly at a Tokyo event that BofA is "open to hiring in Japan as the market revives." Currently employs approximately 800 people in Japan, focused on corporate and investment banking. [Verified: Bloomberg, Japan Times, March 2025] Japan equity and M&A deal flow is recovering — a secular tailwind for specialist hiring. [Verified: Bloomberg "Global Banks Scramble to Hire Top Talent in Booming Japan," June 2025]

### Branch & Consumer Expansion (US)

Opening 165+ new financial centres by end 2026 across four new US states [Verified]. Hiring 8,000 individuals from community colleges (5-year commitment) and 10,000 veterans [Verified]. This creates sustained volume hiring pressure in the US but is less directly relevant to RWO's EMEA/APAC priority markets.

### Skills-First Hiring Transformation

Bank of America has eliminated degree requirements for nearly all positions, partners with 400+ universities annually including HBCUs and community colleges, and has achieved 42% internal mobility rate (roles filled internally) over five years. [Verified: Josh Bronstein LEADERS interview, 2025] The CPO function is managing a complex dual-track: headcount reduction via attrition in operational/technology roles while simultaneously expanding in client-facing, compliance, and specialist positions.

### DEI Policy Shift

In 2025, Bank of America removed explicit "diversity" language from its 10-K, replacing "Diversity & Inclusion" with "Talent, Inclusion and Opportunity," responding to the Trump administration's DEI policy environment. [Verified: Banking Dive, HR Grapevine, 2025] This does not signal withdrawal from inclusive hiring in practice but changes the public framing.
