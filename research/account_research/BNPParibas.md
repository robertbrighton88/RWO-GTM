## 2. Strategic Context

### 2026–2028 Strategic Plan — Key Themes

BNP Paribas is in the middle of a multi-year transformation plan with several dimensions directly relevant to RWO:

**1. Support function industrialisation and AI-driven efficiency**
The group's cost/income target has been tightened from ~58% to below 56% by 2028. A key lever is the "mutualization and standardisation of support functions" combined with AI automation. 750+ AI use cases are already in production across the bank. An internal "LLM as a Service" platform was launched in June 2025 to give business units shared large language model access. [Verified — BNP Paribas strategic plan materials; The Banking Scene, June 2025]

This translates to: HR operations and TA functions are under efficiency pressure. Any support function that can be industrialised is a candidate for redesign. This is an RWO Talent Advisory entry point.

**2. AXA Investment Managers acquisition and integration**
The €5.1 billion acquisition of AXA IM closed 1 July 2025. By 31 December 2025, AXA IM, BNPP Real Estate IM, and BNP Paribas AM merged their main legal entities into a single structure. ~1,200 jobs are to be cut from the combined asset management platform by end-2027 (~20% of division headcount), implemented in three waves starting mid-2026. Around 600 positions affected in France; ~230 new jobs created. [Verified — Global Banking & Finance, Citywire Selector, BNP Paribas press releases]

This is the largest single workforce complexity event in the group's recent history. Integration HR — harmonising cultures, systems, grading frameworks, and TA operating models across two large asset managers — is exactly the type of work where RWO Talent Advisory and modular RPO are relevant.

**3. French retail banking restructuring**
BNP Paribas is closing ~200 branches in France by end-2026 (80 in 2025, 120 in 2026). Headcount in branches declining at ~5% per year; new hires being redirected to digital and phone channels. Natural attrition is the primary lever — no announced layoffs. [Verified — Bloomberg, Retail Banker International, 2025]

**4. CIB organisational redesign**
Risk-weighted assets (~€12bn) moved from Global Markets to Global Banking, with stated aim of reinforcing worldwide governance. The group also published a governance adaptation document ahead of its "future strategic plan" (which is expected to run 2026–2028). [Verified — GlobeNewswire, March 2026 restatement document]

**5. APAC growth agenda**
BNP Paribas appointed Karine Delvallee as Regional Head of Southeast Asia (CEO Singapore) in February 2025. Singapore is the group's primary CIB hub in APAC, with operations also active in Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and Vietnam. The APAC Talent Programme (ATP) was revamped in 2024–2025, winning the Employee Experience Awards 2025 Hong Kong (Gold). The group is actively hiring graduates across APAC markets. [Verified — BNP Paribas APAC website; Human Resources Online, 2025]

**6. People Strategy 2025**
Anchored on three pillars: employee experience, human capital investment, ethical/inclusive workplaces — framed within the GTS (Growth, Technology, Sustainability) corporate strategy. Emphasis on AI literacy, sustainability skills, digital upskilling, and internal mobility. [Verified — Human Resources Online, 2025]

### Summary of TA Pressure Points
- Integration HR complexity from AXA IM M&A — harmonising two TA operating models, grading, and employer brand across ~6,000 combined AM employees
- Support function efficiency targets create internal headcount pressure on HR/TA teams
- APAC growth hiring across multiple markets (SG, HK, JP, MY) while simultaneously managing European restructuring — dual-mandate complexity
- 750+ AI use cases suggest the group is experimenting with AI in recruitment — RWO can position as a partner to scale responsibly
