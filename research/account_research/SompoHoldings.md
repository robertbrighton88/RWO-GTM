## 2. Strategic Context

### Governance & Regulatory Remediation (High Priority)

Sompo Holdings has been operating under multiple Financial Services Agency (FSA) business improvement orders since early 2024:
- **Big Motor scandal (2024):** Sompo Japan was implicated in fraudulent automobile insurance claims via Big Motor dealership network. FSA issued business improvement order February 2024. Former Chairman/CEO Kengo Sakurada resigned. [Verified — Nikkei Asia, FSA filings]
- **Insurance premium cartel (2024):** Sompo Japan among four insurers penalised for coordinated premium-setting practices. [Verified — PYMNTS]
- **Information leak case (2025):** New business improvement order issued March 2025 for a data/information leak. FSA required improvement plan submission May 2025. [Verified — company filing]

This governance context is significant for HR: cultural transformation, compliance hiring, ethics and conduct frameworks, and management restructuring are all live priorities. New Group CEO Mikio Okumura (appointed December 2025) was brought in explicitly to lead transformation.

### Structural Reorganisation (April 2025)

Sompo restructured from a Japan-domestic model with an international subsidiary into two globally integrated segments: SOMPO P&C and SOMPO Wellbeing. This is a fundamental operating model change with significant HR implications — dual HR strategies now run within each business segment, plus a Group CoE layer at Holdings level. [Verified — Insurance Journal, Reinsurance News, company announcement]

### Top Management Overhaul (April 2026)

Broad reshuffle of senior leadership effective April 1, 2026: Katsuyuki Tajiri appointed Group CFO and Deputy President; multiple senior roles reorganised in risk, IT, PR, and overseas M&A. This signals continued organisational instability and the need to rapidly build leadership capability in global functions. [Verified — TipRanks / company announcement]

### Aspen Integration (2026–2027)

The USD $3.5bn Aspen acquisition closed February 2026 and is the most immediate TA complexity driver. Aspen brings approximately 1,500–2,000 additional staff (Bermuda, London, US), all requiring brand transition, culture integration, and potential role rationalisation. No public information on workforce integration plans has been published. [Verified — acquisition completion; workforce size inferred from Aspen's pre-acquisition scale]

### Growth Agenda

- MTMP FY2024–2026 targets: double market cap and consolidated profits by FY2030; raise adjusted ROE to 13–15% by FY2026
- International commercial premium growth of >USD $1bn by FY2026 through organic growth in US, Continental Europe (incl. UK), Canada, and Singapore [Verified — company MTMP]
- Asia Pacific: Singapore as regional hub for 13 markets; active commercial insurance expansion; key hires made in APAC A&H pricing and consumer lines [Verified — InsuranceAsia News]
- Japan "domestic repair": rebuilding brand trust and operational integrity post-scandal

### Workforce Transformation Agenda

- Shift from generalist to specialist talent model (Group CHRO FY2025 message)
- "Three Rs" framework: English and AI literacy as baseline requirements for all employees
- SOMPO Global University for cross-cultural leadership development
- Group Job Challenge Program: internal mobility across group companies
