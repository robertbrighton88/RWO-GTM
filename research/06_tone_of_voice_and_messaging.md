# Robert Walters Outsourcing — Tone of Voice & Messaging Research Dump

> Sources: robertwalters.com, robertwalters.co.uk, robertwalters.us, Capital Markets Event Transcript 2024

---

## 1. Core Purpose Statement

> **"To power people and organisations to fulfil their unique potential."**

This is the foundational brand statement. It is human-centred, empowerment-oriented, and applies to both candidates and clients.

---

## 2. Vision Statement

> **"To be the world's most trusted talent solutions business."**

Key word: **trusted**. This is a deliberate and repeated word choice across their materials. It positions RWO not as the biggest or cheapest, but as the most reliable, credible partner.

---

## 3. Outsourcing-Specific Messaging

### Primary Value Proposition (Outsourcing)
> "Agile recruitment outsourcing solutions tailored to your unique requirements, seamlessly scalable to match your evolving needs and objectives."

Key messaging elements:
- **Agile** — flexibility and speed
- **Tailored** — bespoke, not off-the-shelf
- **Seamlessly scalable** — grows with you, no friction
- **Evolving** — long-term partnership, not transactional

### Outsourcing Brand Promise
> "In our Outsourcing business, we're not just keeping pace with the future — we're shaping it."

Tone: **confident, forward-looking, proactive** — not reactive or defensive.

### Strategic Partner Positioning
> "As a strategic talent solutions partner, we blend curiosity, innovation, and deep expertise to solve today's workforce challenges and prepare clients for tomorrow — from tech-powered delivery to human-led insight."

Key positioning words: **strategic partner** (not vendor), **curiosity**, **innovation**, **expertise**, **tech-powered + human-led** (dual emphasis — neither pure tech nor pure human).

## 4. Tone of Voice Characteristics

| Dimension | Tone |
|---|---|
| Authority | High — speaks with expertise and confidence |
| Warmth | Present — "human-led," "people-first," empathy |
| Formality | Semi-formal — professional but accessible |
| Urgency | Moderate — action-oriented but not pressuring |
| Data orientation | Strong — metrics, benchmarks, insights referenced often |
| Aspiration | High — "shaping the future," "fulfilling unique potential" |

**Overall:** Confident, trusted advisor tone. Not salesy or pushy. Positions as a long-term partner who understands complexity.

## 5. Key Messaging Themes & Pillars

### Theme 1: Partnership, Not Vendor
- "Strategic talent solutions partner"
- "Acting as an extension of your team"
- "We align with company culture"
- Consistent use of "partner" language vs. "provider" or "supplier"

### Theme 2: Human + Technology Balance
- "Tech-powered delivery and human-led insight"
- "AI is not going to replace recruiters, but recruiters without data fluency... will be replaced"
- RW Plus: "ethical AI and automation"
- Positions tech as an enabler of human expertise, not a replacement

### Theme 3: Data & Intelligence
- RW Insights platform — real-time talent data
- "Data-driven solutions designed to empower businesses"
- Pay benchmarking, diversity metrics, attrition data
- Case: European bank achieved 36% more hires using RW intelligence

### Theme 4: Trust & Quality
- "Trusted to deliver end-to-end recruitment, outsourcing and advisory"
- "Quality of service consistently delivered for close to four decades"
- "The world's most trusted talent solutions business"
- Trust as a brand cornerstone — built over nearly 40 years

### Theme 5: Future of Work
- "Not just keeping pace with the future — we're shaping it"
- "Against the background of an ever-changing world of work"
- "Prepare clients for tomorrow"
- Macro narrative around workforce evolution, flexibility, skills transformation

### Theme 6: Scale + Agility
- "Global footprint spanning 30 countries"
- "Seamlessly scalable"
- "Tailored solutions" — bespoke approach within a global infrastructure
- Ability to serve "startups, medium-sized enterprises, and the world's leading organisations"

## 6. Client-Facing Copy Examples

**On understanding clients:**
> "The businesses we partner with want to make things happen. And they need talent to do it. They have goals. They have challenges. They need people. They want answers."

→ **Tone:** Direct, punchy, empathetic. Short sentences. Action-oriented. Shows they "get it."

**On quality:**
> "We deliver better experiences and higher quality outcomes by taking the time to listen to, and fully connect with, the people and organisations we partner with."

→ **Tone:** Relationship-led, quality-focused, listening-first.

**On differentiation:**
> "Our ability to truly understand them and share their compelling stories is what sets us apart."

→ **Tone:** Storytelling-led, human-centric differentiation.

## 8. Channel-Specific Tone Observations

### Website
- Clean, confident, professional
- Moderate use of statistics and proof points
- Service-specific pages are structured and informative
- Strong use of short, declarative sentences

### Press Releases
- More formal, achievement-focused
- Awards and recognition prominently featured
- Leadership quotes used to add human voice

### Thought Leadership / Whitepapers
- Authoritative, research-led
- Salary surveys and market reports position RW as a market intelligence authority
- Topics: future of work, talent scarcity, DEI, flexibility

### Capital Markets / Investor Communications
- "Disciplined entrepreneurialism" — distinctive strategy language
- Clear, financial language; CEO Toby Fowlston speaks plainly
- "A year of strong strategic development" — positive framing despite difficult markets

## 9. Brand Refresh Signal (2024)

- Won **Gold for Best Visual Identity** at Transform Awards for global rebrand
- Described as converting 50+ websites and 1,000+ pieces of collateral
- The rebrand represents a deliberate shift to a **unified, premium talent brand** (not siloed brands)
- Visual identity refresh aligns with "trusted," "professional," "global" brand positioning

## 10. What's Absent from Their Messaging

(Useful for identifying white space or differentiation opportunities)
- Very little price/value discussion — they don't compete on cost
- Minimal mention of specific technology vendor partnerships (unlike some RPO competitors)
- Limited "urgency" or fear-based messaging — no "in a talent crisis" alarm language
- Not heavy on AI hyperbole — measured, responsible AI narrative ("ethical AI")
