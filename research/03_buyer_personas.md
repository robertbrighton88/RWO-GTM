## 1. Overview — Who RWO Targets

Robert Walters Outsourcing serves organisations "of all shapes and sizes" — from startups to the world's leading enterprises — but their outsourcing offering (RPO/MSP/Advisory) is clearly directed at:

- **HR decision-makers** at mid-to-large organisations
- Businesses with **sustained, volume hiring needs** (permanent and/or contingent)
- Organisations in **complex, regulated sectors** (financial services, tech, pharma, legal)
- Typically **1,000+ employee organisations** based on complexity of engagement
- **Multinational enterprises** needing multi-country hiring solutions

## 2. Inferred Primary Personas

### Persona 1: The CHRO / Chief People Officer
**Title:** Chief Human Resources Officer / Chief People Officer
**Seniority:** C-Suite
**Company size:** Large enterprise (5,000+ employees), often multinational

**What they care about:**
- Talent strategy aligned to business goals
- Cost efficiency across TA and HR operations
- Quality of hire and employer brand
- DEI/ED&I outcomes
- Being seen as a strategic business partner (not just operational)
- Future of work readiness

**Pain points RWO addresses:**
- Internal TA team capacity/capability gaps
- Cost and inefficiency of decentralised hiring across markets
- Need for data and insights to make board-level decisions
- Complexity of managing contingent workforce compliantly

**How RWO speaks to them:**
- "Strategic talent solutions partner"
- Thought leadership on future of work, DEI, talent market insights
- Route into C-suite through advisory/consulting services
- Broadening and deepening the relationship beyond transactional hiring

---

### Persona 2: Head of Talent Acquisition / VP Talent Acquisition
**Title:** Head of TA / VP TA / Global Talent Acquisition Director
**Seniority:** Senior Director / VP
**Company size:** Mid-market to enterprise (1,000–50,000+ employees)

**What they care about:**
- Time-to-hire and time-to-fill metrics
- Quality of candidate pipeline
- Hiring manager satisfaction
- TA team productivity (doing more with less)
- Technology and process efficiency
- Scalability during hiring surges

**Pain points RWO addresses:**
- Peaks and troughs in hiring volume — RPO provides scalable capacity
- Limited internal bandwidth for specialist or volume hiring
- Poor candidate experience damaging employer brand
- Lack of data and reporting on TA performance

**How RWO speaks to them:**
- Modular and Project RPO — flexible, scalable, fast to implement (6 weeks)
- RW Insights platform — real-time data and benchmarking
- "Extension of your TA team" positioning
- Tech-enabled delivery + human expertise

---

### Persona 3: Procurement / CPO (Contingent Workforce / MSP Angle)
**Title:** Head of Procurement / CPO / Category Manager (HR/Workforce)
**Seniority:** Director / VP
**Company size:** Large enterprise with significant contingent workforce spend

**What they care about:**
- Cost control and spend visibility
- Supplier rationalisation
- Compliance with labour laws across markets
- Contract management and risk reduction
- ROI on workforce spend

**Pain points RWO addresses:**
- Lack of visibility on contingent workforce spend across multiple suppliers
- Compliance and audit risk in contingent worker management
- Administrative burden of managing multiple staffing vendors
- Inconsistent quality across supplier network

**How RWO speaks to them:**
- MSP + VMS solution — full visibility and control
- Vendor neutral management
- Cost reduction through consolidation
- Compliance and risk mitigation

---

### Persona 4: CFO / Finance Leadership (Secondary Influencer)
**Title:** CFO / VP Finance
**Seniority:** C-Suite
**Company size:** Any enterprise

**What they care about:**
- Cost of talent acquisition and workforce
- Predictability of HR spend
- ROI on outsourcing investment
- Risk reduction

**Pain points RWO addresses:**
- Unpredictable cost of internal TA team + agency spend
- Hidden costs of poor hiring (quality of hire, attrition)
- MSP provides spend visibility and control

**How RWO speaks to them (indirectly):**
- Cost-per-hire metrics and savings
- Flexible commercial models (management fee + pay-for-performance)
- Advisory/benchmarking data

## 5. Company Size & Complexity Signals

- Outsourcing positioned for **"often very large multinational enterprises who hire large volumes of people year-in, year-out"**
- Best fit indicator: **1,000+ employees**
- Resource Solutions (now RW) manages a **recruitment budget of over £2 billion** and hires **tens of thousands of employees per year** — indicating very large client base
- Serves startups to enterprise (per website language), but RPO/MSP economics typically require volume
