## 2. Industry-Standard RPO Pricing Models (Applied by RWO)

### Model 1: Management Fee
- Client pays a **monthly or hourly fee**
- Fee covers: staff costs, technology, sourcing, account management, overhead
- **Benefit:** Budget predictability for client
- **Risk:** Less flexibility; provider is paid regardless of hire volume
- **Best for:** Long-term, stable hiring programmes (Full RPO)

### Model 2: Pay-for-Performance (Cost Per Hire)
- Client pays an **agreed fee per successful hire**
- Structure: open fee per requisition + close fee per filled position
- **Benefit:** Provider shares risk; client only pays for results
- **Risk:** Provider may deprioritise difficult-to-fill roles
- **Best for:** Project RPO, time-bound hiring campaigns

### Model 3: Hybrid / Blended
- Combination: monthly management fee + pay-for-performance component
- Captures best of both: predictability (management fee) + performance alignment (CPH)
- True-up mechanism: volume fluctuations balanced over contract period
- **Best for:** Established RPO relationships with variable hiring volumes

## 3. Price Positioning Signals

### Premium-to-Mid Positioning (Inferred)

Evidence RWO positions at the **quality/premium end** of the market:

1. **"Quality over volume" narrative** — emphasis on specialist mid-to-senior roles (not high-volume entry-level)
2. **"Nearly 40 years of quality service"** — brand heritage justifies premium
3. **"World's most trusted"** — trust and quality as the value proposition, not lowest cost
4. **Case study: European bank +36% hires** — ROI focus, not cost-saving focus
5. **Specialist sectors** (finance, legal, pharma) — higher-complexity, higher-value engagements
6. **Does not compete on cost** — no pricing language in marketing; zero "cost-effective" messaging

**What this means:** RWO is NOT positioning as the cheapest option. They compete on expertise, trust, quality of hire, and long-term partnership value.

### Who They're NOT For (By Implication)
- Entry-level, high-volume commodity hiring programmes
- Clients primarily seeking the lowest cost-per-hire
- Short-term, transactional relationships
- Organisations without dedicated HR/TA functions

## 4. Commercial Model Signals — Contextual

### Scale Required for RPO Economics
- Resource Solutions (now RW) manages a **recruitment budget of over £2 billion** for clients
- Hires **tens of thousands of employees per year** across client base
- Implies minimum viable client size: likely **500+ annual hires** for Full RPO to be economical
- Project RPO and Modular RPO more accessible for lower-volume clients

### Geographic Complexity = Higher Cost
> "Geographic reach can also impact the pricing structure — national or global RPO services will generally be more expensive due to the added complexity and resources required."

Multi-country engagements command premium pricing — aligns with RWO's global delivery capability.

### Implementation Timelines (Cost Implication)
- **Project RPO:** Operational within weeks (lower setup cost)
- **Full RPO:** 1–3 months to integrate (significant implementation investment)
- **Modular RPO:** Variable (mid-range)

## 5. ROI Narrative (How They Justify Price)

Rather than competing on price, RWO frames value through ROI metrics:

| Metric | Signal |
|---|---|
| Time-to-fill reduction | Faster hiring = lower cost of vacancy |
| Cost-per-hire improvement | Efficiency vs. internal TA + agency spend |
| Quality of hire | Reduced attrition, better performance |
| Spend visibility (MSP) | Contingent workforce cost control |
| Data intelligence | RW Insights helps clients hire 36% more efficiently |
| Advisory value | Workforce consultancy NFI up 51% — clients paying for insights |

**Key framing:** The total cost of poor hiring (attrition, re-hiring, lost productivity) vs. the cost of an outsourced partner who gets it right.
