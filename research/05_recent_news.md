## 1. ⚠️ CRITICAL — Brand Unification (July 2024)

**Biggest recent news: Robert Walters merged its three brands into one.**

- **Robert Walters** (specialist recruitment)
- **Walters People** (volume/professional recruitment)
- **Resource Solutions** (RPO/MSP)

→ All now operate under the single **Robert Walters** brand

**Implications for GTM:**
- "Resource Solutions" is no longer the outsourcing brand — everything is "Robert Walters Outsourcing" now
- Required converting 50+ client/candidate-facing websites and 1,000+ pieces of collateral
- Won **Gold for Best Visual Identity** (Professional Services) at Transform Awards for the rebrand
- This is a significant signal: the group wants to present a unified talent solutions business, not siloed brands

## 2. 2024 Financial Performance — Key Facts

**Context: 2024 was the second consecutive year of challenging market conditions**

- Q4 2024 net fee income: **down 14% in constant currency**
- Specialist professional recruitment NFI: **down 14%** (perm down 18%, temp down 10%)
- Recruitment outsourcing NFI: **down 7%** (sequential improvement on 2024 pacing)
- Full year result: broadly **breakeven** at profit before tax level
- **Net cash: ~£53m** as at 31 December 2024 (vs. £50m at end of September 2024)
- Total headcount at Q4 end 2024: **3,294** (down 5% QoQ, down 17% YoY)
- Fee earner headcount: **1,964** (down 7% QoQ, down 17% YoY)

**CEO Quote (Toby Fowlston):** "2024 closed with conditions in global hiring markets remaining challenging — marked by muted client and candidate confidence."

Source: [FY24 Results Statement](https://www.robertwalters.com/content/dam/robert-walters-redesign/global/files/investors/2025/RWA%20FY24%20results_Analyst%20conference%20call%20transcript.pdf)

## 3. Strategic Initiative — "Disciplined Entrepreneurialism" (2024–ongoing)

Announced at Capital Markets Event (26 September 2024):

**Five building blocks to drive higher margin business:**
1. Improving fee earner productivity
2. Driving efficiencies in front and back-office teams
3. Optimising the office network
4. Leveraging more co-ordinated procurement
5. Structural cost savings — **£4m+ already secured** in 2024

**Key message from leadership:** "A year of strong strategic development" — despite weak markets, 2024 was used to restructure and strengthen the business foundation.

**Outlook for 2025:** "An improvement in end markets is unlikely to be seen in the near term" — cautious but confident in medium-term strategy.
